Macro Landscape

Mississippi is aggressively transitioning from a decentralized, agency-siloed model toward a unified, enterprise-wide service delivery architecture. Guided by Gov. Tate Reeves and ITS, the state is heavily centralizing procurement to combat ransomware threats and replace decade-old legacy software.

For enterprise technology providers like Dell Technologies, Mississippi presents an incredibly lucrative battlefield. The state's "cloud-right" strategy does not equate to a wholesale public cloud migration; they are investing heavily in on-premises private cloud capabilities within their Primary Data Center (PDC) and Co-Processing Data Center (CPDC).

The 4 Central Pillars of IT Investment

Public Safety & Cyber Resilience

Expanding the Capitol Complex fiber network and fortifying the enterprise perimeter against ransomware.

Digital Equity & Rural Healthcare

$206M CMS grant targeting telehealth infrastructure, interoperable EHRs, and remote patient monitoring.

Legacy Systems Modernization

Replacing 25-year-old case management systems (MDHS/Medicaid) to meet federal data interoperability standards.

AI & Digital Governance

Creation of the AIR Task Force and strict AI Acceptable Use policies requiring on-prem/sovereign architectures.

Budget & Procurement

Understanding the financial models and purchasing vehicles governing state IT.

IT Governance & Financial Model

The "Hub and Spoke" Model

Mississippi uses a chargeback financial model established by SB 2779. This is a major friction point for agencies.

The Hub (ITS Core Services): ITS receives direct General Fund appropriations ($26.7M) to build foundational enterprise infrastructure.
The Spoke (Agency Consumption): Agencies consume ITS shared services (data center compute, storage, DR) and are charged back via the ITS Revolving Fund.

Procurement Vehicles

IT Hardware EPL 3760

The primary vehicle for enterprise infrastructure. Utilizes a Manufacturer-Sponsored Reseller Group model. Purchasing limit extended to $500,000 for enterprise IT hardware without ITS Board approval. Dell is a dominant incumbent here.

The CP-1 Process

For projects exceeding EPL thresholds or requiring complex integrations. Demands full RFP and ITS Board presentation. Highly delayed—agencies hate it.
