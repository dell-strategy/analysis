Dell Govt Affairs

Executive Mandate

Section Context: This introductory view establishes the foundational philosophy of Dell's government relations strategy. It clearly defines the shift from passive monitoring to active, measurable policy execution for all external consultants.

Dell Technologies retains external government consultants and lobbyists to secure our operational environment, drive public sector revenue, and shape emerging technology policy globally.

This document serves as the absolute, prescriptive baseline for our expectations. We do not pay for passive monitoring, networking without targeted outcomes, or generalized political summaries. We require proactive intervention, precise intelligence gathering, and measurable return on investment (ROI).

Consultants are evaluated solely on policy mitigated, procurement opportunities captured, and strategic relationships formalized that directly benefit Dell's business units.

🎯

Action-Oriented

Transition from advisory roles to targeted execution. Identify risks before they are public, and present mitigation strategies simultaneously.

📡

Proprietary Intel

Do not report on news available in the press. We require forward-looking intelligence: unfiled draft language and internal agency shifts.

Strategic Priorities

Section Context: Outlines the four core policy pillars where Dell demands consultant focus. Interacting with the "Consultant Directive" button on each card reveals the specific, actionable expectations tied to that policy area, ensuring efforts align with corporate strategy.

💻

Public Sector Procurement

Securing government IT modernization contracts, shaping RFP requirements early, and defending existing infrastructure footprints against competitors.

Mandate: Identify funding streams 12-18 months prior to RFP release. Draft technical specification recommendations favorable to Dell architecture for agency CIOs.
🛡️

Cybersecurity & Privacy

Navigating patchwork data privacy laws and ensuring federal/state cybersecurity mandates are technologically feasible and globally harmonized.

Mandate: Prevent unworkable compliance mandates. Actively insert "safe harbor" provisions for compliant hardware/software providers in all drafting phases.
🤖

AI & Emerging Tech

Ensuring AI regulations focus on end-use applications and risk, rather than stifling foundational hardware, compute infrastructure, and open-source innovation.

Mandate: Educate lawmakers on the distinction between AI software/models and the supporting hardware infrastructure. Block hardware-level regulatory chokepoints.
🌐

Supply Chain & Trade

Advocating for policies that fortify global tech supply chains, utilizing funding mechanisms, and mitigating the operational impact of tariffs.

Mandate: Maintain direct, weekly dialogue with Commerce Department officials. Provide Dell with early warning on export control adjustments before publication.

Prescriptive Output

Section Context: Replaces ambiguous "staying in touch" with concrete, measurable deliverables. Users can click through the frequency tabs to view the strict reporting and output requirements expected on a weekly, monthly, and ad-hoc basis.

🗓️ Weekly Requirements

  • 01
    Legislative Tracking Matrix

    Updated spreadsheet of all bills flagged by Dell, including exact current status, next scheduled committee action, and Dell's probability assessment of passage.

  • 02
    "Forward-Looking" Intelligence Memo

    A strict 1-page bulleted list of rumors, unfiled amendments, and agency budget shifts that are not yet public knowledge.

  • 03
    Strategic Sync Call (15-30 mins)

    Not a reading of the news. Strictly focused on actionable decisions needed from Dell internally to support the consultant's ongoing execution.

📅 Monthly Requirements

  • 01
    Stakeholder Mapping & Introductions

    Delivery and execution of at least 2 highly targeted, substantive introductions between Dell Subject Matter Experts and key committee staff or agency deputies.

  • 02
    Draft Language Submission

    Proactive drafting of bill amendments, testimony text, or RFI responses for Dell's review. Consultants are expected to hold the pen first.

  • 03
    Procurement Pipeline Review

    Identification of at least 1 new, unreleased public sector IT project/budget allocation where Dell can shape the narrative and specifications early.

⚡ Urgent & Ad-Hoc Requirements

  • 01
    Rapid Response Plan (24hr SLA)

    Immediate threat assessment and mitigation action plan required within 24 hours of a hostile legislation introduction or competitive procurement threat.

  • 02
    Executive Hearing Preparation

    Creation of full briefing books, anticipated Q&A documents, and scheduling of mock hearings for any Dell executives testifying before government bodies.

Compliance Rules

Section Context: Outlines the non-negotiable legal, ethical, and corporate guardrails. Consultants act as an extension of the Dell brand, requiring absolute adherence to global anti-corruption and lobbying disclosure laws.

⚠️ Zero Tolerance Policy

Consultants operating on behalf of Dell Technologies must strictly adhere to the U.S. Foreign Corrupt Practices Act (FCPA), the UK Bribery Act, and all applicable local/federal lobbying disclosure laws. Any violation or failure to report will result in immediate termination for cause.

1. Gift and Entertainment Ban

Absolutely no gifts, meals, entertainment, or travel may be provided to any government official or staff member utilizing Dell funds without explicit, prior written approval from the Dell Global Ethics & Compliance Office.

2. Conflict of Interest Disclosure

Consultants must maintain an active disclosure of all other clients in the technology sector. Taking on representation for direct competitors (e.g., HP, Lenovo, Cisco) must be cleared through Dell Govt Affairs leadership prior to engagement.

3. Registration and Reporting

Consultants are solely responsible for filing all necessary lobbying registrations accurately and on time. Dell Accounts Payable will freeze all invoice payments if consultant registrations are found to be lapsed or deficient.
