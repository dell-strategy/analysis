Executive Summary & Macro Landscape

Minnesota's current political climate is defined by a "Trifecta" (DFL control of House, Senate, and Governor's office), leading to rapid legislative movement on progressive initiatives. The state boasts a robust budget surplus, though warnings of future tightening exist. The administration is prioritizing digital equity, modernizing legacy systems (technical debt reduction), and improving citizen experience ("One Minnesota").

For Dell Technologies, this represents a pivotal moment. The state is actively looking to move away from CapEx-heavy legacy ownership models where workforce shortages make maintenance impossible, leaning heavily toward OpEx, managed services, and hybrid cloud architectures.

Legislative Finance Committees

State & Local Gov Committees

Analysis for Dell

Elected officials do not care about "specs." They care about outcomes. Pitch modernization as "Cost Savings," "Cyber Safety for Citizens," and "Faster Service Delivery."

  • Key Meeting: Focus on Agency Commissioners who report to the Governor.
  • Fiscal Trigger: Biennial budget sessions (Odd years are budget setting, Even years are bonding/capital).

Funding Mechanics

While MNIT procures the technology, the Agencies request the funding from the legislature via the Governor's budget. Your sales motion must start with the Agency Commissioner to ensure the project gets into the budget request.

IT Leadership (MNIT)

Tarek Tomes
Commissioner & State CIO (MNIT)

Controls the architecture standards. Central authority for security, identity, and cloud strategy.

Agency-Based CIOs (CBTOs)
Chief Business Tech Officers

Embedded in agencies (DHS, MnDOT, Revenue). They understand the specific business pain points, define technical requirements, and drive demand up to central MNIT.

Enterprise Architecture Board
The Gatekeepers

Reviews all net-new project requests to ensure they align with the state's "Cloud Smart" and consolidation strategies. (Must pass this board for APEX/Hardware approvals).

Analysis for Dell

MNIT is a "consolidated" organization, meaning agency IT staff legally report to the central CIO. However, budgets often remain with the agency.

  • The Gap: MNIT sets standards, but Agency Commissioners hold the budget. You must bridge this gap by selling the business value to the Agency, and the technical validity to MNIT.
  • Key Target: Win the CBTOs (Agency CIOs). They are your champions to the central MNIT authority.

Incumbent Landscape & Technical Debt

⚠️ Compute & Storage

Aging SAN infrastructure across tier-2 agencies. High footprint of legacy VMware environments facing renewal uncertainty. Opportunity: PowerStore tech refresh and VxRail consolidation.

☁️ Cloud Strategy

Initial rush to hyperscalers (AWS/Azure) has resulted in cost overruns and complex egress fees for data-heavy agencies. Opportunity: Dell APEX as a predictable hybrid-cloud alternative for steady-state workloads.

🛡️ Data Protection

Fragmented backup solutions across decentralized agency offices. Lack of immutable storage for ransomware protection. Opportunity: Statewide PowerProtect Cyber Recovery vault initiative.

Strategic Initiatives

Where the state is actively trying to move the needle.

Cloud Smart

Moving away from "Cloud First" to "Cloud Smart." Evaluating workloads for repatriation or hybrid fits (Dell Apex opportunity).

Cyber Resilience

Whole-of-state approach. Protecting counties and schools, not just state agencies. Identity management focus.

Digital Equity

Broadband expansion (BEAD funding) and device accessibility for underserved populations.

Data Modernization

Unlocking data silos between HHS, Corrections, and Education to create a comprehensive citizen view.

High-Impact Agency Modernization Efforts

Dept. of Human Services (DHS)

System Modernization & Case Management Overhaul

Largest IT Spender

DHS is undertaking a massive, multi-year effort to replace legacy mainframe-based eligibility systems (MEDS/MAXIS). This requires massive data migration, secure staging environments, and high-compute hybrid infrastructure capable of handling HIPAA/PII data securely.

Dept. of Transportation (MnDOT)

Connected Corridors & Edge Infrastructure

IIJA Grant Funded

Leveraging Federal Infrastructure bills, MnDOT is expanding IoT sensors along highways, smart snowplow telemetry, and traffic camera analysis. This generates petabytes of unstructured data requiring edge compute (VxRail/XR servers) and scalable Isilon/PowerScale storage.

Dept. of Public Safety (DPS)

NextGen 911 & Body Worn Camera Retention

High Data Velocity

Mandates for state patrol body cameras are driving explosive storage needs. Strict CJIS compliance requires isolated, highly secure on-prem or sovereign cloud storage. Massive opportunity for Unstructured Data Solutions (UDS).
