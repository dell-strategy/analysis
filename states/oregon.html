Oregon budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle and adopted a record $138.9B all-funds budget for 2025–27. For an SLG seller, three things define the motion. First, technology is centralized: Enterprise Information Services (EIS), an office within the Department of Administrative Services (DAS) led by State CIO Terrence Woods, sets statewide IT strategy, runs Data Center Services and Cyber Security Services, and gatekeeps every IT project over $1M. Second, the cloud gate is shifting to GovRAMP — EIS's 'Phase 1: Accept' program now requires GovRAMP-aligned security provisions in all new cloud/hosted-service solicitations under ORS 276A.300, so cloud and as-a-service offerings need to clear that bar early. Third, the Hillsboro-area 'Silicon Forest' (Intel's Gordon Moore Park R&D campus plus a fast-growing Flexential/Aligned data-center cluster drawn by no sales tax and renewable power) makes Oregon a national AI-infrastructure market — even as grid capacity becomes the binding constraint. Buying runs through DAS Statewide Price Agreements, OregonBuys, the ORCPP cooperative, and NASPO ValuePoint.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure / data-center demand tied to the Hillsboro 'Silicon Forest' cluster and Intel's R&D expansion (PowerEdge, networking, services, liquid-cooling).
  • Governed AI adoption as agencies operationalize the State AI Action Plan — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive data with auditable governance.
  • Cyber and cloud modernization under EIS Cyber Security Services as GovRAMP rolls out across statewide cloud contracts.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across a record general-government budget and the higher-ed research-computing buyers (OSU, UO).

  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Hillsboro Silicon Forest data-center cluster and OSU/UO research computing — and bring a power/cooling-efficiency angle given the grid constraint.
  • Clear the GovRAMP gate before pursuing cloud/as-a-service deals — confirm the right certification level for any offering touching state data under EIS Phase 1: Accept.

  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as agencies operationalize the State AI Action Plan.

Budget & Fiscal

Oregon budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle. The 2025 Legislature adopted a $138.9B all-funds budget for 2025–27 — roughly $37.3B General Fund, $1.8B Lottery, $56.1B Other Funds, and $43.7B Federal Funds — a 5.9% increase over 2023–25. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with EIS providing enterprise governance and shared services. Any agency IT project of $1M or more must be documented to EIS, a useful early signal for deal forecasting.

Initiatives & Policy

Silicon Forest Data-Center & Semiconductor Cluster9

Accelerating

Hillsboro and the surrounding 'Silicon Forest' have become one of the nation's most competitive data-center markets — Flexential is building a fifth, ~36MW Hillsboro facility (online 2026) and planning a sixth, alongside Aligned and others, drawn by no sales tax, enterprise-zone incentives, and renewable power. Intel continues to anchor the cluster with a major Gordon Moore Park R&D expansion (D1X/D1A). This is the strongest tailwind for Dell AI-infrastructure and ISG demand in the state — with grid capacity now the gating constraint.

State AI Action Plan4

In implementation

Governor Kotek's AI Advisory Council (chaired by State CIO Terrence Woods, created via Executive Order 23-26) delivered a final Action Plan in February 2025 recommending five executive actions: AI governance structures, privacy/security, reference architecture, and workforce readiness. Agencies now need governed, auditable AI — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive workloads rather than open public models.

GovRAMP Cloud Security (EIS Phase 1: Accept)6

Rolling out

EIS Cyber Security Services has adopted GovRAMP (formerly StateRAMP). The 'Phase 1: Accept' program applies to all agencies under EIS information-security authority (ORS 276A.300): new and renewed cloud/hosted-service solicitations and contracts must carry GovRAMP-aligned provisions, and agencies complete a Cloud Checklist before acquiring cloud services. Any Dell cloud/as-a-service offering touching state data needs to clear this gate early, not at close.

Broadband Equity, Access & Deployment (BEAD)8

Awards finalized

Procurement & Competition

Oregon centralizes technology procurement under the Department of Administrative Services (DAS). DAS Procurement Services establishes Statewide Price Agreements (master contracts usable by all state entities), runs the OregonBuys eProcurement system, and operates the Oregon Cooperative Procurement Program (ORCPP) for local governments and other public bodies. EIS sets the IT and information-security requirements that overlay these vehicles — most importantly the GovRAMP cloud gate. NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts are also widely used.

Oregon is a centralized, contract-driven market — incumbents hold DAS price agreements and NASPO ValuePoint positions across compute, networking, and services.
