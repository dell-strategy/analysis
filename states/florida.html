Florida runs an annual budget on a July–June fiscal year. Gov. DeSantis signed his eighth and final FY2026–27 budget on June 29, 2026 at roughly $117.6B, after about $810M in line-item vetoes (and roughly $1.6B in total reductions counting a vetoed $750M rainy-day-fund transfer and a corrections bill). Three structural shifts matter for SLG sellers. First, the Florida Digital Service (FLDS) under State CIO Warren Sponholtz remains the central authority consolidating enterprise IT governance, security, and procurement oversight — the 2026 effort to move FLDS into the Governor's office as a new technology division (SB 480) DIED on the Senate calendar (March 13, 2026), so FLDS stays under the Department of Management Services; repeated restructuring attempts remain a watch item, especially with a new governor taking office in January 2027. Second, Florida enacted a first-in-the-nation hyperscale data-center law (SB 484, signed May 7, 2026) regulating power and water costs — a signal of how fast AI infrastructure is landing in the state. Third, $1.16B in federal BEAD broadband funding is moving into deployment in 2026 with the state's final proposal NTIA-approved and subgrant agreements being executed. For Dell, the buying motion runs through DMS State Term Contracts and MyFloridaMarketPlace, with FLDS gating large IT projects over $10M.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure demand tied to university research computing (UF HiPerGator AI 2.0) and the hyperscale data-center buildout (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • Enterprise security and lifecycle modernization as FLDS centralizes IT governance and consolidates agency purchasing.
  • Governed, private/on-prem AI for state agencies as Florida studies and regulates government AI use under FLDS direction.
  • Endpoint, client, and infrastructure refresh across a ~$115B general-government budget and 20+ agencies.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the hyperscale data-center buildout and the State University System's research-computing buyers (UF HiPerGator).
  • Map every targeted IT pursuit to FLDS standards and the $10M presolicitation certification gate — engage enterprise governance early, not at close.
  • Validate Dell's DMS State Term Contract coverage and resellers are current and correctly scoped, and confirm MyFloridaMarketPlace registration is in order.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive state workloads as Florida studies and regulates government AI use.
  • Track BEAD deployment for adjacent edge-compute, networking, and managed-services opportunities serving newly connected rural communities.

Budget & Fiscal

Florida budgets annually on a July 1–June 30 fiscal year. Gov. DeSantis signed the FY2026–27 budget on June 29, 2026 at roughly $117.6B — his eighth and final budget before leaving office — after about $810M in line-item vetoes (and roughly $1.6B in total reductions counting a vetoed $750M general-revenue transfer to the Budget Stabilization Fund and a separate corrections bill). The Budget Stabilization Fund sits at its $5.0B constitutional limit and total reserves reach nearly $18B. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with FLDS providing enterprise governance and oversight. The prior FY2025–26 General Appropriations Act totaled roughly $115B after ~$576M in vetoes.

Initiatives & Policy

Florida Digital Service — IT Consolidation2

Ongoing

FLDS, under State CIO Warren Sponholtz, is the central authority for statewide IT strategy, cybersecurity, enterprise governance, and project oversight, including a presolicitation certification gate for IT projects over $10M. The 2026 effort to restructure state IT and move FLDS into the Governor's office (SB 480) died on the Senate calendar in March 2026, so FLDS remains under DMS — but repeated restructuring attempts (SB 7026/ASSET in 2025, SB 480 in 2026) make enterprise IT governance a live watch item, especially with a gubernatorial transition due January 2027.

Hyperscale Data-Center Regulation (SB 484)6

Signed May 2026

SB 484 (signed May 7, 2026) is a first-in-the-nation framework requiring large 'hyperscale' AI data centers to pay their own power and infrastructure costs and protecting ratepayers and water supplies. The law signals the scale and speed of AI-infrastructure investment landing in Florida — a tailwind for Dell ISG, networking, and services demand even as it adds siting and utility scrutiny.

BEAD Broadband Deployment7

Deploying 2026

Florida's $1.16B BEAD allocation moved from competitive subgrant selection (with $970M+ announced in July 2025) into NTIA-approved deployment, with the state's final proposal approved and subgrant agreements being executed in 2026. The program expands rural connectivity and digital-adoption infrastructure — adjacent demand for edge compute, networking, and managed services serving newly connected communities and the agencies coordinating the rollout.

Government AI Policy & Study4

In progress

Florida's 2026 session debated AI heavily: SB 146 directs FLDS to study state-agency AI use, while the broader 'AI Bill of Rights' (SB 482) passed the Senate but died in the House in March 2026. The direction is toward governed, auditable AI in government — an opening for private/on-prem and controlled-cloud AI on sensitive state data rather than open public models.

University Research Computing (UF HiPerGator)8

Live

The University of Florida launched HiPerGator AI 2.0 in October 2025 — among the fastest university-owned supercomputers in the U.S., built on NVIDIA DGX B200 SuperPOD architecture and serving the State University System. Florida's research universities are large, independent AI-infrastructure and research-computing buyers with recurring refresh and expansion needs.

Procurement & Competition

The Florida Department of Management Services (DMS) is the lead procurement agency, running State Term Contracts, Alternate Contract Sources, and the MyFloridaMarketPlace (MFMP) eProcurement and vendor-registration system. The Florida Digital Service (FLDS) sets enterprise IT standards and oversees large IT projects, including a presolicitation certification gate for IT procurements over $10M.

Competitive landscape

Florida is a competitive, contract-driven market — incumbents hold DMS State Term Contracts across compute, networking, and services. Differentiation tends to come from AI-infrastructure capability, governed-AI and security offerings aligned to FLDS standards, and services mapped to the data-center buildout and university research computing rather than price alone.
