Florida runs an annual budget on a July–June fiscal year; the FY2025–26 General Appropriations Act came in near $115B after roughly $576M in line-item vetoes, and the Governor's proposed Floridians First FY2026–27 budget is about $117.4B. Three structural shifts matter for SLG sellers. First, the Florida Digital Service (FLDS) under State CIO Warren Sponholtz is consolidating enterprise IT governance, security, and procurement oversight — and 2026 legislation (SB 480) floated moving FLDS into the Governor's office as a new technology division, the latest in repeated attempts to restructure state IT. Second, Florida just enacted a first-in-the-nation hyperscale data-center law (SB 484, signed May 7, 2026) regulating power and water costs — a signal of how fast AI infrastructure is landing in the state. Third, $1.16B in federal BEAD broadband funding is moving into deployment in 2026. For Dell, the buying motion runs through DMS State Term Contracts and MyFloridaMarketPlace, with FLDS gating large IT projects.

Budget & Fiscal

Florida budgets annually on a July 1–June 30 fiscal year. The FY2025–26 General Appropriations Act totaled roughly $115.1B as passed (about $117.4B including back-of-the-bill items), reduced by ~$576M in line-item vetoes to a GAA near $114.8B. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with FLDS providing enterprise governance and oversight. The Governor's proposed Floridians First FY2026–27 budget was about $117.4B; the Legislature passed a roughly $114.5B FY2026–27 budget on May 29, 2026, which awaits the Governor's signature and line-item vetoes before the July 1 fiscal-year start.

Initiatives & Policy

Florida Digital Service — IT Consolidation2

Ongoing

FLDS, under State CIO Warren Sponholtz, is the central authority for statewide IT strategy, cybersecurity, enterprise governance, and project oversight. 2026 legislation (SB 480) strengthens FLDS authority over IT project management and procurement, adds a presolicitation certification gate for IT projects over $10M, and stands up new oversight structures — concentrating decisions and standards at the enterprise level.

Hyperscale Data-Center Regulation (SB 484)6

Signed May 2026

SB 484 (signed May 7, 2026) is a first-in-the-nation framework requiring large 'hyperscale' AI data centers to pay their own power and infrastructure costs and protecting ratepayers and water supplies. The law signals the scale and speed of AI-infrastructure investment landing in Florida — a tailwind for Dell ISG, networking, and services demand even as it adds siting and utility scrutiny.

BEAD Broadband Deployment7

Deploying 2026

Florida's $1.16B BEAD allocation moved from competitive subgrant selection (with $970M+ announced in July 2025) into NTIA-approved deployment beginning spring 2026. The program expands rural connectivity and digital-adoption infrastructure — adjacent demand for edge compute, networking, and managed services serving newly connected communities and the agencies coordinating the rollout.

Government AI Policy & Study4

In progress

Florida's 2026 session debated AI heavily: SB 146 directs FLDS to study state-agency AI use, while the broader 'AI Bill of Rights' (SB 482) passed the Senate but died in the House in March 2026. The direction is toward governed, auditable AI in government — an opening for private/on-prem and controlled-cloud AI on sensitive state data rather than open public models.

Procurement & Competition

The Florida Department of Management Services (DMS) is the lead procurement agency, running State Term Contracts, Alternate Contract Sources, and the MyFloridaMarketPlace (MFMP) eProcurement and vendor-registration system. The Florida Digital Service (FLDS) sets enterprise IT standards and oversees large IT projects, including a presolicitation certification gate for IT procurements over $10M.

Competitive landscape

Florida is a competitive, contract-driven market — incumbents hold DMS State Term Contracts across compute, networking, and services. Differentiation tends to come from AI-infrastructure capability, governed-AI and security offerings aligned to FLDS standards, and services mapped to the data-center buildout and university research computing rather than price alone.
