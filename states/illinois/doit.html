Overview

The Illinois Department of Innovation & Technology (DoIT) is the state's consolidated central IT agency, created to bring technology staff, systems, and infrastructure for the agencies under the Governor into a single organization. DoIT sets statewide technology direction, runs enterprise application delivery and legacy modernization, operates shared data-center and cloud infrastructure, and houses statewide cybersecurity (including the state Security Operations Center) and the state's generative-AI strategy work. It manages roughly $1.1B in state technology spend across its client agencies. Historically DoIT was funded largely by billing those client agencies for services; beginning in FY2026 the state started shifting a portion of that to direct General Revenue Fund appropriation, with corresponding reductions in client-agency IT lines. For a Dell SLG seller, DoIT is the enterprise technology buying center for Illinois state government — but the contract and purchasing mechanics run through Central Management Services (CMS) and the independent Chief Procurement Office, so the relationship map and the contract path are two different things.

Initiatives & Priorities

Cybersecurity & state SOC

DoIT runs statewide cybersecurity for agencies, boards, and commissions under the Governor, including a state Security Operations Center for monitoring and rapid response, MFA enforcement, and support extended to local governments and schools. Security modernization and threat-detection tooling are an active buying track.

Cybersecurity SOC
Generative AI strategy & governance

DoIT is standing up an enterprise-AI operating model: Illinois named its first Chief AI Officer (Kader Sakkaria, effective June 1, 2026) and CIO Brandon Ragle has described a 'triangle' of AI governance across the Chief AI Officer, Chief Data Officer, and Chief Enterprise Architect. Early use cases are role-based with a human-in-the-loop model, and DoIT has scaled a generative-AI chatbot to tens of thousands of state users. The state's Generative AI & NLP Task Force filed its landmark report in late 2024 / early 2025 with an addendum due Dec 31, 2026 — AI governance, use-case piloting, and procurement guardrails will shape near-term technology decisions.

Legacy modernization & enterprise apps

DoIT leads consolidation and modernization of legacy state systems and enterprise applications across more than 700 technology staff. Application refresh, platform migration, and the underlying compute/storage are recurring needs.

Procurement

Selling to Illinois state government means separating the relationship (DoIT, the technology owner) from the contract path. Procurement authority is centralized but split: Central Management Services (CMS) administers many statewide contracts, and the independent Chief Procurement Office (CPO) holds procurement authority under the Illinois Procurement Code. Solicitations and orders for IT/telecom typically run through the state's BidBuy system.
