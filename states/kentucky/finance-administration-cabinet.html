Kentucky Finance & Administration Cabinet

Kentucky's central business cabinet — it houses the Commonwealth Office of Technology (COT, the statewide IT authority) and the Office of Procurement Services (statewide contracts), making it the front door for both how the Commonwealth buys technology and how it runs it.

Overview

The Finance & Administration Cabinet (FAC) is the Commonwealth of Kentucky's central business and administrative arm — it manages the state budget execution, accounting, revenue, facilities, and statewide procurement. Two units inside FAC matter most to a technology seller. First, the Commonwealth Office of Technology (COT) is Kentucky's central IT organization: it sets enterprise IT policy and standards, runs the consolidated executive-branch infrastructure (data center, network, cloud, voice), and provides shared services and security across state agencies through an IT-consolidation model. Second, the Office of Procurement Services (within FAC's Office of the Controller) is the central purchasing authority for the executive branch — it owns the statewide Master Agreements and the eProcurement system most state, and many local and education, buyers transact through. FAC is therefore best understood as both the policy/operations owner (via COT) and the contracting path (via Procurement Services) for Kentucky public-sector technology. Recent themes: implementing the state's new AI-governance law (SB 4, 2025), a continued cloud-migration and IT-consolidation push under permanent CIO Jim Barnhart, and a 2026 legislative move to shift the KentuckyWired broadband network under COT/FAC.

Budget & Fiscal

FAC is a central administrative cabinet rather than a large program spender, so its leverage is structural — it executes the state budget, owns the procurement vehicles, and runs consolidated IT cost-recovery for the executive branch. Kentucky's enacted FY2026–2028 biennial budget (HB 500) is roughly $32.4B, appropriating about $15.4B General Fund in FY27 and $15.6B in FY28, with executive-branch base reductions of ~4% then ~3% — a tightening backdrop for discretionary IT spend.

Initiatives & Priorities

AI governance under SB 4

Kentucky's SB 4 (signed March 2025) created a risk-based AI-governance framework and an AI Governance Committee housed inside COT — setting standards, a registry of generative-AI systems, disclosure, and human-oversight requirements for state agencies. Any AI offering must fit this governance process; it is also an entry point for responsible-AI and AI-infrastructure conversations.

Cloud migration & IT consolidation

Under permanent CIO Jim Barnhart, COT continues a multi-year executive-branch IT-consolidation and cloud-migration strategy — Barnhart led prior consolidation, cloud, and statewide microwave/infrastructure modernization efforts. Infrastructure refresh, hybrid cloud, and managed services have a clear lane.

Cybersecurity & zero trust

COT's Office of the Chief Information Security Officer (OCISO) runs statewide security policy and is advancing toward a zero-trust model for data protection across remote and consolidated operations. Note the named CISO role has shown turnover — confirm current ownership before forecasting security deals.

KentuckyWired now under COT/FAC (HB 314 enacted)

HB 314 became law in March 2026 after the General Assembly overrode Gov. Beshear's veto (emergency clause; effective immediately). It moves the KentuckyWired statewide fiber network and the Kentucky Communications Network Authority (KCNA) out of the Office of the Governor and under COT / the Finance & Administration Cabinet, abolishing KCNA's separate executive-director post and placing COT's leader in charge. This materially expands COT's network/broadband remit — but note the transition is contested (Beshear called it an unconstitutional power grab) and rides atop ongoing KCNA/Accelecom litigation, so operating models and buying centers will be unsettled near-term.

Procurement

Selling to or through FAC runs on the Commonwealth's central vehicles, not one-off agency RFPs. Two things to confirm first: that Dell's Kentucky statewide Master Agreement / NASPO ValuePoint participating-addendum coverage is active and correctly scoped, and which buyer is authorized for a given vehicle (COT vs. agency vs. non-executive entities). Most opportunities and vendor registration run through Kentucky's eProcurement portal.
