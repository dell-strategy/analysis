Overview

Washington Technology Solutions (WaTech) is the state's consolidated central IT services agency, headquartered in Olympia. It operates Washington's shared technology backbone — central data centers, the state network, cloud platforms, and enterprise applications — and serves state agencies plus county, city, and tribal governments and public-benefit nonprofits. WaTech sets statewide IT strategy and enterprise architecture, and it houses both the Office of the State CIO and the Office of Cybersecurity (OCS), Washington's central security organization. A 2024 reorganization (SHB 1947) folded the former standalone Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO) into WaTech, concentrating policy, oversight, and shared-services delivery under one roof. WaTech is best understood as a proprietary (central-service) agency: rather than a large general-fund appropriation, most of its revenue comes from billing the agencies it serves through allocated rates, fee-for-service, and agreements. That makes it the enterprise relationship for selling into Washington state government, though much agency-specific buying and the largest IT budgets still sit with the line agencies themselves.

Budget & Fiscal

WaTech is a proprietary agency funded mainly through cost recovery — allocated rates, fee-for-service, and agreements billed to the agencies it serves — rather than a single large general-revenue line. Its operating authority sits largely in the WA Technology Solutions Revolving Account, so its budget reflects services delivered statewide more than its own discretionary spend. Initiatives & Priorities

IT Strategic Plan 2025-2028 & resident portal

WaTech's current plan centers on a trusted digital-government experience, data-driven decisions, emerging tech, and workforce. A flagship effort is a statewide resident portal with an AI chatbot to replace fragmented agency portals — a pull for cloud, data, and AI-ready infrastructure.

Digital Government AI Cloud
AI adoption & state AI Task Force

Washington issued generative-AI guidance under executive order and stood up a legislative AI Task Force (ESSB 5838, administered by the Attorney General) whose final report is due July 1, 2026. WaTech is piloting AI uses including reading legacy COBOL mainframe logic in plain English. Policy is still forming — track it as the gate on what AI offerings are buyable.

AI Policy Modernization
Legacy modernization & cloud

Modernization rose to NASCIO's top priorities, and WaTech is advancing it across agencies — mainframe/COBOL modernization, cloud platforms (noted use of Databricks, AWS, Azure), and data-center consolidation. A durable theme for infrastructure refresh, hybrid cloud, and migration services.

Modernization Data Center Cloud
Statewide cybersecurity (Office of Cybersecurity)

The Office of Cybersecurity, recognized in state law in 2021, is Washington's central defense and incident-response organization led by the State CISO. Continued investment in detection, response, identity, and resilience for state and local entities is an ongoing track.

Procurement

Selling into Washington state runs on pre-competed vehicles, not one-off RFPs. Statewide IT contracts and cooperative agreements are administered by the Department of Enterprise Services (DES), including NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts; WaTech is the enterprise IT customer and the policy/oversight authority. Confirm Dell's coverage on the relevant DES master and NASPO ValuePoint agreements first — it's the access path to far more than WaTech alone.
