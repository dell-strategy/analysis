Overview

The Washington Department of Social & Health Services (DSHS) is one of the state's largest agencies, delivering behavioral health (state psychiatric hospitals and community supports), long-term care and developmental disabilities services, and economic services such as SNAP/TANF benefits and child support. Leadership has publicly described the agency as running on roughly 18,000 staff and a two-year budget near $25 billion across all fund sources. For a technology seller, DSHS matters less as a policy authority than as a high-volume operational agency with a large, partly legacy data and IT estate — most notably the 30-year-old mainframe-based Automated Client Eligibility System (ACES), which underpins benefits eligibility statewide and is the centerpiece of the multi-agency Integrated Eligibility & Enrollment (IE&E) modernization. DSHS does not buy IT in a vacuum: major technology investments run through statewide governance, with Washington Technology Solutions (WaTech) and the Office of the CIO providing oversight and approval, and most procurement flowing through Department of Enterprise Services (DES) statewide and cooperative master contracts.

Budget & Fiscal

DSHS is a large direct spender, not a pass-through. The enacted 2025–27 state operating budget appropriates roughly $12.1 billion general fund–state to DSHS, and agency leadership has publicly cited an all-funds two-year budget near $25 billion. Capital and behavioral-health investments add to that, including a new forensic hospital on the Western State Hospital campus.

Initiatives & Priorities

Integrated Eligibility & Enrollment (IE&E) modernization

Multi-year, multi-agency program to replace the eligibility experience built on the aging ACES mainframe with a modern cloud foundation. Public materials describe roughly eight concurrent projects spanning mainframe stabilization and a new cloud platform — a long runway for cloud, data, integration, and services work.

Modernization Cloud Benefits
ACES legacy mainframe risk

ACES is a ~30-year-old system with millions of lines of legacy code carrying elevated failure risk. Public roadmap materials reference a Mainframe-as-a-Service arrangement to stabilize the platform while modern capabilities are built and ACES components are gradually decommissioned.

Behavioral health capital & data

Construction of a new forensic hospital at Western State Hospital plus community behavioral-health expansion creates demand for facility IT, clinical data systems, and secure infrastructure across the state hospital system.

AI, cyber & cloud under statewide priorities

Washington's CIO and WaTech are pushing AI, modernization, and cybersecurity — the top NASCIO 2026 themes. DSHS programs that touch eligibility, client, and health data sit squarely in scope for secure cloud, data protection, and emerging AI use cases under OCIO oversight.

Procurement

Selling to DSHS runs through Washington's centralized procurement and IT-oversight structure rather than purely agency-by-agency RFPs. Confirm three things early: that Dell holds the right DES statewide / cooperative master contracts, that any major IT investment is mapped to WaTech / OCIO approval and oversight, and whether a given workstream is procured directly by DSHS or jointly through the IE&E coalition.
