Delaware is the most centralized technology buyer in the country: the Department of Technology & Information (DTI) runs cybersecurity and IT for all three branches of government and the entire K-12 public/charter network, and statewide purchasing flows through OMB's Government Support Services. Three shifts matter for any SLG seller in 2026. First, new leadership — Governor Matt Meyer (in office since January 2025) installed Bob Osmond, formerly Virginia's Commonwealth CIO who directed ~$1.3B/year in tech procurement, as Delaware CIO in May 2026. Second, DTI is aggressively cloud-first: roughly 77% of state servers now live in Microsoft Cloud (Nebraska/North Carolina), and the FY27 budget ask funds more cloud, a new web-security system, and VPN/identity replacement. Third, AI governance is already operational — DTI's Enterprise GenAI Policy (July 2025) and a mandatory statewide AI training launched March 2026, both gated to US-based, enterprise tools. Delaware is small but disciplined: win the enterprise standard at DTI and you win the state.

Where Dell can play
  • Enterprise client and lifecycle refresh across a centralized executive branch and the statewide K-12 network DTI secures.
  • Cloud and hybrid infrastructure as DTI continues migrating off two internal data centers — storage, networking, and the on-prem footprint that remains.
  • Cybersecurity modernization: web-security system, VPN-to-modern-identity replacement, and admin-account hardening called out in the FY27 budget.
  • Governed, US-based AI for sensitive workloads that fits DTI's GenAI policy (enterprise Copilot/Bedrock allowed; foreign-origin tools prohibited).

Dell Action Plan

  • Build the relationship with new CIO Bob Osmond's DTI team now — a leadership reset is the moment to influence the enterprise standard that governs the whole state.
  • Lead with hybrid: position storage, networking, edge, and lifecycle for the on-prem estate that remains as DTI runs ~77% in Microsoft Cloud — complement, don't fight, the cloud-first posture.
  • Map directly to the funded FY27 security scope: web-security system, VPN-to-modern-identity replacement, and admin-account hardening.
  • Position US-based, governed/private AI that fits DTI's GenAI policy (enterprise tools allowed, foreign-origin prohibited) for sensitive workloads.
  • Confirm Dell and reseller coverage on GSS statewide contracts and MyMarketplace, and pursue University of Delaware separately as an independent research-computing/AI-infrastructure buyer.

Budget & Fiscal

Delaware budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1–June 30). Governor Meyer's FY2027 recommended budget, unveiled January 29, 2026, totals $6.94B in operating funds (about +4.99%) plus roughly $1.0B in capital/bond spending. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology is concentrated in DTI, whose FY27 request is about $69M — roughly $8M above its ~$61M base — with the increase aimed at cloud, security, and digital services.

DTI Enterprise GenAI Policy & AI Training9

In effect

DTI's Enterprise GenAI Policy (July 3, 2025) permits public tools (ChatGPT, Gemini) and enterprise tools (Microsoft Copilot, Amazon Bedrock) but bars uploading sensitive data to public models and prohibits tools originated or hosted outside the US (e.g., DeepSeek). A mandatory 'Responsible AI for Public Professionals' training launched March 2026. The Delaware AI Commission (created July 2024) guides adoption. The bar against foreign-origin tools favors governed, US-based and private AI.

Broadband Expansion (BEAD)6

Buildout phasing

Delaware was among the first states with an approved BEAD final proposal ($107M federal allocation). Federal rule changes in 2025 delayed rural Kent/Sussex fiber; the latest approved plan targets 4,728+ unserved/underserved locations with $27.8M federal plus ~$75M private (Verizon, Comcast), construction expected to begin Fall 2026. More a partner/ecosystem signal than a direct Dell motion, but it shapes the state's digital-services demand.

Procurement & Competition

Delaware procures technology as a single enterprise. OMB's Government Support Services (GSS) is the state's central contracting unit and administers most statewide contracts; covered agencies must buy material, equipment, and non-professional services through these contracts. DTI's Procurement Services team partners with OMB/GSS to facilitate selected technology agreements, and DTI sets the enterprise technology direction the rest of state government follows. The procurement portal is MyMarketplace.

Competitive landscape

Delaware is a small, tightly centralized market where the enterprise standard at DTI effectively decides the state. Microsoft is deeply embedded (cloud + Copilot), so incumbency is the main barrier. Differentiation comes from hybrid/on-prem and edge offerings that complement the cloud-first posture, US-based governed AI that fits DTI's policy, and security/identity capability — not price-per-unit alone.
