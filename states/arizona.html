Arizona is one of the most important AI-infrastructure stories in the country, and the buying motion is driven more by economic-development gravity than by a single big appropriation. TSMC's Phoenix campus has scaled from $12B to a committed ~$165B (six fabs, advanced packaging, R&D), and the company bought another 900 acres at a January 2026 state land auction — the largest foreign greenfield investment in U.S. history. Around it, Arizona has become a top-two U.S. data-center market alongside Texas, with multi-gigawatt projects (Hassayampa Ranch, the La Osa/Vermaland park) landing fast. On the state side, Governor Hobbs signed an $18.3B bipartisan FY2026 budget on June 13, 2026 that includes a three-year moratorium on data-center tax incentives — a signal the state is managing growth, not stopping it. Her 'Arizona Capacity and Efficiency' (ACE) Initiative consolidates IT purchasing and deploys AI across agencies to save $40M-$100M, and the state's first AI Steering Committee is delivering governance and procurement guidance in 2026. For Dell, the center of gravity is AI infrastructure (PowerEdge, networking, services) plus a real consolidation/standardization opening as ADOA-ASET centralizes spend.

Budget & Fiscal

Arizona budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1-June 30). Governor Hobbs signed an $18.3B bipartisan FY2026 budget on June 13, 2026. There is no single published 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with ADOA-ASET providing centralized enterprise technology services and the ACE Initiative now consolidating purchasing. The state's most consequential tech dollars sit outside the appropriations bill — in the private semiconductor and data-center buildout it is courting and, increasingly, managing.

Initiatives & Policy

TSMC Phoenix Semiconductor Megaproject4

Accelerating

TSMC's North Phoenix campus has expanded from $12B to a committed ~$165B — planned for six wafer fabs, two advanced-packaging (CoWoS) facilities, and an R&D center, the largest foreign greenfield investment in U.S. history. Fab 1 is in volume production; Fab 3 broke ground in 2025 for N2/A16; and the board approved buying another 900 acres at a January 2026 state land auction. This anchors an AI-hardware supply chain in-state and is the strongest tailwind for Dell ISG and AI-infrastructure demand around it.

Procurement & Competition

The Arizona Department of Administration (ADOA) State Procurement Office (SPO) is the central procurement authority. Most state and public-sector technology buys run through SPO statewide contracts and the Arizona State Procurement Cooperative Program, which gives eligible local governments, tribes, school districts, and other public bodies shared access to competitively sourced agreements (900+ statewide contracts). ADOA-ASET (Arizona Strategic Enterprise Technology) sets statewide IT policy and delivers shared enterprise technology, data-center, and security services.
