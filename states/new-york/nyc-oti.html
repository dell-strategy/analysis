Overview

The NYC Office of Technology & Innovation (OTI) is the City of New York's consolidated central technology agency, created in 2022 when the legacy Department of Information Technology and Telecommunications (DoITT, the prior account name "NYC Information Technology and Telecommunications") was reorganized and merged with the Mayor's Office of the CTO, NYC Cyber Command, the Mayor's Office of Data Analytics, the Office of Information Privacy, and the Algorithms Management and Policy Officer. OTI sets technology strategy and policy for roughly 100 City agencies and offices, runs shared IT infrastructure (data centers, networks, the Citywide Service Desk), and owns the public-facing digital layer: NYC.gov, the 311 non-emergency system, NYC Open Data, the MyCity benefits/services portal, and the Big Apple Connect broadband program. Cybersecurity for the City sits inside OTI via NYC Cyber Command, which protects and defends systems across all agencies. OTI is best understood as both a large IT operator and the technical/contractual gatekeeper for citywide technology buys — though agency procurement runs through NYC's contracting machinery (ACCOs, MOCS, DCAS) rather than a single state-style cooperative vehicle. A mayoral transition in January 2026 brought a new administration and a new technology leadership team.

Budget & Fiscal

OTI is a substantial direct IT spender and operator, not just a policy shop. For FY2026 the agency's expense budget is roughly $743M, supporting ~1,500 positions, plus about $203M in capital for IT modernization, cybersecurity, 311, and emergency-communications upgrades. Citywide capital technology investment tied to OTI runs into the high hundreds of millions to ~$1B across multi-year plans.

Initiatives & Priorities

MyCity portal & AI chatbot

OTI's flagship single front-door for City services and benefits, including the first citywide AI-powered chatbot. The chatbot expanded in 2025 to pull 311 and broader government-service content; a Jobs NYC portal was added. Modernization, content, and responsible-AI governance are active workstreams.

Digital Services AI
NYC AI Action Plan & generative-AI governance

OTI published the nation's first comprehensive municipal AI Action Plan (37 actions) and stood up an AI Steering Committee; by FY2025 it had initiated or completed 32 of 37 actions. Generative-AI and computer-vision guidance were advanced in 2025 with updates planned for early 2026 — a governance gate for any AI offering sold into the City.

AI Governance
Cybersecurity via NYC Cyber Command

Cyber Command sits inside OTI and defends ~100+ agencies (identify/protect/detect/respond/recover), runs a vulnerability-disclosure program, and leads citywide incident response. Security infrastructure, identity, and resilience are ongoing investment areas under the City CISO.

Cybersecurity Resilience
Broadband & digital equity

Big Apple Connect (the nation's largest municipally subsidized broadband program, 330K+ NYCHA residents) was extended to June 2028, and OTI released its first Digital Equity Roadmap (11 initiatives) in March 2025. Council scrutiny has pushed OTI toward a broader broadband strategy beyond BAC.

Broadband Digital Equity

Procurement

Unlike a state co-op model, NYC technology buying runs through the City's centralized contracting framework. OTI sets citywide IT direction and leverages the City's buying power through requirement/citywide contracts, while procurement mechanics flow through Agency Chief Contracting Officers (ACCOs), the Mayor's Office of Contract Services (MOCS), and DCAS. Confirm contract vehicles and OTI's cloud-review gate before forecasting.
