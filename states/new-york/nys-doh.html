Overview

The New York State Department of Health (DOH) is the state's lead agency for public health, healthcare regulation, and the Medicaid program — and by virtue of Medicaid it is one of the largest single line items in the entire state budget. DOH oversees disease surveillance, health-facility oversight, the state's vital records and registries, and the Medicaid program that covers roughly 7+ million New Yorkers. That mission rides on heavy-duty technology: the eMedNY Medicaid Management Information System (MMIS) that processes claims and payments, large public-health and health-data platforms, and the eligibility and enrollment systems New Yorkers use to access coverage. Most of DOH's enterprise IT is delivered through the consolidated NYS Office of Information Technology Services (ITS) rather than a standalone DOH IT shop, so the buying picture spans both the agency's program offices and the central ITS organization. For a Dell SLG seller, DOH is best understood as one of New York's largest health-data and Medicaid-systems buyers — a source of sustained demand for compute, storage, data platforms, security, and modernization, much of it routed through ITS and the state's centralized OGS contracts.

Budget & Fiscal

DOH's budget is dominated by Medicaid, which makes it one of the biggest spending agencies in New York. In the FY2026–27 enacted budget, DOH Medicaid is projected near $40B (state reporting cited ~16% growth, though much of that reflects a managed-care-organization tax mechanism; underlying growth was closer to ~7.5%). Technology spend is a smaller slice of that total and is largely delivered and procured through NYS ITS and OGS rather than appearing as a discrete DOH IT line.

Initiatives & Priorities

Medicaid eligibility modernization (MECM)

DOH is running a multi-year effort to modernize Medicaid eligibility and enrollment — moving toward a centrally administered, consumer-centric model and a modern Medicaid Eligibility and Client Management System (MECM). It's framed as a foundation for future DOH IT projects, which makes it a strategic platform and infrastructure opportunity.

Medicaid Modernization
eMedNY / MMIS claims platform

eMedNY is the state's Medicaid Management Information System — real-time eligibility verification, claims processing, and payments at very large scale, long operated under a system-integrator contract (GDIT). Any compute, storage, data, or modernization tied to MMIS is high-value and long-lived.

Medicaid Data Systems
Provider Services Portal (PSP)

DOH is rolling out an online provider enrollment portal (PSP) to modernize the provider-enrollment process — part of the broader shift from paper/legacy workflows to digital systems, with associated application, data, and infrastructure needs.

Modernization Digital Services
Public-health data & security platforms

DOH runs large public-health surveillance, registry, and vital-records systems carrying sensitive PHI. Data-platform modernization, resilience, and cybersecurity for these systems are recurring demand — typically delivered through ITS and subject to state security standards.
