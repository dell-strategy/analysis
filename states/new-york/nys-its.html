Overview

The NYS Office of Information Technology Services (ITS) is New York's consolidated executive-branch IT agency, created in 2012 to merge dozens of fragmented agency IT shops into a single enterprise organization — one of the largest consolidated public-sector IT operations in the country. ITS delivers infrastructure, networking, end-user services, application development, cybersecurity, and increasingly AI for the executive branch's 50+ agencies. Under State CIO and ITS Director Dru Rai (appointed October 2023), the agency is executing a 'singular service provider' strategy: positioning ITS as the central buyer and operator of technology for all state agencies, moving toward a Dedicated Agency service model that pairs centralized ITS support with embedded agency subject-matter experts. ITS runs a hybrid environment — it maintains the state's on-premises data center while layering in public cloud, most notably through a June 2026 enterprise agreement with AWS for cloud and AI services. For a Dell SLG seller, ITS is the dominant enterprise account in New York: it controls statewide infrastructure refresh, the on-prem data-center footprint, and the cyber and AI buildout for the bulk of state government.

Budget & Fiscal

ITS is a large direct spender and operator, not just a procurement conduit — it runs the state's enterprise infrastructure and recovers cost from served agencies. Its most recently documented all-funds appropriation is roughly $1.2B, sitting inside a state all-funds budget in the ~$260B range for FY2027. Recent budgets have grown the agency specifically to fund cybersecurity and AI and to add staff that displaces contractor spend.

Initiatives & Priorities

AWS enterprise cloud & AI agreement

In June 2026 ITS announced a three-year enterprise agreement making AWS cloud and AI services available to participating state agencies at below-independent-procurement cost, part of an enterprise-agreement strategy ITS credits with ~$58M in savings. ITS frames this as additive to a hybrid model — public cloud alongside the retained on-prem data center.

Cloud AI
'Singular service provider' / Dedicated Agency model

Rai's strategy positions ITS as the single technology provider for all NYS agencies, transitioning to a Dedicated Agency service model that pairs centralized ITS delivery with embedded agency experts. This concentrates buying authority — the enterprise relationship is with ITS, not the individual agencies.

Modernization Operating Model
AI governance & adoption

ITS appointed a Chief AI Officer (Eleonore Fournier-Tombs) and is standing up statewide AI governance and adoption alongside the state's broader AI investments (e.g., Empire AI). AI infrastructure, data, and governance tooling are an emerging demand center.

AI Governance
Cybersecurity expansion

Under CISO Chris DeSain, ITS continues to expand statewide cyber operations — including the Joint Security Operations Center — protecting state employees, systems, and devices. Recent budgets have specifically funded cyber staffing and tooling.

Cybersecurity Modernization

Procurement

Selling to ITS runs through two channels: New York's centralized OGS contracts (the statewide procurement backbone most agencies buy off) and ITS's own enterprise agreements. ITS's 'singular service provider' posture and its enterprise-deal strategy (e.g., the AWS agreement) mean the highest-leverage path is the central ITS enterprise relationship, not agency-by-agency selling.
