Overview

The New York State Unified Court System (UCS) is the judicial branch of New York and one of the largest court systems in the country. It is administered by the Office of Court Administration (OCA), with operations split between Albany and a headquarters presence in Lower Manhattan (25 Beaver Street). By the state's own published figures the system spans 300+ state courts plus roughly 1,300 town and village courts across 13 judicial districts and all 62 counties, with about 1,200 state-paid judges and roughly 15,500 non-judicial employees, hearing on the order of three million-plus cases a year. For a Dell SLG seller the relevant fact is that UCS runs this on a large, centrally managed technology estate — statewide network, data centers, e-filing (NYSCEF), and case-management systems supported by OCA's Division of Technology — making it a distinct enterprise IT buyer alongside the executive-branch state agencies, not a sub-unit of them. The system is independently budgeted: the Judiciary submits its own budget request to the Legislature each year rather than competing inside the Governor's executive agency lines.

Budget & Fiscal

The Judiciary is funded as its own branch and submits an annual budget directly to the Legislature, signed by the Chief Judge and Chief Administrative Judge. The FY2027 request totals roughly $3.2 billion in state operating funds — an increase of close to $200 million (about 6.7%) over the prior year — and notably carries dedicated capital dollars for technology and public-safety infrastructure. IT spend is embedded in operating and capital rather than broken out as a single public line, so figures below frame the envelope, not a discrete tech budget.

Initiatives & Priorities

NYSCEF e-filing & digital case management

The New York State Courts Electronic Filing system (NYSCEF) handles electronic filing across civil, criminal, family, surrogate's, appellate and other courts, with more than a million cases e-filed to date. A parallel modernization track is the Universal Case Management System (UCMS), live in Surrogate's Courts statewide and integrating with NYSCEF. Both depend on resilient compute, storage and integration infrastructure.

Digital Courts Modernization
Interim Policy on the Use of AI

In October 2025 the UCS issued a first-of-its-kind Interim Policy on the Use of Artificial Intelligence, growing out of an advisory committee formed in 2024. It limits generative-AI use to UCS-approved tools and sets guardrails on fairness, accountability and security. An AI advisory committee has since issued an inaugural report — signaling demand for governed, on-prem/private AI tooling.

Cyber resilience & infrastructure hardening

As a high-value judicial target holding sensitive case data, UCS continues to invest in cybersecurity and infrastructure resilience across its statewide network and data centers. Public reporting on any specific 2025 incident is limited; frame this as an ongoing resilience and modernization posture rather than a single named event, and confirm specifics before forecasting.

Statewide IT consolidation under OCA

OCA's Division of Technology centrally supports network, data center, software development and end-user technology for ~20,000 court personnel across hundreds of locations — a consolidated enterprise estate that favors standardized refresh, lifecycle and managed-services plays over fragmented court-by-court buying.
