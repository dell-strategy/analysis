Overview

The New York City Department of Environmental Protection (DEP) manages the city's water supply, wastewater treatment, and water/sewer infrastructure — the largest municipal water utility in the U.S. It delivers more than 1 billion gallons of drinking water a day to roughly 8 million city residents (and additional upstate communities), operates about 6,600 miles of water mains and 7,400+ miles of sewers, and runs 14 wastewater treatment plants handling on the order of 1.3 billion gallons a day, plus 19 reservoirs across a multi-county watershed. DEP's technology profile is two-sided: a deep operational-technology (OT) layer — SCADA, PLCs, HMI, telemetry, and pumping/plant control across all five boroughs — sits alongside enterprise IT for billing, asset management, and customer service. Citywide IT strategy, cybersecurity policy, cloud standards, and much procurement run through the NYC Office of Technology and Innovation (OTI), but DEP retains its own substantial engineering and technology footprint, including a Bureau of Wastewater Treatment 'digital transformation' organized around SCADA support, IT/cybersecurity, asset management, and application development. For a Dell SLG seller, DEP is an OT-modernization and critical-infrastructure-security account that must be navigated alongside OTI's citywide policy and contracting layer.

Budget & Fiscal

DEP is funded primarily through the self-supporting NYC water and sewer system — water/wastewater rates set by the NYC Water Board cover operations and capital debt rather than the city's general fund. The FY2026 operating budget is roughly $1.88B, and DEP runs one of the city's largest capital programs, on the order of $33–37B over ten years with capital commitments approaching ~$3B a year.

Initiatives & Priorities

Bureau of Wastewater Treatment digital transformation

DEP's wastewater bureau is modernizing around four pillars — SCADA support/development, IT support and cybersecurity, asset management, and application development — across 14 treatment plants and 90+ pumping facilities. A direct opening for OT/IT infrastructure, edge compute, and managed services.

OT/SCADA Modernization
Automated meter upgrade (AMR → AMI)

DEP is upgrading 600,000+ automated meter-reading devices citywide toward an advanced metering (AMI) network for real-time usage data and leak detection (network provided by Aclara). Generates data-platform, analytics, and security workloads.

IoT/Metering Data
OT/IT segmentation & water-sector cybersecurity

Proposed New York State water/wastewater cyber regulations would require separating OT systems from IT, incident-response plans, and 24-hour incident reporting — pushing utilities like DEP toward network segmentation, OT visibility, and resilience investment.

Cybersecurity Compliance
Asset management & resiliency

Aging mains, sewers, and treatment assets plus stormwater/coastal resiliency mandates drive an asset-management and data-modernization track — sensors, digital records, and analytics to prioritize state-of-good-repair capital.
