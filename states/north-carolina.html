North Carolina is one of the hottest AI-infrastructure markets in the country, but it is buying technology through an unusual political moment. The structural story is opportunity: Amazon's $10B Richmond County campus is the largest single capital project in state history, and Apple, Microsoft, Meta and Google are all expanding data centers in the state. Governor Josh Stein's September 2025 Executive Order 24 stood up an AI Leadership Council, an AI Accelerator inside NCDIT, and agency AI oversight teams, while NCDIT published the state's first five-year cybersecurity strategic plan (2025-2030) and doubled its security program. The complication: the General Assembly failed to enact a full FY2025-27 budget — North Carolina was the only state to end 2025 without one — and has been operating on mini-budgets. For Dell, demand and policy tailwinds are strong, but full-budget uncertainty means timing discipline matters. The buying motion runs through NCDIT Statewide IT Contracts, where RFP cycles have been cut from ~8 months to ~8 weeks.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure pull-through tied to the nation-leading data-center boom (PowerEdge, networking, services) and UNC-system research computing.
  • Governed, on-prem and private-cloud AI for agencies operationalizing Executive Order 24 and the NCDIT AI Accelerator pilots.
  • Whole-of-state cybersecurity buildout under the new 2025-2030 plan and a doubled NCDIT security program — secure compute, infrastructure, modernization.
  • Endpoint, client and lifecycle refresh across cabinet agencies and a faster NCDIT procurement pipeline (8-week RFPs).

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the record data-center boom and UNC-system research-computing buyers.
  • Engage NCDIT's AI Accelerator and agency AI oversight teams early — position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive EO 24 workloads.
  • Map to the 2025-2030 cybersecurity plan and whole-of-state model — secure compute, zero-trust infrastructure, and managed services across state and local.
  • Validate that Dell's NCDIT Statewide IT Contract coverage and resellers are current and scoped for target agencies; move at the pace of 8-week RFP cycles.
  • Watch the full-budget situation — sequence proposals around mini-budget timing and confirm appropriations before forecasting large agency deals.

Budget & Fiscal

North Carolina budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle, but the FY2025-27 cycle is atypical: the House and Senate deadlocked over income-tax cuts and never enacted a full budget, leaving the state operating on a series of 'mini-budgets.' Governor Stein's recommended budget was roughly $33.6B for FY2025-26 and $34.3B for FY2026-27. There is no single statewide 'IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies and a set of named technology, AI, cybersecurity and broadband programs.

Procurement & Competition

The N.C. Department of Information Technology (NCDIT) is the central technology procurement authority. Its Statewide IT Procurement Office develops and manages Statewide IT Contracts available to state agencies, local government and educational entities, and sets standards for IT products and services. Non-IT goods run through the Department of Administration's Division of Purchase & Contract. NCDIT has sharply compressed RFP cycles — from roughly eight months to eight weeks.
