Mississippi is punching far above its budget weight in technology. AWS has committed $25B across the state — the largest capital investment in Mississippi history — anchored by 'Project Atlas' in Madison County and additional campuses in Warren, Hinds and Clinton, turning the Magnolia State into national AI infrastructure. At the same time, the 2026 legislature passed a tight pair of laws: the IT Optimization Act (SB2653), which centralizes technology administration and kills duplicative contracts through ITS, and SB2654, creating a statewide State Security Operations Center (SOC) inside ITS. The Mississippi Department of Information Technology Services (MDITS/ITS) is the central procurement and approval gate for essentially all state IT — any acquisition above a modest threshold must run through ITS. For Dell, the data-center boom is the headline AI-infrastructure angle, but the buying motion runs through ITS, and the new SOC plus a centralizing mandate create fresh infrastructure and refresh demand.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure / 'AI factory' demand tied to the $25B AWS data-center buildout and the broader central-Mississippi compute corridor (PowerEdge, networking, services, supporting ecosystem).
  • Stand-up of the State Security Operations Center inside ITS — secure compute, infrastructure, and managed services for a greenfield cyber function.
  • IT Optimization Act consolidation — contract rationalization and shared services create displacement and standardization openings across dozens of agencies.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across state agencies and the public university (IHL) system as ITS centralizes purchasing.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the $25B AWS data-center buildout and the central-Mississippi compute corridor, including the supporting ecosystem and IHL research-computing buyers.
  • Engage MDITS/ITS as the decisive central gate — confirm Dell's ITS contract/EPL coverage and resellers are current and correctly scoped before pursuing any agency.
  • Pursue the new State Security Operations Center as a greenfield cyber account inside ITS — secure compute, infrastructure, and managed services.
  • Map consolidation and standardization plays to the IT Optimization Act — position Dell as the standard for refresh and shared services as ITS rationalizes contracts.
  • Use the MDA / data-center momentum and Orgeron's AWS background as relationship entry points, then route the actual buying motion through ITS.

Budget & Fiscal

Mississippi budgets on an annual cycle with the fiscal year beginning July 1. The FY2026 General Fund is roughly $7.0B, set against an adopted revenue estimate near $7.6B. There is no single consolidated 'state IT budget' line — technology spend is distributed across agencies and a handful of named initiatives, with MDITS/ITS acting as the central procurement and approval authority rather than a large direct spender.

Procurement & Competition

The Mississippi Department of Information Technology Services (MDITS/ITS) is the central technology procurement and approval authority for state government. ITS facilitates procurement of IT hardware, software, and services for state agencies and public universities (IHLs) as required by statute, and holds approval authority over IT acquisitions — acquisitions above ~$250,000 lifecycle cost require ITS involvement, with limited delegated authority below that threshold. This central-gate structure means the ITS relationship is decisive for nearly every state deal.

Competitive landscape

Mississippi is a centralized, ITS-gated market — winning means being correctly positioned on ITS contracts and standards rather than competing agency-by-agency. Differentiation comes from AI-infrastructure capability tied to the data-center boom, security offerings mapped to the new State SOC, and consolidation/standardization plays aligned to the IT Optimization Act.
