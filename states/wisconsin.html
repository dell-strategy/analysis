Wisconsin enters the 2025–27 biennium with a $111.1B all-funds budget (2025 Act 15, signed July 3, 2025) and a once-in-a-generation AI-infrastructure story. Microsoft is building one of the world's most powerful AI data centers on the former Foxconn site in Mount Pleasant — a $3.3B first phase coming online in early 2026, plus a committed second campus that pushes total investment past $7B. Separately, Vantage/Oracle/OpenAI broke ground in December 2025 on the $15B+ 'Lighthouse' Stargate campus in Port Washington (~900MW). For Dell, the state's technology buying is centralized: the Department of Administration's Division of Enterprise Technology (DET) sets IT direction and the State Bureau of Procurement runs statewide contracts published through VendorNet, with DOA Secretary sign-off required on IT buys over $150K. Governor Evers' AI and broadband (BEAD, $1B+) agendas add agency-modernization tailwinds.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure / 'AI factory' demand tied to the Mount Pleasant and Port Washington hyperscale buildout — PowerEdge, networking, storage, and services to the broader supply-chain and edge ecosystem around these campuses.
  • Whole-of-state cybersecurity buildout under DET and the State & Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (SLCGP) — secure infrastructure and managed detection/response for state, local, K-12, and tribal entities.
  • Agency modernization and endpoint/lifecycle refresh across a record $111.1B biennial budget centralized under DET.
  • Governed AI adoption following the Governor's Task Force on Workforce and AI — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive government workloads.

Budget & Fiscal

Wisconsin budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle. The 2025–27 budget, enacted as 2025 Act 15 (signed July 3, 2025), totals roughly $111.1B all funds. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with the Department of Administration's Division of Enterprise Technology (DET) providing shared infrastructure and the State Bureau of Procurement running statewide IT contracts. Cybersecurity and broadband are funded through a mix of state appropriations and federal grant programs.

Whole-of-State Cybersecurity (DET / SLCGP)11

Active

DET sets statewide cybersecurity policy on a NIST 800-53 Rev. 5 baseline and coordinates a whole-of-state approach across state agencies, local government, K-12, higher-ed, and tribes. The federally funded State & Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (SLCGP), administered with Wisconsin Emergency Management, funds local hardening — projects run June 2026 through August 2027, capped at $100K per entity. An opening for secure infrastructure and managed detection/response.

Procurement & Competition

Technology buying in Wisconsin is centralized under the Department of Administration (DOA). The State Bureau of Procurement (SBOP) administers procurement law and runs statewide contracts for goods and services used across state government, UW System, and participating municipalities. Contracts and solicitations are published through VendorNet, the state's online purchasing portal, which also supports cooperative purchasing. Under Executive Order 288, agencies must obtain DOA Secretary approval before making an IT purchase over $150,000.
