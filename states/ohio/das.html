Overview

The Ohio Department of Administrative Services (DAS) is the state's central business-operations agency — it provides shared services across HR, fleet, real estate, general services, and information technology to Ohio's executive-branch agencies. For a technology seller, two parts of DAS matter most. First, the DAS Office of Information Technology (OIT), led by the State Chief Information Officer, sets statewide IT policy and standards, runs the InnovateOhio Platform (the state's shared cloud, data-analytics, and OHID digital-identity stack), and houses the Office of Information Security and Privacy. Second, DAS Procurement Services owns the State Term Schedules (STS) and the Cooperative Purchasing Program — the contract vehicles state agencies, counties, cities, K-12, higher ed, and other political subdivisions buy IT through, transacted on the OhioBuys e-procurement system. DAS is best understood as both Ohio's statewide IT authority and the procurement path into the broader Ohio public sector, rather than the state's largest end-user of technology.

Budget & Fiscal

DAS is funded through a mix of general revenue and cost recovery on the shared services it provides to agencies; its leverage is statewide IT direction and the contract vehicles, more than a single large IT line item. Ohio's FY2026–FY2027 operating budget (Am. Sub. H.B. 96) was signed by Gov. DeWine on June 30, 2025.

Initiatives & Priorities

InnovateOhio Platform (IOP)

The state's shared digital platform built on three pillars — user experience, OHID digital identity, and data analytics — with the data layer hosted in AWS. IOP is the consolidation point for cloud, identity, and analytics across agencies and a natural place to attach infrastructure, data, and AI offerings.

Cloud Data Digital Identity
Statewide AI governance

DAS supports a multi-agency AI Council and published a state AI policy setting guardrails for generative AI use, approval workflows, employee training (via InnovateUS), and procurement disclosure. By mid-2026 the program had scaled to 130+ approved AI use cases (roughly 87 in production, the rest in development/PoC), 15,000+ employees trained, and a dedicated multi-environment AI sandbox — a large, active governed-AI buying environment (figures per State CIO Flory; confirm before forecasting).

Cloud migration & modernization

OIT is moving agencies toward cloud with a 'teach them to fish' playbook model, plus CMS modernization (HCL to Contentful) and a contact-center platform refresh (RFP issued late 2025). Infrastructure, migration, and managed-services opportunities sit here.

Cybersecurity (OISP) with embedded AI

The Office of Information Security and Privacy runs statewide security tooling, now with AI-assisted threat monitoring and fraud detection. Note a leadership transition: CISO Holly Drake departed in December 2025, with deputy Matthew Hemker stepping in as interim CISO.
