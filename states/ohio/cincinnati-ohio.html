Overview

Cincinnati is Ohio's third-largest city (roughly 310,000 residents) and the urban core of the Cincinnati metro. It operates under a council-manager form of government: a directly elected Mayor (Aftab Pureval) and a nine-member City Council set policy, while a professional City Manager (Sheryl Long) runs day-to-day operations and the ~6,000-employee workforce. Central technology is consolidated in the Enterprise Technology Solutions (ETS) department, which owns citywide IT strategy, infrastructure, enterprise applications, cybersecurity, emergency communications, and the regional law-enforcement data systems (RCIC/CLEAR). For a Dell SLG seller, Cincinnati is a mid-market municipal account where buying decisions concentrate in ETS and the City Manager's office, purchasing runs through the city's Office of Procurement, and — like most Ohio public entities — cooperative and state contract vehicles are common paths to award.

Budget & Fiscal

Cincinnati funds a sizable enterprise: the FY2026–2027 biennial budget runs more than $1.8B all funds, with the General Fund (the discretionary pool where most IT operating dollars sit) at roughly $593M. The city closed a ~$29.5M General Fund gap in the FY27 cycle largely through vacancy savings, which tends to tighten new discretionary spend.
