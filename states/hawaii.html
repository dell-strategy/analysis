Hawaii budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle and runs roughly a $20B all-funds operating budget, with about $10.6B in general funds. Technology spend is not a single line item — it sits with the Office of Enterprise Technology Services (ETS) and is distributed across agencies and the University of Hawaii. Three threads matter most for a Dell seller. First, leadership: Christine Sakuda became State CIO and ETS administrator in 2025, the state's first female CIO of Native Hawaiian ancestry, and is driving a practical AI roadmap for agencies. Second, connectivity: the Connect Kākou initiative has $500M+ in federal, state, and private funds, including a ~$120M inter-island undersea fiber system (Hawaiian Islands Fiber Link) targeting service in late 2026 and ~$149.5M in BEAD funding. Third, context: Hawaii hosts U.S. Indo-Pacific Command (INDOPACOM), Pearl Harbor, and DISA Pacific — a dense federal/defense buyer base adjacent to (but separate from) the state market. Buying runs through the State Procurement Office and NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts; the University of Hawaii is a large independent research-computing and broadband buyer.

  • Broadband and inter-island connectivity buildout (Connect Kākou / HIFL) — networking, edge compute, and resilient infrastructure across six islands.
  • Disaster-resilience and recovery modernization in the wake of the 2023 Maui wildfires — secure, redundant compute and data protection.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across state agencies, the Department of Education, and the University of Hawaii.

Initiatives & Policy

Connect Kākou — Statewide Broadband5

Building

Connect Kākou is the Green administration's flagship broadband program with $500M+ in federal, state, and private funds. It includes ~$149.5M in BEAD funding (final federal approval to draw down, Jan 2026) to serve ~7,000 locations, ~82% via fiber. The University of Hawaii Broadband Office oversees implementation and contracts. A multi-island connectivity buildout creates demand for networking, edge compute, and resilient infrastructure.

Hawaiian Islands Fiber Link (HIFL)4

Under construction

A ~$120M open-access, carrier-neutral inter-island undersea fiber system linking Oahu, Hawaii Island, Maui, Kauai, Lanai, and Molokai. UH awarded the build contract in 2025; the 24-fiber-pair system has a 25-year design life and targets ready-for-service in late 2026. It addresses Hawaii's core constraint — island isolation and single-points-of-failure — and pairs naturally with redundant data infrastructure and disaster-resilient compute.

Federal / Defense Adjacency (INDOPACOM)8

Context

Hawaii hosts U.S. Indo-Pacific Command, Pearl Harbor Naval Shipyard, Joint Base Pearl Harbor-Hickam, and the DISA Pacific data center. The state carries roughly 8% of the military's construction budget — the highest share of any state — including a $1.2B Pearl Harbor dry dock. This is a separate federal buying motion from the state market, but a major regional tailwind for Dell Federal/ISG and a reason to coordinate state and federal account teams.

Procurement & Competition

The State Procurement Office (SPO) is the central authority on procurement statutes and rules for the State and its counties. Technology purchasing commonly runs through SPO price-and-vendor-list contracts and the Cooperative Purchasing Program, which leverages NASPO ValuePoint and other multi-state cooperatives. The Office of Enterprise Technology Services (ETS) sets IT governance, the statewide IT portfolio/roadmap, and the IT budget/spend-request cycle that agencies follow.

Competitive landscape

Hawaii is a relationship- and cooperative-driven market with a small in-state vendor base and high logistics/connectivity costs. Differentiation comes from resilient, redundant, island-aware infrastructure, AI-roadmap alignment with ETS, NASPO-ready offerings, and the ability to coordinate across state, UH, and the large adjacent federal/defense footprint rather than price alone.
