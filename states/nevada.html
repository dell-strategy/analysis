Nevada budgets biennially; the legislatively approved 2025–27 budget is roughly $56.7B. Three shifts define the technology landscape for SLG sellers. First, security: a major ransomware attack in August 2025 took 60+ state agencies offline for up to 28 days, and the state's response — refusing the ransom, recovering ~90% of data, and standing up a centralized Security Operations Center via special-session legislation (AB1) — has made cyber resilience the top funded priority. Second, organization: enterprise IT moved out of the Department of Administration into the Office of the CIO inside the Governor's Office (the Governor's Technology Office), centralizing strategy under State CIO Tim Galluzi. Third, infrastructure: the Tahoe Reno Industrial Center has become one of the world's largest AI data-center clusters (Google, Switch, Apple, Vantage, Novva, PowerHouse), a major tailwind for Dell ISG/AI demand. The buying motion runs largely through NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts and the State Purchasing Division.

Where Dell can play
  • Whole-of-state cybersecurity rebuild post-attack: secure compute, backup/recovery, SOC tooling, and managed services tied to AB1 and the new statewide SOC.
  • AI-infrastructure demand from the Tahoe Reno / TRIC data-center cluster and from in-house AI-enhanced app development at the Governor's Technology Office.
  • CORE.NV ERP modernization (financials live; HR/payroll deployed) — infrastructure, reporting, and lifecycle needs as it enters its optimization phase.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh and resilient backup across 60+ agencies hardening after the 2025 incident.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with cyber resilience: position backup/recovery, endpoint security, identity, and SOC-enablement infrastructure tied to AB1 and the agencies hardening after the 2025 attack.
  • Engage the Governor's Technology Office (OCIO) as the central account standing up the statewide SOC — secure compute, monitoring infrastructure, and managed services.
  • Tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Tahoe Reno / TRIC data-center cluster and to the OCIO's AI-enhanced in-house development push.
  • Confirm Dell and reseller positions on relevant NASPO ValuePoint master agreements and Nevada statewide/cloud contracts before pursuing deals.
  • Map services to CORE.NV's optimization phase and to NSHE (UNLV/UNR) research-computing buyers for AI-infrastructure and lifecycle opportunities.

Budget & Fiscal

Nevada budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle. The 83rd Legislature approved a 2025–27 budget of roughly $56.7B all funds — up about 6.1% over the prior biennium. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies and the internal-service rates of the Governor's Technology Office (OCIO), with discrete funding attached to named initiatives such as the statewide SOC and broadband deployment.

Initiatives & Policy

Statewide Security Operations Center (AB1)3

Standing up

After the August 2025 ransomware attack, a special session passed AB1, creating a centralized SOC under the Office of the CIO, a Cybersecurity Talent Pipeline Program, annual SOC reporting, and authority for school districts and underserved communities to use state cyber services. Operationalizing the SOC is the CIO's top 2026 priority — a greenfield need for secure compute, monitoring, backup/recovery, and managed services.

Tahoe Reno / TRIC AI Data-Center Cluster6

Accelerating

The Tahoe Reno Industrial Center — larger than the city of Detroit — has become one of the world's largest data-center markets, with Google (1,210 acres), Switch (potentially 7M+ sq ft), Apple, Vantage, Novva, EdgeCore, and PowerHouse building or expanding. The AI buildout is the strongest tailwind for Dell ISG and AI-infrastructure demand in the state.

In progress

AI-Enhanced In-House Development & AI Policy7

Emerging

The Governor's Technology Office is bringing application development back in-house, with the CIO noting one developer can now do the work of three or four using AI tooling. Nevada has issued a Policy on the Responsible and Ethical Use of AI signed by the CIO — a pragmatic stance that favors governed, auditable AI and opens a door for private/on-prem AI on sensitive workloads.

Procurement & Competition

Nevada's central technology authority sits with the Governor's Technology Office / Office of the CIO (formerly the EITS Division), which moved into the Governor's Office in 2023. Statewide purchasing runs through the State Purchasing Division, which leans heavily on NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts (NASPO's lead-state cooperative model) — including statewide Cloud Solutions contracts — usable by state agencies, counties, cities, K-12, and higher education via participating addenda.

Competitive landscape

Nevada is a cooperative-contract-driven market where NASPO ValuePoint positioning matters more than one-off RFPs. Post-attack, differentiation comes from resilient/secure offerings (backup, recovery, SOC enablement), AI-infrastructure capability tied to the TRIC cluster, and services that map to the SOC stand-up and CORE.NV rather than price alone.
