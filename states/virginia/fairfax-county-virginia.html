Overview

Fairfax County is the most populous jurisdiction in Virginia (~1.15M residents) and one of the wealthiest large counties in the country, anchoring the Northern Virginia / DC suburbs. It runs a council-manager structure: an elected Board of Supervisors sets policy and a professional County Executive (Bryan Hill) runs day-to-day operations across roughly 50 departments and 200+ facilities. Technology is centralized in the Department of Information Technology (DIT), led by the CIO/DIT Director, which delivers enterprise IT, networks, applications, and cybersecurity for county government (and supports Fairfax County Public Schools on an as-needed basis). DIT is a mature, award-winning shop — Fairfax placed near the top of the Center for Digital Government's Digital Counties Survey in 2025 — making this a sophisticated buyer that values modernization, security, and measurable outcomes over net-new build. For a Dell SLG seller, Fairfax is a large, creditworthy local-government account with its own competitive procurement and active cooperative-contract usage, distinct from (but adjacent to) the Commonwealth's VITA-run state motion.

Budget & Fiscal

The Board of Supervisors adopted the FY2027 budget on May 5, 2026 (effective July 1), with a General Fund of roughly $5.9B and — for the first time in years — a real estate tax-rate cut to $1.12 per $100 (down from $1.1225). Over $3B still transfers to Fairfax County Public Schools (nearly $120M more than FY2026), so the directly addressable county-operations and IT spend is a subset of the headline figure. The prior FY2026 all-funds plan exceeded $11B.

Initiatives & Priorities

DIT reorganization & IT architecture optimization

Under new CIO Matt Harrison, DIT is undergoing a significant reorganization to become a more modern, agile, secure organization — including a new Chief Director of Innovation and a countywide data/AI/BI innovation team reporting to the CIO. ITPAC backed a further ~$5M (toward ~$10M) to expand storage/compute, modernize applications, and continue infrastructure optimization across 50+ departments and 200+ facilities — a direct fit for server, storage, and refresh motions.

Multi-cloud & cloud governance

DIT is pursuing cloud expansion with stronger cloud governance as part of its adaptive technology strategy. Hybrid/multi-cloud and as-a-service offerings are in scope — position Dell infrastructure and managed services around governed cloud, not lift-and-shift.

Cybersecurity modernization & AI-driven threat detection

The Information Security Office is building an adaptive security strategy aligned to AI, IoT, and multi-cloud — upgrading security infrastructure, deploying AI-assisted threat detection, and automating operations. Fairfax's security program is nationally recognized and a priority spend area.

Cybersecurity AI
AI adoption & responsible-use governance

County leadership is moving on generative AI — countywide responsible-use training/security guidance plus a regional AI Economy Action Plan (estimated $0.5M–$1.5M with the NoVA Regional Commission). AI is both an internal operations lever (Hill has cited workforce/vacancy pressure) and an economic-development theme.

Procurement

Fairfax runs its own competitive procurement through the Department of Procurement and Material Management and is an active cooperative-contract user — both regionally and nationally. Plan for either a county solicitation or a ride on an existing cooperative vehicle; confirm Dell's coverage on the relevant ones before forecasting.
