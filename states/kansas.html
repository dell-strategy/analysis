Appropriated 2025

Kansas is in the middle of a structural reorganization of state technology that every SLG seller should track. SB 291 (2024) directs the Office of Information Technology Services (OITS) to consolidate IT — especially cybersecurity controls — under the Executive Chief Information Technology Officer, and makes Kansas the only state to mandate NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 compliance (by July 1, 2028). OITS ran a statewide IT assessment with Gartner through 2025 and is now shaping 2026 integration projects, stood up a 24/7 Security Operations Center, and operates a statewide generative-AI policy. Kansas budgets annually (FY2027 total state spending ~$26.8B), and the central buying motion runs through the Department of Administration's Office of Procurement and Contracts using cooperative vehicles like NASPO ValuePoint and Sourcewell. Outside government, the Kansas City metro is a fast-growing data-center and battery hub — Panasonic's $4B De Soto plant and Meta's $1B KC-area campus signal where infrastructure demand is concentrating. For Dell, consolidation creates a centralized buyer, a security-modernization runway, and AI-infrastructure tailwinds.

Where Dell can play
  • IT consolidation under the Executive CITO — server, storage, network, and DCaaS standardization as agencies migrate into shared OITS services.
  • Cybersecurity modernization toward the NIST CSF 2.0 mandate and the 24/7 SOC — secure compute, endpoint, and managed-security demand.
  • Governed AI adoption under the state generative-AI policy — private/on-prem AI for restricted-use data rather than open public models.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh as agencies consolidate onto common OITS-managed platforms.

Dell Action Plan

  • Engage OITS as the centralizing buyer: tie Dell compute, storage, network, and DCaaS to the SB 291 consolidation and shared-services roadmap as agencies migrate.
  • Lead with NIST CSF 2.0-aligned security and SOC-supporting infrastructure ahead of the July 2028 mandate — secure compute, endpoint, and managed-security offerings.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for restricted-use data as agencies operationalize the state generative-AI policy.
  • Confirm Dell coverage and resellers are active on NASPO ValuePoint and Sourcewell, and that IT buys are scoped for the CITO-involved Procurement Negotiating Committee.
  • Map Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the KC-metro data-center and battery buildout and to KU/K-State research-computing buyers.

Budget & Fiscal

Kansas budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle. HB 2513 sets FY2027 total state spending at roughly $26.8B and also carries appropriations into later years. There is no single statewide 'IT budget'; central technology spend flows through OITS (a ~$98.7M FY2026 all-funds request, revised upward toward ~$116.5M), while most agency technology dollars sit inside individual agency budgets. SB 291's consolidation is gradually shifting more of that spend — especially cybersecurity — into OITS.

Initiatives & Policy

SB 291 IT & Cybersecurity Consolidation4

In progress

SB 291 (2024) directs OITS to technically consolidate IT services — especially cybersecurity controls — under the Executive Chief Information Technology Officer. HB 2574 (signed spring 2026) removed the law's original July 1, 2026 sunset, making the consolidation framework permanent. OITS ran a statewide assessment with Gartner through 2025 and is scoping 2026 integration projects. Consolidation centralizes the buyer and creates standardization demand across compute, storage, network, and DCaaS as agencies migrate into shared services.

NIST CSF 2.0 Mandate & 24/7 SOC3

Made permanent (2026)

Kansas is the only state to require NIST Cybersecurity Framework 2.0 compliance, with each branch appointing a CISO and aligning programs by July 1, 2028. HB 2574 (signed spring 2026) removed the original July 1, 2026 sunset, so the consolidation and NIST mandate are now durable — it also ties executive-branch cyber compliance to the legislative budget process and creates a judicial branch technology oversight council. OITS stood up a $2.0M, 24/7 Security Operations Center for real-time threat detection. Recent incidents (Judicial Branch 2023, Wichita 2024) keep urgency high — an opening for secure infrastructure and managed-security offerings.

Statewide Generative AI Policy5

In effect

Governor Kelly directed Executive Branch agencies to adopt an OITS-authored generative-AI policy requiring human review of outputs and barring restricted-use information from public AI tools. Legislators are weighing an AI task force (HB 2592). The governed posture favors private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads over open public models.

Procurement & Competition

Central buying for Executive Branch agencies runs through the Kansas Department of Administration's Office of Procurement and Contracts. IT procurements require the Chief Information Technology Officer (or designee) on the Procurement Negotiating Committee, so the CITO has a direct hand in technology buys. Kansas leverages national cooperative vehicles rather than a single in-state IT catalog, and SB 291 consolidation is steadily centralizing more technology purchasing through OITS.

Competitive landscape

Kansas is a cooperative-driven market where incumbents hold NASPO ValuePoint and Sourcewell positions across compute, networking, and services. Differentiation comes from consolidation-ready standardization, NIST CSF 2.0-aligned security, and governed AI infrastructure rather than price alone — and from mapping offers to OITS's shared-services roadmap.
