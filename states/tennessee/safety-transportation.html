Tennessee — Safety, Corrections & Transportation

Overview

This account groups three of Tennessee's largest operational agencies, all headquartered in Nashville and all carrying heavy field, edge, and data footprints. The Department of Safety & Homeland Security runs driver services (license/ID issuance, REAL ID, self-service kiosks, mobile units) and the Tennessee Highway Patrol, plus the state's homeland-security and fusion functions. The Department of Correction (TDOC) operates the state prison system — roughly 20,000 in custody — and is building a Nashville intelligence center, deploying drones against contraband, and weighing body-worn cameras. The Department of Transportation (TDOT) runs the highway program and a sizable intelligent-transportation-systems (ITS) estate — TDOT SmartWay, the I-24 SMART Corridor with AI-based decision support, and a Memphis–Blue Oval City smart-freight pilot. Crucially, IT for these agencies is delivered largely by Strategic Technology Solutions (STS), the centralized IT organization under Finance & Administration, which sets standards, security, and cloud policy; agency-specific systems (biometrics, ITS, corrections intelligence) are procured on top of that shared platform. For a Dell SLG seller, the demand is concentrated in edge/mobile devices, ruggedized field compute, data-center and storage for records and video, AI/analytics for traffic and corrections intelligence, and the networking behind ITS — most of it routed through the state's Central Procurement Office Statewide Contracts and shaped upstream by STS.

Budget & Fiscal

These agencies are funded through Tennessee's annual (July 1–June 30) budget; the FY2027 budget Gov. Lee signed in May 2026 totals roughly $58B. There is no consolidated 'state IT' line — technology is distributed across agencies and the centralized STS organization, so the relevant signals are agency program budgets plus STS-driven modernization. TDOT received $400M in one-time FY2027 infrastructure funds on top of its ~$1.2B annual work program and the Transportation Modernization Act; TDOC requested roughly $1.5B with discrete technology lines.

Initiatives & Priorities

TDOT ITS — I-24 SMART Corridor & AI decision support

TDOT operates TDOT SmartWay and the I-24 SMART Corridor, a ~28-mile integrated corridor (Nashville–Murfreesboro) with VSL gantries, cameras, and an AI-based decision-support system developed with Southwest Research Institute and Vanderbilt. Phase 2 completed in early 2025; Phase 3 adds ramp metering, fiber, CCTV, and dynamic message signs — sustained demand for edge compute, networking, storage, and AI/analytics.

ITS AI/Analytics Edge
TDOT smart-freight & connected-autonomous pilots

TDOT named a private partner (Cavnue) for a Memphis–Blue Oval City Smart Freight Corridor pilot on I-40 and is piloting connected/autonomous freight technology in real-world conditions — a multi-year program with roadside infrastructure, data, and analytics needs adjacent to the West Tennessee EV manufacturing buildout.

TDOC corrections intelligence, drones & body cameras

The Department of Correction is standing up a Nashville central intelligence center (~30 staff) using AI to monitor inmate activity, plans ~$1.7M in drones to interdict contraband, and faces legislative pressure to pilot body-worn cameras at a private facility — opportunities across analytics compute, storage/retention for video, and edge devices, with public-records and retention constraints to design around.

Driver services modernization (Safety)

Safety & Homeland Security runs license/ID issuance with self-service kiosks and mobile units, REAL ID, and a new noncitizen credential rollout beginning Jan 2026. Tennessee has not broadly launched a mobile/digital driver license — a likely future modernization track around secure issuance and identity infrastructure.

Procurement

These agencies buy through Tennessee's centralized machinery, not standalone agency RFPs in most cases. The primary path is the Central Procurement Office's Statewide Contracts (with a dedicated technology category) and the Edison supplier portal; larger or specialized systems (ITS integration, biometrics, corrections platforms) can run as agency solicitations. STS sets the IT standards, cloud, and security policy that buys must conform to.
