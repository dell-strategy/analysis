Overview

The Tennessee Department of General Services (DGS) is the state's central administrative and procurement agency — it manages state real estate, vehicles and assets, document solutions, and, most relevant to a technology seller, the Central Procurement Office (CPO). The CPO, created under TCA § 4-56-104, is the statewide procurement authority: it competes and awards Statewide Contracts (SWCs) that state agencies — and, for many categories, local governments and nonprofits — can buy from without running their own solicitation. DGS is best understood as the contractual path rather than the IT decision-maker: it owns the vehicles and the Edison supplier portal that gate most buys, while statewide IT strategy, standards, and security policy sit with Strategic Technology Solutions (STS), the centralized IT organization within the Department of Finance & Administration. For a Dell SLG seller, DGS/CPO is the access layer to the entire Tennessee public sector, and the demand signal is largely set upstream by STS modernization and the state's AI agenda. New leadership took over the department in January 2026.

Budget & Fiscal

DGS is not a large direct IT spender — its leverage is the volume of public-sector purchasing that routes through its statewide contracts, not its own appropriation. Tennessee runs an annual (July 1–June 30) budget; the FY2027 budget Gov. Lee signed in May 2026 totals roughly $58B (about $29.8B from state dollars) and notably includes a dedicated $50M for state AI infrastructure. There is no published consolidated 'state IT' line item — technology spend is distributed across agencies and the centralized STS organization.

Initiatives & Priorities

Statewide Contracts (SWC) for technology

The CPO maintains a Statewide Contract Listing with a dedicated technology category; SWCs are the pre-competed agreements agencies and many local/nonprofit buyers use without their own RFP. Confirming Dell's SWC coverage and reseller scope is the single most important access step for the Tennessee motion.

Procurement Statewide Contracts
Edison supplier portal & e-procurement

Edison is the state's mandatory supplier-registration and transactional backbone. Suppliers must be registered and correctly represented in Edison to do business with the state — a gate to clear and keep current for Dell and its partners.

Procurement Supplier Onboarding
STS-driven modernization (ERP + automation)

Demand routed through CPO is shaped by STS, which describes 2026 as a shift into 'execution.' A flagship effort is an ERP modernization whose RFI calls for 'native AI architecture,' alongside broad modernization and automation — multi-year infrastructure and services runway that ultimately procures through CPO vehicles.

Modernization ERP AI
Statewide AI Action Plan alignment

Tennessee's first statewide AI Action Plan (adopted Nov 2025) includes a 'Data & Compute Readiness' pillar — secure statewide infrastructure for safe AI experimentation. Purchases enabling that pillar will be contracted through CPO, an opening for governed, auditable on-prem and private-cloud AI.

Procurement

Selling to Tennessee state government runs through CPO-owned vehicles, not one-off agency RFPs in most cases. Two things to confirm first: that Dell's Statewide Contract coverage is active and correctly scoped for the categories you're targeting, and that Dell and its partners are properly registered in Edison. STS sets the IT standards and security policy that buys must conform to.
