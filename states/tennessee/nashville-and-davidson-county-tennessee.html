Overview

The Metropolitan Government of Nashville & Davidson County is a consolidated city-county — one government covering the City of Nashville and Davidson County — serving roughly 715,000 residents, making it Tennessee's largest local-government account. Mayor Freddie O'Connell's administration filed a proposed Fiscal Year 2027 operating budget of about $3.8 billion (filed May 1, 2026), built around grocery-tax relief, the city's largest-ever housing investment, schools, and public safety. Technology is centralized: the Metro Department of Information Technology Services (ITS) is the shared-services IT organization for the enterprise, supporting roughly 55 departments and agencies with a staff of around 170 and an operating budget reported near $64.5 million. ITS owns infrastructure, networking, cybersecurity, data, and resident-facing digital services (including Metro Public Wi-Fi and digital-inclusion programs), and it stood up a public Metro Government AI Use Registry as part of a governance-first approach to generative AI. For a Dell SLG seller, the practical map is: ITS is the central technology buying center, the Metro Finance Procurement Division runs the contracts, and Metro Nashville Public Schools (MNPS) is a large adjacent buyer with its own procurement.

Budget & Fiscal

Metro's leverage is its scale — one consolidated budget funds city and county services together. Mayor O'Connell's proposed FY2027 budget is about $3.8 billion; the centralized ITS department carries its own operating line within that, and large agencies (Schools, Police, Public Works) drive the bulk of enterprise IT demand.

Initiatives & Priorities

Metro Government AI Use Registry & AI governance

Metro ITS publishes a public AI Use Registry cataloging where the government uses AI, and frames AI around drafting, editing, data analysis, and workflow automation. A governance-led posture — lead with responsible-AI infrastructure, not just tooling.

Cybersecurity & data protection

ITS lists securing and safeguarding data and services as a core priority, with a dedicated CISO (John Griffey). Local governments remain high-value ransomware targets — endpoint, identity, resilience, and recovery are durable conversations.

Digital inclusion & Metro Public Wi-Fi

ITS runs Metro Public Wi-Fi plus Digital SkillsLink programs (TechTies, Digital Navigators) to close the access gap — a resident-facing modernization track tied to connectivity and device access.

Smart-city & regional AI/cloud talent (Nashville Innovation Alliance)

Metro continues smart-city work with Vanderbilt's institute and benefits from the AWS-backed Nashville Innovation Alliance, expanding regional AI/cloud/cyber talent (targeting 1,000+ Tennesseans by 2027). Context for workforce-aligned infrastructure plays.
