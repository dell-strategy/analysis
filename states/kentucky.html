Kentucky budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle, and the FY2026–28 executive budget (HB 500) — roughly $32B in General Fund spending — became law in April 2026 after the GOP supermajority overrode most of Governor Beshear's vetoes. It is a disciplined plan that trims executive-branch spending and grows reserves, so technology dollars are spread across agencies rather than a single IT line. Three structural shifts matter for sellers. First, Senate Bill 4 (signed March 2025) is among the most comprehensive state AI-governance laws in the country: it puts the Commonwealth Office of Technology (COT) in charge of generative-AI policy, creates an AI Governance Committee, and requires every agency to inventory AI use cases — driving demand for governed, auditable AI. Second, an AI-driven data-center boom is landing, anchored by a 400MW campus in Louisville (PowerHouse/Poe), backed by long-running sales-tax incentives. Third, $1.1B in federal BEAD broadband funding is flowing through the Office of Broadband Development. For Dell, the buying motion runs through COT-governed Master Agreements and NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts.

Where Dell can play
  • Governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive agency workloads as SB 4 forces every department to inventory and assess AI use cases.
  • AI-infrastructure demand tied to the Louisville 400MW data-center buildout and the broader Kentucky hyperscale push (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • Cloud-migration and Executive Branch IT consolidation work under COT's modernization agenda (compute, storage, lifecycle).
  • Whole-of-state cybersecurity for state agencies, counties, and schools coordinated through COT's Office of the CISO.

Initiatives & Policy

SB 4 — AI Governance Framework4

In effect

Signed by Gov. Beshear in March 2025, SB 4 is among the most comprehensive state AI-governance laws in the U.S. It tasks COT with creating policies for transparent, ethical generative-AI use, stands up an AI Governance Committee, requires disclosure and human oversight for consequential decisions, and directs every department and agency to report potential (including high-risk) AI use cases. Agencies now need governed, auditable AI — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive data.

Under construction

Procurement & Competition

The Commonwealth Office of Technology (COT), within the Finance & Administration Cabinet, is the central IT authority. Under KRS 42.726, COT oversees and approves Executive Branch IT procurements and contracts for compliance with state standards. Statewide Master Agreements are maintained by the Office of Procurement Services, and agencies are generally required to buy from them. Kentucky is also an active NASPO ValuePoint participant, giving eligible entities access to pre-competed national cooperative contracts.

Competitive landscape

Kentucky is a centralized, contract-driven market — COT governs and approves Executive Branch IT buys, and incumbents hold Master Agreement and NASPO positions across compute, networking, and services. Differentiation comes from governed/private-AI capability aligned to SB 4, AI-infrastructure tied to the data-center boom, and consolidation/efficiency services that fit a tight budget — not price alone.
