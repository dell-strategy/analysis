New York enters its FY2027 fiscal year (April 1, 2026 – March 31, 2027) with a record $268.5B enacted budget and a technology agenda anchored by AI infrastructure. The marquee asset for any infrastructure seller is Empire AI — a more than $500M public/private consortium standing up one of the nation's most powerful academic AI supercomputers at SUNY's University at Buffalo. Its 'Beta' phase, funded with $40M in 2025, runs NVIDIA Blackwell (DGX GB200 / SuperPOD) and the permanent center is slated for completion in 2026. Centralized buying runs through the NYS Office of General Services (OGS) and the Office of Information Technology Services (ITS), the consolidated state IT agency under CIO Dru Rai. On security, New York's Joint Security Operations Center (JSOC) and a $30M+ county shared-services program (free EDR to 50+ counties and five cities) define the cyber motion. The RAISE Act — frontier-AI transparency rules effective Jan 1, 2027 — is the headline AI policy. For Dell, the strongest pull is AI/HPC infrastructure tied to SUNY/CUNY and Empire AI, plus endpoint and lifecycle refresh across a record state budget; Dell already holds an OGS IT Umbrella centralized contract.

Budget & Fiscal

New York budgets annually; its fiscal year runs April 1 to March 31. The FY2027 budget (April 1, 2026 – March 31, 2027) was enacted at $268.5B — a record high, signed in late May 2026 after a 57-day delay, the latest budget in 16 years. There is no single consolidated 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, the consolidated ITS agency, and named initiatives such as Empire AI, ConnectALL broadband, and county cyber shared services.

RAISE Act — Frontier AI Governance5

Effective Jan 1, 2027

The Responsible AI Safety and Education Act (signed Dec 2025) imposes transparency, safety-protocol publication, and 72-hour incident reporting on large frontier-model developers, enforced by the AG with penalties up to $1M/$3M and an oversight office within DFS. It targets developers rather than buyers, but it reinforces the state's appetite for governed, auditable AI — favorable framing for private/on-prem AI in government.

Procurement & Competition

New York centralizes most technology buying through the Office of General Services (OGS), which maintains statewide ('centralized') contracts that state agencies, local governments, and school districts can purchase against without running their own solicitations. The Office of Information Technology Services (ITS) is the consolidated executive-branch IT agency setting statewide direction. Large purchases above the STF §112 threshold require Office of the State Comptroller (OSC) approval.
