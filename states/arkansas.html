Arkansas is in the middle of the biggest restructuring of its technology operations in decades, and every move is led from the Governor's office. Executive Order EO 25-08 (June 2025) renamed the Division of Information Systems to the Department of Shared Administrative Services, Office of State Technology (effective August 6, 2025), centralized IT governance, and stood up a new statewide cybersecurity office — a direct response to a state review finding Arkansas spends only ~$6M a year on cybersecurity versus a ~$60M state average. In parallel, the Governor's AI Center of Excellence delivered its effectiveness-and-safety report in February 2026, recommending a Chief AI Officer, NIST-based evaluation, and an AI-enabled government services portal. Underpinning all of it is a historic private-sector buildout: Google's $4B West Memphis AI campus and AVAIO Digital's $6B (up to $21B) Little Rock data-center hub, plus the Walmart Global Tech gravity well in Bentonville. For Dell, the buying motion runs through the Office of State Procurement (OSP) and approved state/cooperative contracts; the centralization and cyber buildout create a consolidated, greenfield buyer at exactly the moment AI-infrastructure demand is landing in-state.

Where Dell can play
  • Greenfield cybersecurity buildout — Arkansas is closing a ~$6M-to-~$60M spending gap and standing up a new centralized cyber office (secure compute, infrastructure, managed services).
  • IT centralization and consolidation under the Office of State Technology — server, storage, and data-center modernization as duplicative agency systems are consolidated.
  • AI infrastructure tied to the Google West Memphis and AVAIO Little Rock data-center boom, plus the Walmart/Bentonville and University of Arkansas research-computing ecosystem (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • Governed agency AI as the AI Center of Excellence recommendations move to implementation — private/on-prem AI for sensitive Medicaid/SNAP and citizen-service workloads.

Dell Action Plan

  • Engage the new centralized cybersecurity office and Office of State Technology early — Arkansas is closing a ~$6M-to-~$60M cyber gap, a greenfield opening for secure compute, infrastructure, and managed services.
  • Tie Dell offerings to the IT consolidation mandate — position server/storage/data-center modernization as agencies de-duplicate systems toward the $65–130M savings target.
  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: connect Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Google West Memphis and AVAIO Little Rock buildout and to University of Arkansas research-computing buyers.
  • Confirm Arkansas Vendor Services registration and current OSP state-contract coverage — and verify any cooperative contract is OSP-approved before relying on it.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive Medicaid/SNAP and citizen-service workloads as the AI Center of Excellence recommendations move to implementation.

Budget & Fiscal

Arkansas budgets on an annual cycle via the Revenue Stabilization Act (RSA), with a regular legislative session in odd years and a fiscal session in even years. The 2025 session set FY2026 general revenue at $6.49B (a ~3% / ~$182.5M increase); the Governor presented the FY2027 proposal on March 3, 2026 ahead of the April 2026 fiscal session. There is no single 'state IT budget' line — Arkansas spends roughly $680–700M on technology across agencies, and the current reorganization is meant to consolidate that spend under the Office of State Technology.

Procurement & Competition

The Office of State Procurement (OSP), within the Department of Shared Administrative Services (DSAS), has primary responsibility for statewide procurement of commodities, technical and professional services for state agencies, boards, commissions, and colleges and universities. Most technology buys run through OSP state contracts; cooperative contracts are permitted but limited and require pre-approval by OSP. The Office of State Technology (formerly DIS) delivers shared technology products and services to ~300 agencies, K-12, higher-ed, and local government.
