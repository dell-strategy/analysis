Overview

The Alabama Department of Finance is the state's central fiscal and administrative agency — it builds the executive budget, manages the treasury and accounting (STAARS) systems, and houses the operational divisions that run government, including the Office of Information Technology (OIT) and the Division of Purchasing. OIT, established in 2013 and led by the Secretary of Information Technology / State CIO, is Alabama's enterprise IT authority: it sets statewide IT policy, owns the IT strategic plan, and delivers shared services (network, data center, cloud, cybersecurity) to executive-branch agencies. For a Dell SLG seller, Finance/OIT is the most important single relationship in the state — it is both the enterprise standards-setter and, through the Purchasing Division, the gateway to statewide contracts that agencies, and in many cases local and education buyers, purchase against. Recent context matters: a May 2025 statewide cybersecurity event sharpened OIT's focus on identity, resilience, and whole-of-state security, and in Feb 2026 the state stood up a Technology Quality Assurance Board (TQAB) under OIT to govern AI and cybersecurity across executive-branch agencies.

Budget & Fiscal

Alabama runs two main budgets — the General Fund (non-education) and the Education Trust Fund (ETF). Finance/OIT itself is not a large direct end-spender; OIT recovers much of its cost from the agencies it serves rather than a single large general-revenue line. Initiatives & Priorities

Whole-of-state cybersecurity

Following the May 2025 statewide cyber event, OIT and the State CISO are driving a whole-of-state security model — NIST-aligned frameworks, identity hardening, mandatory resets, and shared tooling extended to agencies and local government. A clear opening for security, identity, and resilient-infrastructure plays.

Enterprise IT consolidation & shared services

OIT's mandate is to streamline IT statewide — consolidating network, data center, and shared platforms across executive-branch agencies to reduce duplicated cost. Position infrastructure refresh and managed/as-a-service offerings against the consolidation roadmap.

Cloud & data-center modernization

OIT delivers cloud and hosting as shared services; modernization tracks include hybrid cloud, infrastructure refresh, and resilient hosting for agency workloads. Tie ISG and AI-ready infrastructure to the enterprise hosting environment.

AI & data governance (formalizing)

Alabama's enterprise-AI governance is moving from task-force study to standing oversight: the Technology Quality Assurance Board (TQAB), formed Feb 2026 under Act 2025-369 and OIT's authority, is charged with reviewing emerging tech, setting statewide standards (NIST AI RMF), issuing agency GenAI policies, and maintaining a pre-approved GenAI tool list. Treat AI as a relationship-building motion aligned to TQAB standards — confirm any tool is on the approved path before forecasting AI-infrastructure deals.
