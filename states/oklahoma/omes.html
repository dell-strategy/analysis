Oklahoma Office of Management & Enterprise Services (OMES)

Overview

The Office of Management and Enterprise Services (OMES) is Oklahoma's central administrative agency, combining three functions that matter to a technology seller in one place: consolidated statewide IT (via OMES Information Services), Central Purchasing (the state's procurement authority and statewide-contract vehicle), and budget/finance for the executive branch. Under the Information Technology Consolidation and Coordination Act (62 O.S. §§35.1–35.9), OMES delivers mandatory shared IT services — email, networking, applications, cybersecurity — across executive-branch agencies and charges costs back on a cost-recovery rate model rather than running purely on a large general-revenue line. OMES Information Services supports 125+ agencies, boards and commissions (and the state CISO's security mission spans 180+ entities), which makes OMES both a large IT operator and the procurement path most Oklahoma public buyers travel. For a Dell SLG seller, OMES is best understood as the consolidated buying center and contract gateway for state-government technology in Oklahoma.

Budget & Fiscal

OMES is funded by a mix of direct appropriations and cost-recovery chargebacks: the IT Consolidation Act requires OMES to provide mandatory shared services and bill agencies back to recoup costs, so much of its IT spend flows through a service rate model rather than a single appropriation. Specific appropriation figures are set each session by the Legislature; treat the numbers below as directional and confirm against the current enacted budget.

Initiatives & Priorities

IT Strategic Plan (FY2026–2028)

CIO Dan Cronin's plan (announced Aug 29, 2025) is anchored on four goals: customer centricity, reducing complexity, workforce development, and strategic modernization — prioritizing secure, cloud-native infrastructure and shared platforms that cut duplication. The roadmap for where statewide IT investment is heading.

Statewide AI strategy under first Chief AI Officer

Oklahoma named Tai Phan its first Chief AI & Technology Officer (announced Nov 21, 2025), fulfilling a Governor's Task Force on Emerging Technologies recommendation. He sets AI strategy, guardrails and ethics, and helps agencies find where AI streamlines operations — a buying center for responsible-AI and the infrastructure under it.

AI Governance
Cloud-native modernization & legacy replacement

The strategic plan champions cloud-native infrastructure, shared identity management, and replacing legacy systems with real-time monitoring. Oklahoma is a public Google Cloud reference customer — frame infrastructure plays for a hybrid/cloud-forward environment.

Cloud Data Center
Cybersecurity via Oklahoma Cyber Command

OMES Information Services runs Oklahoma Cyber Command, a 24/7/365 Security Operations Center protecting state users, devices, networks and data. New CISO Daniel Langley (started Oct 2025) oversees Cyber Command and security policy across 180+ entities — a focus on agency collaboration and closing vulnerabilities.
