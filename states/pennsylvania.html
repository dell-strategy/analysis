Pennsylvania is one of the most dynamic SLG technology markets in the country right now, and three forces define it. First, the Shapiro Administration ran the nation's first state-government generative-AI pilot (ChatGPT Enterprise with OpenAI and Carnegie Mellon) and in April 2026 expanded AI to 3,000+ employees across 35 agencies under a new Generative AI Governing Board — agencies are actively operationalizing AI and need governed infrastructure. Second, a roughly $90B wave of AI data-center investment is landing in the Commonwealth, anchored by Amazon's $20B commitment and the $10B Homer City gas-powered campus, making PA national-scale AI infrastructure. Third, the central technology buy runs through the Department of General Services' COSTARS cooperative program and statewide contracts, with the Office of Administration (OA-OIT) setting enterprise IT and security policy. The 2025–26 budget landed late ($50.1B, signed Nov 12, 2025), and 2026–27 negotiations are live now — timing matters for forecasting.

Budget & Fiscal

Pennsylvania budgets annually on a July 1 fiscal year. The 2025–26 General Fund budget of $50.1B was signed November 12, 2025 — 135 days late — and 2026–27 negotiations are underway now. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, the Office of Administration's enterprise IT services, and named digital and broadband initiatives.

SB 377 — NIST Standards in IT Procurement9

Advancing

SB 377 (Phillips-Hill) passed the Senate 49-0 in June 2025, requiring procured endpoint devices and related services to meet NIST cybersecurity guidelines or industry best practices. It pushes a NIST baseline into Commonwealth IT contracting — favorable to vendors who can document NIST-aligned endpoint security and lifecycle controls.

Procurement & Competition

The Department of General Services (DGS) runs central procurement, including the COSTARS cooperative purchasing program ('Cooperative Sourcing to Achieve Reductions in Spend'), which lets registered local governments, school districts, and state-affiliated entities buy from DGS-established contracts — including IT hardware and software — without running their own RFP. The Office of Administration (OA-OIT) sets enterprise IT strategy, security policy, and shared services for the ~80,000 executive-branch employees under the Governor's jurisdiction.

Competitive landscape

Pennsylvania is a contract-driven market where COSTARS and DGS statewide contracts shape access and incumbents hold positions across compute, client, and networking. Differentiation tends to come from AI-infrastructure capability tied to the data-center wave, NIST-aligned security (SB 377), and governed AI for agencies rather than price alone.
