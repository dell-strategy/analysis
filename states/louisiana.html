Louisiana is being reshaped by the single largest private investment in state history: Meta's $10B, 4-million-sq-ft 'Hyperion' AI-optimized data center in Richland Parish (northeast LA), under construction through 2030 and backed by ~$3.3B in equipment tax exemptions. For Dell, the data-center buildout and its supply chain, power, and connectivity ripple effects are the headline angle. Inside state government, technology buying is highly centralized: the Office of Technology Services (OTS) is the executive branch's consolidated IT organization and the sole centralized customer for IT vendor contracts, with new CIO Evelina Broussard (appointed Feb 2025) leading. Governor Landry has set an assertive AI and cyber posture — an October 2025 executive order banning foreign-adversary AI (e.g., DeepSeek), requiring approval before agencies adopt AI, and directing state tech leaders to set AI safety rules by Dec 15, 2025, alongside a state Cyber Threat Analytics Center. Louisiana is also first in the nation to win federal approval to deploy $1.355B in BEAD broadband funding (GUMBO 2.0).

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure tailwind from Meta's Hyperion buildout and Louisiana's positioning as an AI data-center state (PowerEdge, networking, services, ecosystem/supply-chain pull).
  • Centralized modernization through OTS — a single consolidated buyer for executive-branch compute, client, and lifecycle refresh.
  • Governed AI adoption under Landry's restrict-and-approve posture — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive state data rather than open public models.
  • Cybersecurity buildout around the state Cyber Threat Analytics Center / Security Operations Center.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with AI infrastructure: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Meta Hyperion buildout, its supply-chain and power ecosystem, and LSU research computing.
  • Make OTS the anchor relationship — it is the sole centralized IT customer for the executive branch; confirm Dell agreements and resellers are current and correctly scoped there.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads given Louisiana's restrict-and-approve AI posture and foreign-AI ban.
  • Engage the state Cyber Threat Analytics Center / SOC buildout with secure compute, detection infrastructure, and managed security services.
  • Track cooperative and OSP/LaPAC vehicles to extend reach to parishes and political subdivisions riding state contracts.

Budget & Fiscal

Louisiana budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1 - June 30) through the Executive Budget. There is no single statewide 'IT budget' line, but the Office of Technology Services (OTS) is the consolidated executive-branch IT organization and its appropriation is the clearest proxy: the FY2025-26 recommended total means of financing for OTS is ~$671M, funded almost entirely by interagency transfers (chargebacks to the agencies it serves), down ~4% from the prior year. Additional technology spend is distributed across agencies and large externally funded programs (broadband, economic development).

State AI Governance & Restrictions6

In force

Governor Landry's October 2025 executive order bans free foreign-adversary AI tools (e.g., DeepSeek) from schools, universities, and state agencies, requires approval before agencies adopt new AI, and directed state technology leaders to establish AI safety rules by Dec 15, 2025. Procurement & Competition

Louisiana technology procurement is highly centralized. The Office of Technology Services (OTS) is the executive branch's consolidated IT organization and the sole centralized customer for IT vendor contracts — it reviews, coordinates, approves, and manages procurement of hardware, software, networking, and IT consulting on behalf of state agencies. The Office of State Procurement (OSP), within the Division of Administration, runs broader state contracting and the LaPAC contract network, and cooperative purchasing statutes let parishes, municipalities, and other subdivisions ride existing public contracts.

Competitive landscape

Because OTS is the single centralized IT customer, winning in Louisiana means winning the OTS relationship and the right cooperative/statewide vehicles rather than chasing fragmented agency deals. Differentiation comes from AI-infrastructure capability tied to the data-center economy, governed private/on-prem AI for a restrict-and-approve state, and security offerings that map to the Cyber Threat Analytics Center.
