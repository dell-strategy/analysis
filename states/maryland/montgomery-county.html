Overview

Montgomery County is Maryland's most populous and most affluent jurisdiction — roughly 1.05 million residents in the Washington, DC suburbs — governed by an elected County Executive and an 11-member County Council, with day-to-day operations run by a Chief Administrative Officer. Enterprise technology is centralized in the Department of Technology & Enterprise Business Solutions (TEBS), which owns IT governance, enterprise systems, cybersecurity, and the County's broadband/fiber assets; the Office of Enterprise Information Security (OEIS) sits inside TEBS. Like most large local governments, the County is a partly federated buyer: TEBS sets enterprise strategy and runs shared infrastructure, while large departments (Police, Fire & Rescue, Health & Human Services, and the separately governed Montgomery County Public Schools) carry their own substantial technology needs. For a Dell SLG seller, the County is a high-value, well-funded DC-metro local-government opportunity with an unusually mature technology posture — but the entry point is TEBS plus the County's Office of Procurement and Council-approval process, not a single capital line. Note an active leadership transition: County Executive Marc Elrich is term-limited and a new executive will take office in late 2026 following the 2026 election cycle.

Budget & Fiscal

The County Council approved a $7.6B FY2026 operating budget on May 22, 2025, with public education the dominant line — roughly $3.6B for Montgomery County Public Schools, about half the total. Central IT is a small share of that: TEBS's own FY2026 recommended operating budget was about $72.7M (~227 FTEs), a slight decrease from FY2025. As with most large counties, technology spend is spread across departments and capital (CIP) projects rather than concentrated in one IT appropriation.

AI Action Plan & responsible-AI adoption

The County published an AI Action Plan and an AI Progress Report (Sept 2025) framing responsible, equitable AI adoption, and partnered with InnovateUS to upskill employees on safe generative-AI use. An active, executive-visible priority and an opening for AI-infrastructure and data-platform conversations.

FiberNet / MoCoNet broadband & digital equity

TEBS operates FiberNet (the County's fiber backbone for government and schools) and MoCoNet (free residential broadband at affordable-housing sites), and runs ultraMontgomery/digital-equity programs. FiberNet is a recurring CIP project — an infrastructure and network-modernization track.

Cybersecurity resilience (OEIS)

The Office of Enterprise Information Security runs the County's security program; recent modernization work materially reduced attack surface and cut recovery time after incidents. Security is a standing investment area and a clear buying center.

Procurement

Selling to Montgomery County runs through its centralized Office of Procurement under the County's Procurement Code (Chapter 11B of the County Code). Solicitations post through the Maryland eMaryland Marketplace / BidNet Maryland Purchasing Group. The County can also buy via intergovernmental cooperative purchasing to extend other jurisdictions' pricing, and larger or noncompetitive actions draw County Council attention. Confirm which department owns the requirement, then map the Office of Procurement path and any usable co-op vehicle.
