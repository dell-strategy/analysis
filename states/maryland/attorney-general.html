Overview

The Office of the Attorney General (OAG), led by Attorney General Anthony G. Brown, is Maryland's chief legal office. It litigates on behalf of the state, provides legal advice and counsel to the Governor and the General Assembly, and serves as in-house counsel to 40-plus state agencies (education, health, transportation, labor, and more). Its work is organized into divisions including Civil Litigation, Consumer Protection, the Criminal Division, Civil Rights, Antitrust, Securities, the Independent Investigations Division, and the Office of the Solicitor General. For a technology seller, the OAG is best understood as a litigation- and data-intensive operation rather than a large infrastructure spender: the office runs case-management and document-management systems, performs eDiscovery across high-volume litigation, and — through the Consumer Protection Division — administers Maryland's data-breach notification and data-privacy regime (including the Maryland Online Data Privacy Act, with new requirements phasing in through 2026). That mix drives recurring demand for secure storage, data processing/eDiscovery capacity, endpoint refresh for attorneys and investigators, and cyber controls protecting sensitive case data. The OAG is a relatively small agency by headcount, with roughly 404 authorized positions in FY2026 and a budget that is overwhelmingly personnel.

Initiatives & Priorities

Case management & eDiscovery modernization

The OAG procures and operates case-management, document-management, and eDiscovery systems, and has posted manager-level roles overseeing development, vendor RFPs, and a DevOps team. This is the core data-and-storage workload — high-volume litigation drives demand for secure processing, review, and retention capacity.

Data privacy & breach enforcement (MODPA)

Through Consumer Protection, the OAG administers Maryland's breach-notification regime and enforces the Maryland Online Data Privacy Act, with new processing requirements phasing in during 2026. More enforcement means more evidence/data to ingest, store, and analyze securely.

Cybersecurity for sensitive case data

The office holds highly sensitive investigative, criminal, and consumer data, making endpoint security, secure storage, access controls, and resilient backup recurring needs — reinforced by Maryland's statewide push on cyber maturity.

Procurement

The OAG is not a standalone procurement authority for IT — it buys through Maryland's statewide vehicles. Selling here means being positioned on the right Department of Information Technology (DoIT) statewide contracts and the Department of General Services (DGS) Office of State Procurement vehicles, with solicitations posted on eMaryland Marketplace Advantage (eMMA).
