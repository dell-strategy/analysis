Overview

The Maryland Department of Information Technology (DoIT) is the state's central IT agency — it sets statewide technology, cybersecurity, and data policy, operates shared infrastructure and networks, and administers the Information Technology Investment Fund (ITIF) that finances the state's largest modernization projects. Under Secretary and State CIO Katie Savage (appointed 2023), DoIT has pursued a deliberately federated model: rather than centralizing all IT, it has helped individual agencies stand up their own CIOs, CISOs, and product managers while DoIT provides governance, funding, and shared capability. New in-house teams reflect that build-out — the Maryland Digital Service (state product managers, designers, researchers, and engineers) and an Office of AI Enablement led by a Senior Advisor for Responsible AI. DoIT also anchors Governor Moore's headline tech bets, including the $1B "Capital of Quantum" initiative centered at the University of Maryland and a responsible-AI agenda flowing from the Governor's 2024 AI executive order. For a Dell seller, DoIT is both a direct buyer of infrastructure and the governance/funding hub that shapes modernization demand across the executive branch.

Budget & Fiscal

DoIT's leverage shows up through the ITIF — the fund that finances Major Information Technology Development Projects (MITDPs) across the executive branch. DoIT's FY2026 request raised its operating allowance by roughly $65.9M (about 20%), much of it flowing into the ITIF to push large modernization efforts from planning into procurement and build.
