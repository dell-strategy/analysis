Overview

The California Health & Human Services Agency (CalHHS / CHHS) is the cabinet-level umbrella over roughly a dozen departments and offices that administer the state's health and human services — including the Department of Health Care Services (DHCS, which runs Medi-Cal), the Department of Public Health (CDPH), the Department of Social Services (CDSS), the Department of State Hospitals (DSH), and the Center for Data Insights and Innovation (CDII). It is one of the largest organizations in U.S. state government by spend, and it carries a correspondingly large data, IT, eligibility-systems, and cybersecurity footprint. For a seller, CHHS is best understood as a federated buyer: the Agency sets policy and cross-department data strategy, but the heavy IT and modernization budgets sit inside the individual departments (DHCS above all), and most technology buys route through department-level solicitations, statewide contracts administered by the California Department of Technology (CDT) and the Department of General Services (DGS), and CDT's project-approval and oversight process for reportable IT projects. The signature cross-department program is the statewide Data Exchange Framework (DxF) — a first-of-its-kind mandate to securely exchange health and social-services data — which is hitting major compliance milestones in 2026; note that administration of DxF transitioned from CalHHS/CDII to the Department of Health Care Access and Information (HCAI) in August 2025, formalized by SB 660 (signed October 2025).

Budget & Fiscal

CHHS is one of the biggest spenders in state government, but the money — and the IT money — lives in the departments. DHCS/Medi-Cal alone dominates: the Governor's proposed 2026-27 budget put DHCS at roughly $229.1B total funds ($49.5B General Fund), and Medi-Cal covers nearly 15 million Californians (more than one in three residents). Governor Newsom signed a balanced 2026-27 budget on June 29, 2026 (touted as $0 deficit with ~$30B in reserves); Medi-Cal still absorbs cuts tied to federal policy changes and prior-year restrictions — a backdrop that favors efficiency, automation, and modernization pitches.

Initiatives & Priorities

Data Exchange Framework (DxF)

California's first-ever statewide mandate for secure, real-time exchange of health and social-services data. Mandatory signatories were to exchange information by January 31, 2026, and from July 1, 2026 executing the Data Sharing Agreement is a condition of certain contracts with DHCS, CalPERS, and Covered California. Program administration transitioned from CalHHS/CDII to HCAI in August 2025; SB 660 (signed October 2025) expanded the program and stood up a new Stakeholder Advisory Committee that began meeting April 2026. Drives demand for data platforms, integration, interoperability, and security.

Benefits & eligibility-systems modernization

Medi-Cal and human-services eligibility, enrollment, and case-management systems serving ~15M people are a long-running modernization track across DHCS and CDSS — an opening for infrastructure refresh, cloud, and data-platform plays. Confirm which department and which system before forecasting.

Center for Data Insights and Innovation (CDII)

CDII (established 2021) drives data access, sharing, governance, and innovation across CalHHS. It historically administered DxF governance, though DxF program administration moved to HCAI in August 2025; CDII retains an Insights Lab and cross-agency data governance role. Still a data and analytics buying-influence center — a fit for data, AI-ready infrastructure, and analytics conversations. Confirm current director before engaging.

Cybersecurity & data protection

Protecting health and social-services data at Medi-Cal scale (PHI, PII for one-third of the state) is a standing priority intensified by DxF data-sharing obligations. Statewide security policy and project oversight sit with CDT; departments run their own security programs — a layered security opportunity.

Procurement

Selling to CHHS means working the department buying centers plus the statewide vehicles, not a single agency desk. Most technology runs through department RFPs, statewide contracts administered by CDT and DGS (including California Multiple Award Schedules), and CDT's project-approval and procurement oversight for reportable IT projects. Confirm which department owns the requirement, whether a usable statewide contract exists, and whether the project falls under CDT oversight.

Where Dell Can Play

  • Map the federated buying centers — CHHS sets cross-agency data strategy via CDII, but DHCS (Medi-Cal) holds the largest IT and eligibility-systems budgets. Pursue department-level programs, not a single agency line.
  • Anchor on the Data Exchange Framework: position data platforms, integration, storage, and AI-ready infrastructure against the 2026 DxF milestones and SB 660 governance — a funded, mandated, agency-wide driver.
  • Lead with efficiency and modernization in a structural-deficit year: frame infrastructure refresh, automation, and consolidation as cost-takeout for Medi-Cal-scale eligibility and benefits systems.
