Overview

San Bernardino County is the largest county in the contiguous United States by land area (~20,000 sq mi) and home to roughly 2.2 million residents, governed by an elected five-member Board of Supervisors and run day-to-day by an appointed Chief Executive Officer. Enterprise technology is centralized in the Innovation & Technology Department (ITD), led by the County CIO, which delivers infrastructure, applications, data, GIS, and the countywide information-security program across dozens of departments. The county is a self-contained buying center: it sets its own technology direction and procures through its Purchasing Department rather than a state portal, while remaining eligible to leverage California statewide (CMAS) and national cooperative vehicles. For a Dell SLG seller, this is a large, sophisticated single-account opportunity — a recognized 'Digital County' that has leaned into AI productivity tooling, analytics, GIS, and security maturity — where the relationship map runs CEO → CIO/ITD → CISO and Purchasing.

Budget & Fiscal

San Bernardino County's total countywide budget is roughly $10.9B for FY2026-27 (a near-flat budget versus the prior year), funded largely by state and federal program dollars; the discretionary general funding the county controls directly — primarily property tax — is a much smaller slice. IT spend is embedded within ITD's appropriation and individual department budgets rather than published as a single line, so size deals against department initiatives, not a headline IT number.

Initiatives & Priorities

AI productivity & developer tooling

ITD deployed GitHub Copilot in its Solutions Development team, reporting roughly a 30% reduction in project timelines, and uses real-time translation (Wordly) for board meetings. An early, pragmatic AI-adoption posture — a hook for AI-ready endpoints, workstations, and infrastructure.

Data & analytics modernization

The county expanded analytics on Microsoft Dynamics 365 and stood up a Business Relationship Management function to align IT with departments. Analytics and data workloads drive demand for compute, storage, and data-platform infrastructure.
