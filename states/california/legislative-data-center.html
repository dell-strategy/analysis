Overview

The Legislative Data Center (LDC) is the information-technology branch of the California Office of Legislative Counsel — the nonpartisan agency that serves the State Legislature. The LDC delivers the technology that runs legislative business: it maintains the databases of pending legislation and existing law, builds and supports the applications behind bill publishing, Assembly and Senate floor sessions, vote recording, and committee hearings, and operates the public leginfo.legislature.ca.gov website. Per the Office's own description, the data center comprises more than 500 servers, a mainframe, and over 3,000 personal computers and wireless devices, connected by statewide wide-area, local-area, and wireless networks with sophisticated storage systems, and it staffs a customer-support center 24 hours a day, 365 days a year. Crucially for a seller, the LDC sits in the LEGISLATIVE branch — it is distinct from the executive-branch IT bodies (the California Department of Technology and the procurement-running Department of General Services) and generally operates and contracts under the Legislature's own authority rather than CDT/DGS rules. Much of the detail below is hedged: the legislative branch publishes far less operational and budget data than executive agencies.
