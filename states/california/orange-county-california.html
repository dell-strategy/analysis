Overview

Orange County is the local government for ~3.2 million residents — California's third-most-populous county, behind Los Angeles and San Diego — and one of the largest urban-county technology buyers in the state. IT is centralized: OC Information Technology (OCIT) is the county's internal service provider, delivering data-center, applications, client-services, networking, and security across roughly two dozen county agencies under the County Executive Office. OCIT is best understood as a shared-services shop that bills the operating departments it serves (Social Services, Health Care, Sheriff, Assessor, Registrar of Voters, and others) rather than a standalone agency with a large discretionary budget of its own — the program dollars largely sit with those departments. The county's FY2026-27 Recommended Budget is about $10.5B all-funds ($5.2B general fund), released May 20, 2026, with Board adoption scheduled for June 23, 2026. For a Dell SLG seller, the combination of a centralized IT buying center, a large stable budget, and an in-flight cloud and ERP modernization (Oracle Fusion for finance/HR, selected May 2025) points the motion toward infrastructure refresh, hybrid-cloud and data-center modernization, and security — routed through the county's central procurement office.

Budget & Fiscal

Orange County's FY2026-27 Recommended Budget totals about $10.5B all-funds, of which ~$5.2B is the General Fund and ~$1.3B is General Purpose Revenue covering mandated services. It was released May 20, 2026, with Board of Supervisors adoption scheduled for June 23, 2026. IT spend isn't published as a single public line — it flows through OCIT's internal cost-recovery model and the budgets of the ~23 county agencies it serves, so the buying signal is in department modernization plans rather than a standalone IT appropriation.

Initiatives & Priorities

Oracle Fusion Cloud ERP (finance & HR)

The county selected Oracle Fusion Cloud Applications in May 2025 to move finance and HR off legacy systems. A multi-year program signaling appetite for cloud modernization and a reference point for the infrastructure, data, and integration work that surrounds a major ERP migration.

Cloud services migration & data-center strategy

OCIT has run cloud-migration solicitations and lists Cloud, Mobility, Open Data, and Infrastructure as its core technology strategies. The pitch is hybrid-cloud and data-center modernization that pairs on-prem refresh with cloud workloads — Dell's ISG and as-a-service consumption fit this track.

County Cybersecurity Program

OCIT runs a formal county Cybersecurity Program — reducing security risk while enabling secure information exchange across county agencies and partners. FY25-26 strategic priorities lead with strengthening system and data security, making security a durable, recurring entry point for endpoint, identity, and infrastructure-security offerings.

Cybersecurity
High-availability network & systems refresh

OCIT's stated FY25-26 priorities include implementing a high-availability network and enhancing county systems and applications. As a centralized shared-services provider, it carries ongoing server, storage, network, and end-user compute refresh across agencies — a steady lifecycle-and-resilience motion.

Procurement

Selling to Orange County runs through its central County Procurement Office (CPO), which issues solicitations and manages vendor registration. Two paths shorten the cycle: national cooperative contracts the county has already adopted, and California statewide leveraged agreements the county can buy off without a fresh RFP.
