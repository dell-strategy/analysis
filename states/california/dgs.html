Overview

The California Department of General Services (DGS) is the state's central business-services agency — it runs statewide procurement, grants delegated purchasing authority to departments, and manages the state fleet, real estate, and building services. For a technology seller, DGS is best understood as the front door to how California state (and many local) entities buy goods and IT. Its Procurement Division (PD) holds statutory authority to acquire non-IT and IT goods and services for the state and to award Leveraged Procurement Agreements (LPAs) — the umbrella that includes California Multiple Award Schedules (CMAS), Master Service Agreements, statewide contracts, and cooperative agreements that state and local agencies can buy off without running their own solicitation. DGS also accredits departments to make their own delegated purchases. Note the division of labor with the California Department of Technology (CDT): DGS holds the underlying purchasing authority, but CDT has statutory authority over reportable/non-delegated IT projects and runs statewide technology procurement oversight — so larger IT deals typically touch both. Most supplier interaction flows through Cal eProcure, the state's online procurement portal and contracts register.

Budget & Fiscal

DGS is funded largely through cost recovery on the services it provides (the Service Revolving Fund and related funds) rather than a large general-fund appropriation — its leverage is the volume of procurement that flows through its vehicles, not its own spend. The Governor's proposed 2026-27 budget includes targeted technology-services investments routed through DGS and partner funds.

Initiatives & Priorities

Cal eProcure & procurement modernization

DGS continues to operate and refine Cal eProcure as the state's procurement portal and contracts register. Supplier registration, LPA/CMAS records, and the California State Contracts Register all run through it — the system to master for visibility and access.

Statewide digital services & Digital ID

The 2026-27 budget proposes expanding statewide digital service delivery and Digital ID as a service through DGS-related technology revolving funds — a track that pulls in design, data, and identity infrastructure.

Fleet electrification (ZEV)

DGS manages the state fleet and issues the statewide vehicle purchasing contract; California's ZEV mandate requires 50% of state/local fleet purchases be ZEV/NZEV through 2026, moving to 100% from 2027 — infrastructure and charging follow the fleet.

Fleet Sustainability
Delegated purchasing authority program

Through the Statewide Procurement Oversight and Consulting unit (SPOC, formerly PAU), DGS accredits departments to make their own purchases within set thresholds — shaping which deals are run centrally versus at the department level.
