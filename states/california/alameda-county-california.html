Overview

Alameda County is the local government for the Oakland / East Bay region — roughly 1.6 million residents, the 7th-most-populous county in California — and a top-tier urban-county technology buyer. IT is centralized in the Information Technology Department (ITD), an internal service provider of ~230 staff and a $100M+ operating budget that runs countywide cybersecurity, data governance, enterprise applications, cloud services, network infrastructure, digital services, and a 24/7 operations center supporting public safety, health, human services, property, and administrative systems. ITD is best understood as a shared-services shop delivering to the county's operating departments rather than a standalone agency with large discretionary spend — the bulk of the dollars sit with the departments it serves (Health Care Services, Social Services, Sheriff, Assessor, Registrar of Voters). Alameda has been named the nation's #1 digital county in the 1M+ population category two years running, a county that actively funds AI and modernization. For a Dell SLG seller, two things define the moment: a centralized, innovation-forward IT buying center, and a fresh leadership transition at the top of ITD that resets the relationship map.

Budget & Fiscal

Alameda County's FY2026-27 budget is $6.7B all-funds (a ~9% year-over-year increase) with a ~$4.3B general fund, presented by County Administrator Susan Muranishi on May 29, 2026 and unanimously adopted by the Board of Supervisors on June 25, 2026. The plan closed a $91.4M funding gap without layoffs or major service cuts — by eliminating 45 vacant positions, trimming departmental expenses, and 'right-sizing' vendor contracts. ITD runs a separate operating budget of over $100M, funded largely through internal cost recovery on the departments it serves rather than a single general-revenue line.

Initiatives & Priorities

AI-driven government services

A recognized national leader: ITD's published CIO Goals for 2026 prioritize 'promoting AI and other new technologies in automating business processes.' Award-cited proof points include a Conversational AI Assistant for the Board of Supervisors that improved search results ~35% and a revamped budget system that halved formulation/processing time. AI is real and funded here — tie infrastructure (compute, data platform) to concrete county workflows.

Legacy modernization & enterprise architecture

The 2026 CIO goals call to 'modernize legacy systems and infrastructure' and advance enterprise architecture, delivered through ITD's Project Life Cycle (PLC) on scope/schedule/budget. A durable lane for infrastructure refresh, data-center, and modernization that demonstrably lowers run-cost.

Modernization Data Center
Cybersecurity & data governance

ITD owns countywide cybersecurity, data governance, and a 24/7 operations center across critical systems. The 2026 goals direct staff to 'support and promote Centralized Technology, Cybersecurity, and AI policies.' Security assessments and resilience are recurring themes — a stable entry point for endpoint, identity, and infrastructure security.

Cybersecurity
Cloud services & 24/7 operations

ITD delivers cloud services and network infrastructure as part of its shared-services portfolio. As a centralized provider serving every county department, the pitch in a 'right-sizing' year is consolidation and lifecycle efficiency rather than greenfield expansion.

Procurement

Selling to Alameda County runs through the General Services Agency (GSA) Procurement Department, the central buying office for goods and services across all county departments. The county now publishes solicitations and receives vendor responses through an OpenGov eSourcing portal, and runs a Small, Local and Emerging Business (SLEB) program that requires prime vendors to secure 20% SLEB participation unless they qualify themselves.
