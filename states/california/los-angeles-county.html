Overview

Los Angeles County is the most populous county in the United States — roughly 10 million residents, larger than most U.S. states — governed by a five-member Board of Supervisors and run day-to-day through a County Chief Executive Office (CEO) overseeing roughly three dozen departments. Enterprise technology direction lives with the County Chief Information Office (CIO), which sits inside the CEO and oversees technology, data, security, and privacy countywide; the Chief Data Office, Privacy Office, and Chief Information Security Office report up through it. Operational IT, telecom, and — critically for sellers — most procurement run through the Internal Services Department (ISD). The County is best understood as a federated buyer: the CIO sets strategy and governance, large departments (Health Services, DHS, Sheriff, DPSS, the courts-adjacent agencies) carry their own substantial IT budgets and run their own modernization, and ISD plus the Board of Supervisors gate the contracts. For a Dell SLG seller, this is one of the largest single local-government opportunities in the country, but the entry point is the procurement machinery and the department-level buying centers, not a single central IT wallet.

Budget & Fiscal

The Board unanimously adopted a $50.3B FY2026-27 budget in late June 2026, up ~$1.5B from the prior year (the April 2026 Recommended Budget was $47.9B; the increase came largely from shifting ~$1.3B in restricted/special funds and ~$900M in new state behavioral-health money). It remains a cost-pressure cycle: the County carries a combined ~$4.8B AB 218 childhood-sexual-assault settlement liability (with ~150 new claims/month), plus federal/state funding risk. The adopted plan holds services largely intact but is roughly flat on headcount (a net ~202-position reduction to ~115,683 budgeted positions). IT spend is distributed across departments rather than concentrated in one central line.

Initiatives & Priorities

Generative AI adoption & AI Implementation Plan

The County is operationalizing generative AI countywide — an AI Implementation Plan with a 2026-27 phase to stand up AI Transformation Teams and bake responsible-AI practices into procurement and legal review, plus an annual County AI Professional Development Summit. AI-infrastructure and data-platform conversations are live.

Cloud data platforms & RPA Center of Excellence

The CIO has championed modern cloud data platforms and a Center of Excellence for Robotic Process Automation to drive automation and information sharing across departments — a fit for infrastructure, data, and as-a-service plays.

Regional cyber resilience ahead of 2026/2028 mega-events

LA is hardening critical-infrastructure cyber defenses ahead of the 2026 FIFA World Cup and 2028 Olympic & Paralympic Games. A Regional Cyber Coalition and a multi-county Threat Intelligence Sharing Platform are sharing real-time threat intel across the greater LA region.

Enterprise ERP & shared systems

The CIO oversees countywide Financials, Budget, Payroll, Personnel, HR, and property-tax systems serving ~110K employees — a long-running modernization and infrastructure-refresh track underpinning the whole enterprise.

Procurement

Selling to LA County means navigating its procurement machinery, not a single buying desk. ISD's Procurement division buys goods and services for County departments and provides governance through the Office of Countywide Contracting; the Board of Supervisors must approve service contracts over $100,000. The County also participates in cooperative purchasing to extend other jurisdictions' pricing and terms. Confirm which department owns the requirement, then map the ISD path and any co-op vehicle.

  • Map the federated buying centers — CIO plus new CEO Joseph Nicchitta for enterprise strategy, but Health Services, Sheriff, and DPSS hold large independent IT budgets. Pursue department-level programs, not a single central IT line.
  • Lead with efficiency and consolidation in an austerity year: position infrastructure refresh, automation (RPA), and AI-ready data platforms as cost-takeout, not net-new capital.
  • Tie ISG / AI-infrastructure to the County's generative-AI and cloud-data-platform push and the AI Implementation Plan's 2026-27 operational phase.
