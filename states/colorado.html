Colorado heads into FY2026–27 with a tighter $46.8B budget signed in May 2026 after lawmakers closed a roughly $1.5B shortfall, mostly through Medicaid cuts while sparing K-12. Three structural shifts matter to an SLG seller. First, the Governor's Office of Information Technology (OIT) just went through a major reorganization in May 2026 — roughly 173 positions eliminated, a new operating model, and a brand-new state CIO, Sarah Tuneberg, who started June 1, 2026. Second, the Colorado AI Act (SB 24-205) was repealed-and-replaced by SB 189 in May 2026, pushing the effective date to January 1, 2027 and pivoting from a heavy EU-style risk framework to a narrower disclosure/ADMT model. Third, the AI data-center wave is colliding with Colorado politics — two incentive bills died in the 2026 session, yet hyperscale interest (QTS in Aurora) and the Space Force/aerospace cluster keep demand alive. For Dell, the buying motion runs through OIT and the State Purchasing & Contracts Office (SPCO) plus NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts.

Budget & Fiscal

Colorado budgets annually via the 'Long Bill,' constrained by the TABOR revenue cap. The FY2026–27 budget signed May 8, 2026 totals about $46.8B — roughly $4B more than the prior year — but only after closing an estimated $1.5B shortfall, largely through Medicaid reductions while protecting K-12. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend runs through OIT (which has managed a roughly $373M annual budget plus about $1B in federal grant spend) and is distributed across agencies.

Procurement & Competition

Colorado runs a comparatively decentralized procurement model. The State Purchasing & Contracts Office (SPCO), within the Office of the State Controller, manages purchasing for state agencies and higher-ed, while the Governor's Office of Information Technology (OIT) is the central technology authority for the executive branch. Colorado leans heavily on NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts (NASPO is headquartered in the region and Colorado leads several cooperative solicitations), letting agencies buy from pre-competed agreements.
