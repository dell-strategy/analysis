SB 149 / AI Policy Office
First-in-nation AI policy office + Learning Lab

Utah punches well above its size as a digital-government leader and is one of the most consequential SLG accounts in the Mountain West. Three things define the opportunity. First, Utah was the first state to create a dedicated Office of Artificial Intelligence Policy (SB 149, 2024) and an AI 'Learning Lab' regulatory sandbox; in late 2025 Governor Cox layered on a $10M 'Pro-Human' AI initiative and lawmakers funded $15M of compute capacity for state AI use — agencies are actively operationalizing governed AI. Second, Utah's IT is highly centralized: the Division of Technology Services (DTS), under CIO Alan Fuller, runs a ~$277M budget, 750+ projects, and the new MyUtah citizen portal, making DTS the dominant decision point. Third, the 'Silicon Slopes' data-center buildout (Creekstone's multi-gigawatt Delta 'gigasite', Box Elder, DataBank SL6, BluSky in Nephi) plus Texas Instruments' multi-fab investment make Utah a national AI-infrastructure flashpoint. For Dell, the buying motion runs through the Utah-led NASPO ValuePoint Cloud Solutions cooperative (Dell is an awarded supplier) and state cooperative contracts, with GovRAMP now the cloud-security gate.

Where Dell can play
  • Governed AI for state agencies: $15M compute appropriation + $10M Pro-Human initiative open the door for private/on-prem AI on sensitive data under Utah's SB 149 framework.
  • AI-infrastructure demand tied to the Silicon Slopes data-center boom (PowerEdge, networking, services) and university research computing.
  • DTS modernization: legacy payroll/HR replacement, MyUtah portal, digital ID/verifiable credentials, and a whole-of-state cybersecurity strategy.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across a centralized executive branch and the local-government devices DTS already protects.
  • Health AI: University of Utah's Utah Health AI Vault (UHAIV) build-out with state support.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with governed AI: tie Dell on-prem/private AI and secure compute to Utah's SB 149 framework, the $10M Pro-Human initiative, and the $15M state AI compute appropriation for sensitive-data workloads.
  • Ride the Silicon Slopes data-center boom — position Dell ISG / AI-infrastructure with operators and the university research-computing and health-AI (UHAIV) buyers.
  • Make DTS the anchor account: align to MyUtah, legacy payroll/HR modernization, digital ID, and the AI-for-employees program in a centralized, single-decision-point IT org.
  • Confirm GovRAMP readiness for any cloud/as-a-service offering touching state data before pursuing deals — it is now Utah's cloud-security gate.
  • Leverage the Dell NASPO ValuePoint Cloud Solutions award (Utah-led cooperative) and validate state cooperative-contract and reseller coverage for the agencies you're targeting.

Budget & Fiscal

Utah budgets on an annual fiscal year (July 1–June 30) cycle. The 2026 Legislature approved a roughly $31.6B FY2027 budget, of which about $12.3B is state funds; Governor Cox's recommended plan was ~$30.7B and framed around 'responsible fiscal management.' There is no single 'state IT budget' line — most executive-branch technology spend is centralized in DTS (~$277M), with named one-time AI and compute items funded in the 2026 session.

Procurement & Competition

Utah's IT is unusually centralized. The Division of Technology Services (DTS), within the Department of Government Operations, is the executive-branch IT provider and a primary decision point. Statewide buying runs through the Division of Purchasing and General Services (state cooperative contracts) and, notably, the Utah-led NASPO ValuePoint Cloud Solutions cooperative — Utah is the lead state for the national cloud cooperative used by SLG entities nationwide.
