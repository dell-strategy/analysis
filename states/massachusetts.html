Massachusetts pairs a deep public-sector technology footprint with the densest concentration of research universities in the country, and the Healey administration has put applied AI at the center of its agenda. The Massachusetts AI Hub — a MassTech-run initiative recommended by the Governor's AI Task Force — anchors the strategy, backed by a $31M state grant to expand sustainable high-performance computing through the Massachusetts Green High Performance Computing Center (MGHPCC) and a projected ~$120M combined five-year investment with member universities. Central IT runs through the Executive Office of Technology Services and Security (EOTSS) under Secretary & Commonwealth CIO Jason Snyder, while purchasing flows through Operational Services Division (OSD) statewide contracts on COMMBUYS. The Mass Leads Act ($3.9B economic-development law) added permitting and investment tools for data centers, life sciences, and applied AI. For Dell, the strongest angle is research compute and AI infrastructure tied to the AI Hub, MGHPCC, and the BU/Harvard/MIT/Northeastern/UMass cluster, layered over enterprise refresh and a maturing cybersecurity program.

Where Dell can play
  • Research compute and AI infrastructure tied to the Massachusetts AI Hub and MGHPCC's new AI Compute Resources (AICR) environment (PowerEdge, GPU, networking, services).
  • University research-computing demand across the six MGHPCC members — Boston University, Harvard, MIT, Northeastern, UMass, and Yale.
  • Enterprise and endpoint refresh across executive agencies consolidated under EOTSS.
  • Cybersecurity infrastructure and managed services as the Commonwealth SOC and municipal cyber programs scale.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with research compute and AI infrastructure: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Massachusetts AI Hub, the MGHPCC AICR environment, and the BU/Harvard/MIT/Northeastern/UMass cluster.
  • Engage MassTech and the AI Hub (Sabrina Mansur) as the front door to applied-AI and compute investment — influence early, before vehicles are set.
  • Emphasize sustainability in HPC and data-center proposals — it is a stated MGHPCC and Commonwealth priority, not a nice-to-have.
  • Validate Dell's position on OSD statewide IT contracts (ITC73 hardware and the software/services vehicles) on COMMBUYS for state, municipal, and education buyers.
  • Map secure-compute and managed-services offerings to the EOTSS Commonwealth SOC and the CyberTrust municipal cyber program as those programs scale.

Budget & Fiscal

Massachusetts budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1–June 30). Governor Healey signed the $60.9B FY2026 General Appropriations Act on July 4, 2025 — roughly $3.2B above FY2025 but about $1B below her January filing, reflecting fiscal caution. There is no single 'state IT budget'; technology spend is distributed across agencies, consolidated enterprise services run through EOTSS (line item 1790-0100), and a set of named AI, compute, and cybersecurity initiatives funded outside the core operating budget through MassTech and capital programs.

Initiatives & Policy

Massachusetts AI Hub5

Active / scaling

A MassTech-run initiative and the centerpiece of the Healey administration's applied-AI strategy, recommended by the Governor's Strategic AI Task Force. It coordinates state AI investment, runs the AI Models Innovation Challenge (up to $2M in domain-specific model grants), and partners with MGHPCC to expand compute. Sabrina Mansur was named inaugural executive director in May 2025. The Hub is the front door to the state's applied-AI and research-compute demand.

AI Compute Resources via MGHPCC17

Funded

A $31M state grant (announced May 2025) expands sustainable high-performance computing through the Massachusetts Green High Performance Computing Center, creating an AI Compute Resources (AICR) environment for universities, startups, and businesses. In December 2025 the state selected Cambridge Computer — partnering with Dell Technologies and VAST Data — to build it in Holyoke on hundreds of NVIDIA B200/RTX GPUs powered by 100% carbon-free hydro. Combined state and university investment is projected to exceed $120M by 2030. MGHPCC's six members — BU, Harvard, MIT, Northeastern, UMass, and Yale — make this the strongest tailwind for Dell AI-infrastructure and ISG demand in the state.

Mass Leads Act6

In effect

A $3.9B economic-development law signed November 2024 to keep Massachusetts a leader in life sciences, climatetech, and applied AI. It streamlines permitting (Permit Extension Act, a State Permitting Regulatory Office) and drives investment in data centers and emerging industries. A follow-on Mass Wins Act extends the agenda to AI, quantum, defense, and robotics — relevant context for data-center and infrastructure demand.

Commonwealth Cybersecurity Program8

Maturing

EOTSS runs a unified Commonwealth Security Operations Center (SIEM, vulnerability management, threat intel, incident response). In 2025 it handled 41,000+ security events and mitigated 400,000+ vulnerabilities (a 26% YoY increase). Separately, CyberTrust Massachusetts operates a SOC for municipalities, with the Cyber Resilient Massachusetts grant raising per-entity awards to up to $50K. Secure compute, infrastructure, and managed services map directly to this buildout.

Procurement & Competition

Massachusetts splits technology governance from purchasing. The Executive Office of Technology Services and Security (EOTSS) sets enterprise IT strategy, policy, and security and runs consolidated services for executive agencies. The Operational Services Division (OSD) establishes the statewide contracts that state, municipal, and public-education buyers use, transacted through the COMMBUYS e-procurement system. For Dell, the practical buying motion runs through OSD IT statewide contracts.

Competitive landscape

Massachusetts is a competitive, contract-driven market with strong incumbents on OSD IT statewide contracts and deep vendor relationships across the university cluster. Differentiation tends to come from AI-infrastructure and HPC capability, sustainability (a stated MGHPCC and state priority), and services that map to the AI Hub and cybersecurity buildout rather than price alone.
