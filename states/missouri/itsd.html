Overview

The Information Technology Services Division (ITSD) sits within Missouri's Office of Administration (OA) and is the state's consolidated executive-branch IT organization. A team of roughly 1,000 IT professionals delivers client engagement, information security, networks and telecommunications, and web/application development across about 15 IT-consolidated executive departments (Agriculture, Corrections, Education, Health & Senior Services, and others), plus the Governor's and Lt. Governor's offices. Consolidation is not total: the Departments of Conservation and Transportation run their own IT, and several statewide elected offices (Attorney General, Auditor, Secretary of State, Treasurer) operate independently — so ITSD is the central executive-branch buyer and standards layer, not a single front door for all of Missouri government. Procurement is a separate OA function: the Division of Purchasing establishes statewide contracts through MissouriBUYS and extends them to local governments via the Cooperative Procurement Program. For a Dell SLG seller, ITSD is both a large enterprise IT buyer and the entity whose standards, security gates, and refresh cycles agency-level deals must align to — set against a fast-moving Missouri AI and data-center backdrop (the $15B Google New Florence project and a new statewide AI executive order).

Budget & Fiscal

ITSD's technology spend is appropriated through the Office of Administration in Missouri's annual budget (state fiscal year begins July 1). ITSD is best read as a delivery-and-standards layer: much of the real buying power sits in the consolidated departments' IT lines that ITSD supports, plus the OA digital-government and infrastructure appropriations. Initiatives & Priorities

Statewide AI strategic framework (EO 26-02)

Governor Kehoe's Executive Order 26-02 (signed Jan 14, 2026) directs OA Commissioner Ken Zellers to build a strategic framework for AI integration across Missouri government — covering service delivery, data privacy/security, human-in-the-loop decision-making, transparency, and data quality — with findings due to the Governor by Nov 30, 2026. ITSD is the natural delivery arm. Position AI-ready infrastructure, compute, and secure data platforms behind responsible-AI adoption.

Data-center & energy context (Google New Florence)

Google's ~$15B data-center investment in New Florence (Montgomery County), code-named Project Spade, is the largest single private investment in state history and sits beside an Amazon-committed site. Paired with SB 4 (signed Apr 9, 2025), which makes large-load data centers pay 100% of their power and infrastructure costs, it positions Missouri as an AI-infrastructure hub. Relevant as market context and a state energy/policy priority rather than an ITSD purchase — useful framing for AI-infrastructure conversations.

Enterprise cybersecurity & whole-of-state posture

ITSD's Information Security office runs executive-branch security; statewide, federal SLCGP grants (administered by the Dept. of Public Safety) push funding to local governments and schools. A combined enterprise-plus-grant security landscape that reaches beyond state agencies into county, city, and K-12 buyers.

Procurement

Selling to ITSD and the broader Missouri public sector runs through the OA Division of Purchasing, not one-off agency RFPs. Statewide contracts are established competitively and published on the MissouriBUYS Contract Board; eligible locals buy off them via the Cooperative Procurement Program. Two things to confirm first: that Dell holds the relevant Missouri statewide / NASPO ValuePoint coverage, and that Dell is registered and current in MissouriBUYS.
