Overview

The Colorado Governor's Office of Information Technology (OIT) is the state's central IT organization, established to consolidate and operate technology across executive-branch agencies. By statute, most IT procurement and services for state agencies route through OIT, which delivers enterprise infrastructure, applications, networking, identity, cybersecurity, and customer-facing digital services — the technology behind programs like SNAP benefits, unemployment insurance, and driver-license renewals. OIT also houses the Colorado Broadband Office (CBO), giving it ownership of the state's federally funded broadband buildout, and it leads statewide AI governance through Colorado's GenAI strategy and policy. For a seller, OIT is best understood as the consolidated front door to executive-branch technology rather than a federated set of agency CIOs: the central agency owns the standards, the procurement path, and much of the delivery. OIT is in the middle of a significant 2026 reset — a new CIO took over June 1, 2026, alongside a restructuring that eliminated roughly 173 positions and is shifting OIT toward a 'human-centered,' product-delivery operating model that pairs technology teams with agencies. Re-mapping buying centers and confirming program ownership is essential before forecasting.

Budget & Fiscal

OIT's own footprint is cited at roughly $373 million annually (per its May 2026 restructuring announcement), and it also stewards large federal grant dollars — most visibly the broadband program. The agency budgets through the state's FY2026-27 process and the Joint Technology Committee (JTC) / Joint Budget Committee (JBC), which set IT capital and operating appropriations. Treat the headline figure as OIT's operating scale; the largest program dollars (e.g., broadband) flow as federal grants rather than general fund.

Initiatives & Priorities

2026 restructuring & 'human-centered' operating model

OIT announced a May 2026 strategic realignment — eliminating ~173 roles and shifting to a product-delivery model that embeds technology teams with agencies (an Amazon/Google/Microsoft-style approach) to cut technical debt, manual processes, and complex governance. Re-map the buying centers and confirm who owns a given program before forecasting.

Modernization Transition
GenAI strategy & statewide AI policy

OIT leads statewide AI governance, with a 2026 'Version 2.0' GenAI strategy scaling use cases that automate routine work and piloting GenAI for resident-facing interactions, plus a Statewide GenAI Policy for security and compliance. Colorado's separate AI consumer-protection law (originally SB 24-205) was repealed/replaced by SB 26-189 with an effective date moved to Jan 1, 2027 — a shifting regulatory backdrop. A driver for AI-ready infrastructure and governance tooling.

AI Governance
Broadband buildout (Colorado Broadband Office / BEAD)

OIT's Colorado Broadband Office stewards ~$826M in federal BEAD funds (Final Proposal approved; $420M+ unlocked) plus middle-mile and state-funded Advance-Local programs to connect unserved homes and businesses. A multi-year infrastructure program with networking, connectivity, and edge implications.

Cybersecurity & enterprise modernization

OIT's Office of Information Security sets statewide security standards and runs security operations for executive-branch agencies, alongside ongoing infrastructure, cloud, and application modernization to retire technical debt. A standing opportunity for security, infrastructure refresh, and managed services — confirm current security leadership before engaging.
