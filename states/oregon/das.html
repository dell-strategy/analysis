Overview

The Oregon Department of Administrative Services (DAS) is the state's central administrative agency — it provides shared business services (HR, finance, fleet, facilities, procurement) to other agencies and sets statewide operating policy. For a technology seller, the relevant engine inside DAS is Enterprise Information Services (EIS), the division led by State Chief Information Officer Terrence Woods that owns statewide IT strategy, the State Data Center, enterprise security, and emerging AI governance. EIS sets the policies and standards that other Oregon agencies follow, runs shared infrastructure, and is the office shaping how the state approaches generative AI and cloud. DAS Procurement Services operates the statewide contracting vehicles — including Statewide Price Agreements and the OregonBuys eProcurement platform — that most state agencies, and many local governments through cooperative purchasing, buy technology through. DAS is best understood as both a policy-and-standards authority (via EIS) and the central path to market (via Procurement Services) rather than the largest end spender of IT dollars.

Budget & Fiscal

DAS is funded largely through assessments and charges to the agencies it serves rather than a single large general-fund IT line, so its leverage is in the standards it sets and the contracts that run through it. Oregon budgets on a two-year cycle; the 2025–27 biennial budget is the relevant planning window, and enterprise IT investments are reviewed through the state's IT governance and stage-gate process.

Initiatives & Priorities

State AI Action Plan & AI governance

Oregon has moved to govern state use of generative AI through an AI advisory body and action plan coordinated through DAS/EIS. Expect procurement guardrails, acceptable-use policy, and pilots — align AI-infrastructure and trusted-AI messaging to the state's own framework.

AI Governance
Cloud security gate (GovRAMP)

Oregon is among states adopting GovRAMP (formerly StateRAMP) as a cloud-security authorization standard. Cloud and as-a-service offerings should hold the appropriate authorization level to clear procurement — treat it as an early gate, not an afterthought.

Cloud Compliance
State Data Center & infrastructure modernization

EIS operates Oregon's enterprise data center and shared infrastructure. Ongoing refresh, hybrid-cloud, and resiliency work is a recurring track for ISG infrastructure, storage, and managed services.

Data Center Modernization
Enterprise cybersecurity & Cyber Oregon

EIS houses enterprise security and the state CISO function, with statewide standards and incident response. Security modernization and zero-trust alignment are durable themes across state and, via shared services, local entities.
