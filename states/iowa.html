Iowa is in the middle of one of the most consequential state-IT shifts in the country, and every SLG seller should understand three things. First, on June 9, 2026 Gov. Reynolds announced a sweeping migration of executive-branch IT to Amazon Web Services with Cognizant Government Solutions running day-to-day operations — retiring dozens of state data centers and thousands of physical servers, with roughly 200 DoIT staff moving to Cognizant and service starting August 3, 2026. That changes who owns infrastructure decisions and compresses the legacy on-prem footprint. Second, Iowa is a national hyperscale data-center hub: Google's $7B Cedar Rapids/Council Bluffs expansion and Microsoft's $6B+ West Des Moines campuses anchor a pipeline of AI-infrastructure investment. Third, Iowa's enterprise generative-AI policy (effective March 31, 2025) sets guardrails for state-agency AI use. For Dell, the central buying motion runs through Iowa DAS statewide Master Agreements and DOM-DoIT IT procurement; the cloud consolidation reshapes the on-prem opportunity while higher-ed and hyperscale buildouts open AI-infrastructure demand.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure / 'AI factory' demand tied to the Google and Microsoft hyperscale data-center buildout and the supporting ecosystem (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • Higher-ed research computing at the University of Iowa and Iowa State — independent, AI-forward infrastructure buyers.
  • Edge, endpoint, and lifecycle refresh as the state standardizes under a consolidated DOM-DoIT and a leaner on-prem footprint.
  • Governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive agency workloads as Iowa operationalizes its generative-AI policy.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead the AI-infrastructure conversation: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Google and Microsoft hyperscale buildout and the supporting ecosystem and partners.
  • Prioritize higher-ed — University of Iowa and Iowa State research computing are independent, AI-forward infrastructure buyers outside the executive-branch cloud move.
  • Re-scope the executive-branch motion around the AWS/Cognizant migration: focus on edge, endpoint, lifecycle, and AI workloads that stay off the public cloud, and engage Cognizant as an operations influencer.
  • Confirm Dell's DAS statewide Master Agreement coverage and reseller scope, and ensure registration/activity in IMPACS for the agencies you target.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as agencies operationalize Iowa's generative-AI policy.

Budget & Fiscal

Iowa budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1 – June 30). The Legislature enacted roughly $9.42B in General Fund appropriations for FY2026 — about 5.3% above revised FY2025. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies and through the centralized Department of Management Division of Information Technology (DOM-DoIT, formerly the OCIO). The state's cloud migration is expected to shift IT cost from on-prem infrastructure toward managed cloud services over the next decade.

Enterprise Generative-AI Policy6

In effect Mar 31, 2025

Iowa's statewide generative-AI policy (Enterprise PY-AI) sets minimum requirements and prohibited uses for agency AI, mandating human evaluation of AI output before it informs decisions. A state DOGE task force is also exploring AI and automation to cut costs. Agencies now need governed, auditable AI — an opening to lead with private/on-prem AI for sensitive data rather than open public models.

Procurement & Competition

Iowa centralizes technology under the Department of Management Division of Information Technology (DOM-DoIT, formerly the Office of the Chief Information Officer), which leads IT standards, cybersecurity, and IT procurement, contracting, and vendor management. General and IT purchasing run through the Department of Administrative Services (DAS) Central Procurement, whose statewide Master Agreements support a Cooperative Purchasing Program open to state agencies, counties, cities, and other Iowa public entities. IT service agreements established by DAS-CP or DOM-DoIT can carry terms up to 10 years.

Competitive landscape

Iowa is a centralized, contract-driven market consolidating onto AWS-managed cloud and operated by Cognizant — so the executive-branch on-prem opportunity is narrowing.
