Overview

Clark County is the most populous county in Nevada — roughly 2.4 million residents and about 43 million annual visitors — and the seat of the Las Vegas metro, the Las Vegas Strip, and Harry Reid International Airport. It is governed by a seven-member Board of County Commissioners elected from geographic districts, with day-to-day operations run by an appointed County Manager overseeing roughly three dozen departments and more than 10,000 employees, including major enterprises such as University Medical Center and the airport. Enterprise technology direction lives with the County's Information Technology department, led by the Chief Information Officer, which runs IT strategy, digital services, infrastructure, data, and security for more than 700 county services. The County is best understood as a large, federated buyer: IT sets enterprise strategy and standards while individual departments carry their own programs, and the Purchasing & Contracts Division plus the Board of Commissioners gate the contracts. For a Dell SLG seller, Clark County is one of the largest single local-government opportunities in the Mountain West, but the entry point is the procurement machinery (NRS 332) and the department-level buying centers rather than a single central IT wallet.

Budget & Fiscal

Clark County's total spending plan across all funds runs roughly $11.7B, with an FY2026 (begins July 1, 2025) operational budget of about $2.1B adopted in May 2025. FY2026 is a cost-pressure cycle: a softening economy and declining sales-tax (C-Tax) receipts opened a roughly $56M general-fund gap, which the County closed with a one-time transfer from capital funds. IT spend is distributed across departments rather than concentrated in a single central line.

Initiatives & Priorities

Applied AI ('augmented intelligence') across services

The County is applying AI to its 700+ services rather than running it as a standalone program — multiple AI pilots are active, with an emphasis on privacy, security, and AI literacy among staff. The County participates in the GovAI Coalition for responsible implementation. A live opening for AI-infrastructure and data-platform conversations.

Cybersecurity posture & broadband

The County reports defending against roughly a million intrusion attempts per week and treats security as a standing investment. A 'last-mile' broadband / digital-equity effort is being reassessed and locally re-funded after federal program changes (Digital Equity Act termination). Security and connectivity are durable buying centers.

Procurement

Selling to Clark County runs through its Purchasing & Contracts Division, which conducts solicitations (bids, RFPs, RFIs) and prepares contracts for the County's departments in compliance with the Nevada Local Government Purchasing Act (NRS 332). Vendors must register to access opportunities, and sizable awards require Board of Commissioners approval. Confirm which department owns the requirement, then map the Purchasing path and any usable cooperative vehicle.
