Nevada Dept of Administration

Overview

The Nevada Department of Administration (DoA) is the state's internal-services department, providing centralized support functions to executive-branch agencies through roughly ten divisions — including Administrative Services, Human Resource Management, Risk Management, State Public Works, and, most relevant to a technology seller, the State Purchasing Division. For a Dell SLG seller the important structural point is that the DoA is the procurement door, not the IT-strategy owner: the Purchasing Division (NRS Chapter 333) is the central authority that runs Nevada's statewide contracts and buys goods and services on behalf of state agencies. Note an org change that is easy to get wrong — the Enterprise Information Technology Services (EITS) division, historically housed under the DoA, transitioned in 2023 into what is now the cabinet-level Governor's Technology Office (GTO) / Office of the CIO. Statewide IT policy, the enterprise service desk, security, cloud, and the new GovRAMP cloud-security mandate therefore live with the GTO today, while the DoA retains the central purchasing and broader administrative functions. Treat the two as paired but distinct buying centers.

Budget & Fiscal

The DoA is an internal-services department funded largely through cost allocations and assessments to the agencies it serves rather than a single large general-fund line, so its leverage for a technology seller is the procurement volume it channels rather than its own IT appropriation. For scale, Governor Joe Lombardo's 2025–2027 executive budget proposed roughly $12.7B in general-fund spending across the biennium — the pool that statewide-contract purchases ultimately draw from.

Initiatives & Priorities

GovRAMP cloud-security mandate

Nevada has adopted GovRAMP as its statewide cloud third-party risk and authorization program, aligned to NIST 800-53 Rev. 5. Public reporting points to a July 2026 effective point after which new executive-branch cloud contracts require demonstrated progress on the GovRAMP path. Owned by the GTO/CIO, but a direct gate on anything cloud sold through Purchasing — clear it early.

EITS → Governor's Technology Office consolidation

Cyber resilience & zero trust

Following a publicly reported ransomware incident, state IT leadership has emphasized zero-trust architecture, identity and access management as the 'new firewall,' and security-by-verification. Infrastructure refresh, endpoint, and identity modernization are live themes — largely GTO-led but procured through statewide vehicles.

Procurement

Selling into Nevada state agencies runs through the DoA's State Purchasing Division and its pre-negotiated statewide contracts under NRS Chapter 333, rather than one-off agency RFPs wherever a contract already exists. Two things to confirm first: that Dell sits on the right active statewide contract category (e.g., computer equipment, software, cloud), and that any cloud or as-a-service offering can demonstrate GovRAMP progress ahead of the July 2026 gate.
