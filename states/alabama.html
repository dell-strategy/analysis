Alabama enters FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025–Sept 30, 2026) with a record $3.7B General Fund and a nearly $10B Education Trust Fund, and several structural shifts a Dell seller should track. First, Act 2025-369 (HB207) formally codified cybersecurity under the Office of Information Technology (OIT) and its CIO and made OIT the centralized approving authority for new technology acquisitions — concentrating both security and buying decisions in one office. Second, Governor Ivey stood up a Technology Quality Assurance Board in February 2026 to set statewide standards for AI and emerging tech, building on her GenAI Task Force and a NIST-aligned governance posture. Third, Alabama has become a magnet for AI data-center investment (Google's $1.5B Jackson County expansion, Meta's Montgomery and Huntsville campuses, and large proposed AI sites), while U.S. Space Command's phased move to Redstone Arsenal deepens an already dense Huntsville defense and aerospace tech cluster. For Dell, the motion runs through OIT-governed statewide contracts and cooperative purchasing, and the strongest tailwinds are AI infrastructure, the defense/space buyers around Huntsville, and a modernizing, cyber-focused state IT office.

Budget & Fiscal

Alabama budgets annually on a fiscal year running October 1 to September 30, split between the General Fund (non-education government) and the Education Trust Fund (ETF). The FY2026 budgets, signed by Governor Ivey in May 2025 and effective Oct 1, 2025, set records: roughly $3.7B General Fund (up about 10% / $347M) and nearly $10B ETF. There is no single published 'state IT budget' line — OIT operates largely on a chargeback model funded through a Telecommunications Revolving Fund, and technology spend is distributed across agencies.

Initiatives & Policy

OIT Cybersecurity Codification (Act 2025-369 / HB207)2

Enacted May 2025

HB207 (Act 2025-369), signed May 2025, formally placed cybersecurity responsibilities under the OIT and its CIO, consolidated technology-operations law, confirmed OIT as the centralized approving authority for new technology acquisitions, and created a Telecommunications Revolving Fund for IT services. A strengthened, centralized security mandate under CIO Daniel Urquhart and CISO Chadwick Smith means secure-infrastructure, endpoint, and managed-services needs concentrate in one office.

Technology Quality Assurance Board3

Formed Feb 2026

Announced February 13, 2026, the TQA Board sets statewide standards for AI and emerging tech across executive agencies (Education, Finance, Law Enforcement, Medicaid, Technology, Transportation, Revenue). It operationalizes the GenAI Task Force recommendations — NIST AI Risk Management Framework adoption, agency GenAI policies, and employee AI training — creating demand for governed, auditable AI rather than open public models.

Procurement & Competition

Alabama's Office of Information Technology (OIT) is the centralized approving authority for new state technology acquisitions, a role reinforced by Act 2025-369. Statewide contracts are competitively bid through the Alabama Department of Finance's State Purchasing Division, and a 2009 cooperative-purchasing law (Act 2009-763) lets state entities buy off agreements competitively let by approved purchasing cooperatives. OIT's IT Procurement Policy (230-00) requires use of established statewide contracts where applicable and lets the CIO or State Purchasing Director hold non-compliant requests.
