Overview

The New Jersey Department of the Treasury is the state's finance and administration backbone. It houses the Office of Management and Budget (OMB), which builds and executes the state budget, and the Division of Purchase & Property (DPP), the central procurement agency that runs the NJSTART eProcurement portal and awards the statewide term contracts that state agencies, and many counties, municipalities, schools, and authorities, buy off of. For a technology seller, Treasury is best understood as the contracting path and the budget authority rather than the primary end user of IT — central IT strategy, the state data center, cybersecurity, and the NJ AI Assistant are run by a separate agency, the NJ Office of Information Technology (NJOIT), led by the Chief Technology Officer. The account sits inside a leadership transition: Governor Mikie Sherrill took office in January 2026 with a new Treasurer (Aaron Binder) and a new CTO (Kevin Dehmer), so buying centers and priorities are being reset across both Treasury and NJOIT.

Budget & Fiscal

Treasury owns the budget process through OMB, so its leverage is the whole state spending plan, not a single agency line. Governor Sherrill signed her inaugural FY2027 budget into law on June 30, 2026 at about $60.7B, with a surplus of roughly $6.08B; the bulk flows out as property-tax relief and aid to schools and local governments rather than direct state operations.

Initiatives & Priorities

NJSTART eProcurement modernization

NJSTART is NJ's centralized, web-based procurement portal run by DPP — vendor registration, solicitations/RFPs, awarded term contracts, and electronic invoicing all flow through it. Being registered and correctly categorized in NJSTART is the precondition to selling to or through the state.

Procurement Digital Government
NJ AI Assistant & responsible-AI push

NJ (via the NJ Innovation Authority with NJOIT) launched the NJ AI Assistant, a genAI environment for state employees, alongside an AI Task Force and statewide responsible-use policy and training. AI adoption is led outside Treasury at NJOIT/Innovation, but it signals demand for AI-ready infrastructure across state agencies.

AI Workforce
Digital government & efficiency agenda

The new administration is prioritizing permitting/licensing modernization, clearing backlogs, and cutting wait times — work that runs through NJOIT and individual agencies and is funded via OMB. Modernization and managed-services plays should map to these stated efficiency goals.

Modernization Citizen Services
Cybersecurity posture (NJOIT / NJCCIC)

Statewide cyber is run outside Treasury — by NJOIT and the NJ Cybersecurity & Communications Integration Cell (NJCCIC) — with the CTO mandate to strengthen defenses against AI-driven threats. Treasury/DPP is the contracting path; the security buying center sits with NJOIT.

Procurement

Selling to New Jersey runs through DPP's central vehicles, not one-off agency RFPs in most cases. Two things to confirm first: an active, correctly scoped NJSTART vendor registration, and which statewide term contract(s) a Dell offering falls under. Cooperative purchasing also lets local governments, schools, and authorities ride state contracts.
