Overview

The New Jersey Turnpike Authority (NJTA) is an independent state authority that operates and maintains two of the nation's busiest toll roads — the New Jersey Turnpike and the Garden State Parkway — from its Woodbridge headquarters. It is funded almost entirely by toll revenue rather than general appropriations, which gives it an unusually large and self-directed capital program: NJTA's board has authorized a multi-year capital plan reported at roughly $24B for the Turnpike and Parkway, financed in part by scheduled toll increases (a 3% hike took effect January 1, 2026). Beyond roadway and bridge construction, the Authority runs a deep technology footprint — E-ZPass and cashless-tolling systems, intelligent transportation systems (ITS) and traffic management, operational technology (OT) for field devices and SCADA-style controls, and enterprise IT and cybersecurity across its workforce and customer-service operations. For a Dell SLG seller, NJTA is a large, capital-rich, self-funded buyer whose tolling-modernization, data-center, OT/IT-convergence, and cyber needs run on its own public bidding process rather than a state co-op.

Budget & Fiscal

NJTA is toll-funded, not appropriation-funded, so its spending power tracks traffic and toll policy rather than the state budget. Its 2026 budget is reported at roughly $2.8B, with toll revenue expected to supply more than $2.3B of it; alongside that operating budget sits a multi-year capital program reported in the $24B range for the Turnpike and Parkway.

Initiatives & Priorities

E-ZPass back-office modernization

In September 2024 NJTA awarded a long-term contract (reported at ~$1.73B over 13 years) to TransCore for a new E-ZPass back-office account-management system and enhanced customer-service operations. A large, multi-year tolling-platform refresh with surrounding data, integration, and infrastructure needs.

Tolling Modernization
Cashless / all-electronic tolling

New Jersey toll roads are moving toward all-electronic tolling (the neighboring Atlantic City Expressway went fully cashless in January 2026). Removing toll plazas in favor of gantries and back-office processing shifts spend toward sensors, networking, edge/OT, and data systems.

Tolling ITS
ITS & traffic management

Intelligent transportation systems — variable message signs, cameras, detection, and traffic operations centers — are an ongoing modernization track tied to the capital program. Edge compute, networking, storage, and analytics are natural infrastructure plays.

ITS Infrastructure
Cybersecurity & OT/IT convergence

NJTA's IT organization has prioritized hardening cybersecurity across enterprise IT and the operational-technology environment that runs tolling and field systems. OT/IT convergence, segmentation, and resilient infrastructure are active needs as tolling goes fully digital.

Procurement

NJTA buys through its own public bidding and RFP process, not a state cooperative — vendors register with the Authority and respond to advertised solicitations. Two things to confirm early: that Dell is a registered NJTA vendor, and which contracts run direct versus through a reseller or any applicable state cooperative.
