New Jersey Judiciary (Courts)

Overview

The New Jersey Judiciary is one of the most centralized court systems in the country: the Supreme Court, the Appellate Division, the trial-court Superior Court across 15 county-based vicinages, the Tax Court, and the municipal courts all operate as a single statewide enterprise rather than a loose federation of county courts. The Administrative Office of the Courts (AOC), headquartered in Trenton, is the operational hub — it handles budget, personnel, training, statistics, and the development and operation of the Judiciary's information systems. That centralization makes the AOC the single technology decision point for the entire branch, which is unusual and useful from a seller's perspective: case management, e-filing, networking, end-user compute, and security are set centrally and rolled out to every vicinage. The Judiciary is also an established digital-court leader — its eCourts electronic filing and case-management platform processes the large majority of court documents electronically, and the branch has moved aggressively into livestreaming and AI governance. For a Dell SLG seller, the relationship is enterprise IT for a statewide judicial branch, sold as a separate constitutional branch from the Executive (state CIO / NJOIT) and procured largely through the Judiciary's own solicitations on top of statewide vehicles.

Budget & Fiscal

The Judiciary is a constitutional branch with its own appropriation, not a line inside an executive department. For FY2026 the Governor's budget recommended roughly $928 million in Direct State Services for the Judiciary, plus federal funds (about $133.9 million tied to the Title IV-D child-support program). Treat these as the publicly reported appropriation envelope; the IT share is a slice of the branch total, not separately broken out in the public summary.

Initiatives & Priorities

eCourts digital case management & e-filing

eCourts is the Judiciary's electronic filing and case-management backbone, now handling the large majority of court documents electronically across civil, criminal, and other practice areas, with JEDS for self-represented and non-registered filers. An ongoing modernization track — storage, compute, database, and resilience under a statewide caseload.

Digital Court Case Management
AI governance — Committee on AI and the Courts

The Supreme Court's Committee on Artificial Intelligence and the Courts (convened 2023) produced preliminary attorney guidelines (Jan 2024) and principles for the Judiciary's own AI use emphasizing confidentiality and system security. Signals appetite for governed AI in case administration and public access — sell into the governance, security, and infrastructure layer.

AI Governance
Courtroom livestreaming & public access

The Judiciary began livestreaming Appellate Division oral arguments (Sept 2024, with limited exceptions per the March 2025 Notice) and posts briefs online; trial-level proceedings may be livestreamed at judicial discretion. A transparency push that drives demand for media, streaming, networking, and storage capacity.

Digital Court Transparency
Cybersecurity & data protection

As a statewide repository of sensitive case data, the Judiciary's stated AI and systems principles foreground confidentiality and the security of Judiciary systems. Court systems nationally are high-value ransomware targets, making resilience, backup/recovery, and endpoint/identity security a recurring infrastructure priority.
