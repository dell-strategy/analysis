Overview

The New Jersey Department of Law & Public Safety (LPS) is the state's chief law-enforcement and legal-affairs department, led by the Attorney General as New Jersey's top law-enforcement officer. It is one of the largest and most technology-intensive departments in state government, housing the New Jersey State Police, the Division of Criminal Justice, the Juvenile Justice Commission, the Division of Consumer Affairs, the Division on Civil Rights, the Office of the Insurance Fraud Prosecutor, and more. The department employs roughly 8,000 people and the Attorney General sets policy and standards for the 42,000-plus state and local law-enforcement officers across New Jersey. For a Dell SLG seller this is a high-value, multi-program account that spans field and mobile (patrol vehicles, in-car and body-worn systems, mobile data computers), secure data-center and storage (records management, criminal-justice data, digital evidence), real-time intelligence and analytics (the State Police Regional Operations & Intelligence Center / fusion center), forensics and biometrics, and cybersecurity. The account sits inside a fresh leadership reset: Attorney General Jennifer Davenport took office in January 2026 under Governor Mikie Sherrill and was confirmed by the State Senate in February 2026, so priorities and buying-center contacts are being re-established. Note that central IT, the state data center, and statewide cyber strategy run outside LPS at NJOIT, while NJCCIC (the state's cyber threat-sharing cell) sits with the Office of Homeland Security & Preparedness — LPS owns its own operational, CJIS-governed public-safety systems.

Budget & Fiscal

LPS is a large direct spender funded through the state budget, with the New Jersey State Police as the biggest cost center inside the department. Governor Sherrill signed her inaugural FY2027 budget (~$60.7B statewide) into law on June 30, 2026; the LPS appropriation is a line within it, and the Legislature's LPS budget analysis recommends about $515.6M in Direct State Services for the Division of State Police in FY2027 (up ~$1.6M over FY2026), funding roughly 4,668 State Police full-time positions. Initiatives & Priorities

Body-worn cameras & digital-evidence management

New Jersey requires patrol officers statewide to wear body cameras (AG directive), and the AG's Office administered roughly $58M in grants to equip nearly 500 agencies. The state deliberately chose not to build one central footage repository, leaving large, recurring needs for secure storage, retention, redaction, and digital-evidence management — a clear infrastructure and storage opening.

State Police CAD/RMS & mobile modernization

The State Police run statewide computer-aided dispatch (CAD), records management (RMS), and mobile data computers for field operations. These mission-critical, CJIS-governed systems are periodic modernization tracks that map to server, storage, resiliency, and ruggedized edge/mobile compute.

ROIC fusion center & real-time intelligence/AI

The NJ Regional Operations & Intelligence Center (ROIC) is the state's primary hub for information-sharing, threat analysis, and homeland-security intelligence, partnering with federal, state, and local agencies. Real-time data ingestion, analytics, and AI for threat detection map directly to high-performance compute, storage, and secure-data-center needs.

Forensics, biometrics & criminal-justice data

The State Police lab and the Division of Criminal Justice run forensic science, fingerprint/biometric identification, and criminal-history systems under CJIS rules. Modernizing these data-heavy, high-availability systems is an opening for secure storage, disaster recovery, and compute.

Procurement

LPS buys through New Jersey's central procurement framework, not primarily one-off agency RFPs. The contracting path runs through the Treasury Division of Purchase & Property (DPP) and the NJSTART eProcurement portal, with NJOIT shared services for IT and cooperative purchasing extending awards to local agencies. Two preconditions: an active, correctly categorized NJSTART vendor registration, and the right DPP statewide term contract for the offering. Anything touching criminal-justice data carries CJIS security obligations.
