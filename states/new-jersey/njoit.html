Overview

The New Jersey Office of Information Technology (NJOIT) is the state's central technology agency — under the 2007 Office of Information Technology Reorganization Act it is the exclusive government IT provider for New Jersey's Executive Branch, powering the business operations of dozens of departments and agencies in Trenton and across the state. NJOIT runs enterprise infrastructure (including the state data center), networking, cloud, cybersecurity, application development, and shared services, and sets statewide IT policy and standards; it employs 500+ technologists. For a technology seller, NJOIT is the central IT decision-maker and buying center for statewide infrastructure, while the contracting and budget mechanics run through a separate agency — the NJ Department of the Treasury's Division of Purchase & Property (DPP) and the NJSTART portal. The account sits inside a fresh leadership reset: Governor Mikie Sherrill took office in January 2026 and named Kevin Dehmer permanent Chief Technology Officer in February 2026, with an explicit mandate to modernize digital infrastructure, speed permitting and licensing, and strengthen cyber defenses against AI-driven threats.

Budget & Fiscal

NJOIT's leverage is operational rather than a single large appropriation line: it runs enterprise infrastructure as a shared service across the Executive Branch and recovers much of its delivery cost from the agencies it serves. The macro pool is the state budget — Governor Sherrill's first budget, an about-$60.7B FY2027 Appropriations Act, was signed at the fiscal-year start (June 30, 2026) — and a flagship NJOIT capital effort, the new Enterprise Data Center, is slated to come online in FY2027.

Initiatives & Priorities

Enterprise Data Center & infrastructure modernization

NJOIT is standing up a new Enterprise Data Center to provide 24/7 access to the IT services and applications state agencies and residents rely on, targeted for FY2027. Infrastructure refresh, hybrid cloud, and resilience are the core motion — a direct ISG and data-center play.

NJ AI Assistant & responsible-AI program

NJOIT, with the NJ Innovation Authority, runs the NJ AI Assistant — a secure genAI environment hosted on state infrastructure (not third-party training data). Since its July 2024 launch ~20,000 state employees have used it, with 1M+ prompts and 80%+ satisfaction by Feb 2026, and a 2026 upgrade. Signals real demand for AI-ready compute under state control.

Permitting / licensing efficiency & digital government

A named CTO priority: improve the efficiency and transparency of state permitting, adopt technology to clear licensure backlogs, and cut wait times. Modernization, automation, and managed-services plays should map to these stated efficiency goals.

Cybersecurity & NJCCIC

Statewide cyber runs through NJOIT and the NJ Cybersecurity & Communications Integration Cell (NJCCIC), with Dehmer's mandate to strengthen defenses against AI-driven threats. The security buying center sits with NJOIT — position secure infrastructure, identity, and resilience here.

Procurement

NJOIT is the IT decision-maker, but the contracting mechanics run through Treasury's Division of Purchase & Property (DPP) and the NJSTART portal, not one-off NJOIT RFPs in most cases. Two things to confirm first: an active, correctly scoped NJSTART vendor registration, and which statewide term contract(s) a Dell offering falls under. Cooperative purchasing lets local governments, schools, and authorities ride state awards.

Vehicle / gateWhat it means for Dell
NJSTART vendor registration (DPP)The mandatory front door — vendors register and maintain a NJSTART profile to bid, hold contracts, and invoice the state electronically. Confirm Dell's registration and categories are current.
Statewide term contracts (DPP)DPP awards master/term contracts agencies (and NJOIT) buy off without separate RFPs. IT-relevant examples include computer equipment & peripherals, cloud & software solutions, and IT consulting. Verify exact contract numbers/scope before forecasting.
Cooperative purchasing & national co-opsNJ's Cooperative Purchasing Program (N.J.A.C. 5:34) lets registered counties, municipalities, schools, and authorities ride state term contracts; national co-ops can supplement. Extends a single award across the broader NJ public sector.
