Georgia budgets annually on a July–June fiscal year. Governor Kemp signed the $38.5B FY2027 budget in May 2026, a fiscally cautious plan that directs agencies to withhold more than $300M in proposed new spending — a 'disciplined growth' signal sellers should price in. Three structural themes matter for SLG sellers. First, the Georgia Technology Authority (GTA) runs a centralized, security-first technology motion under State CIO Shawnzia Thomas, whose 2026 philosophy is explicitly 'AI Governance Is Security' — NIST controls applied to AI the same as any other system, with every agency required to publish an AI usage plan by Dec 31, 2026. Second, Georgia is deploying ~$1.3B in BEAD broadband funding through GTA, moving from planning into build-out. For Dell, the buying motion runs through GTA Direct and statewide contracts, and the security-first culture rewards governed, on-prem and private-cloud AI for sensitive workloads.

Budget & Fiscal

Georgia budgets on an annual July–June fiscal cycle, with an Amended FY (AFY) mid-year adjustment. The General Assembly passed and Governor Kemp signed a $38.5B budget for FY2027 in May 2026 — about 2% (~$738M) above current spending. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies, with GTA providing centralized infrastructure, security, and procurement services and named programs (broadband, AI) funded separately.

Initiatives & Policy

Statewide AI Governance Framework5

Scaling

GTA's Office of Artificial Intelligence runs a maturing framework: an AI Roadmap and Governance Framework, a Generative AI Responsible Use Standard, a Georgia Board for Artificial Intelligence, and a mandate that every agency publish a detailed AI usage plan by Dec 31, 2026. Governance is treated as security (NIST controls applied to AI). This is an opening for governed, auditable, private/on-prem AI on sensitive data rather than open public models.

Metro-Atlanta Data-Center Market8

Tax Breaks Intact

Metro Atlanta is a top-tier U.S. data-center hub (Microsoft, QTS, CoreSite, DataBank and others). The 2026 session (ended April 3) saw a flood of bills targeting data-center tax exemptions and energy costs (SB 410, SB 34, HB 1063), but none cleared both chambers — Georgia left its data-center sales-tax breaks intact despite an estimated $2.5B in 2026 forgone revenue. The policy cloud has lifted for now, keeping this the strongest Dell AI-infrastructure tailwind in the state, though energy/grid scrutiny and local moratoriums persist.

Procurement & Competition

The Georgia Technology Authority (GTA) is the state's central IT and cybersecurity agency. Most state technology buys run through GTA Direct — a managed-services path that gives agencies, local governments, colleges/universities, and boards of education access to IT services through pre-qualified, market-leading providers. For transactions over $25,000, GTA procurement uses solicitation types (RFQ, RFP, RFQC) prioritized by 'best value,' often establishing pools of pre-qualified contractors. GTA also endorses major agency technology procurements.
