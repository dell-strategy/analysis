Overview

DeKalb County is the fourth-most-populous county in Georgia, with roughly 760,000-770,000 residents across the eastern half of metropolitan Atlanta (county seat Decatur). It is one of Georgia's few counties governed by a strong, separately elected Chief Executive Officer rather than a county-manager model: Lorraine Cochran-Johnson took office in January 2025 as the county's first Black woman CEO, working alongside a seven-member Board of Commissioners. Central technology is consolidated in the Department of Innovation & Technology (DoIT), which supports 40-plus county departments with enterprise applications, infrastructure and cloud (Microsoft 365), strategic planning/procurement, and cybersecurity, with a workforce of roughly 110 IT professionals. For a Dell SLG seller, DeKalb is a large, urban county account in active modernization mode — recent ERP/HR/payroll platform work and a publicly stated cloud-migration push — but the dominant near-term theme is security: the county was hit by Lynx ransomware in October 2025 (with a resident data-breach notice in March 2026) and its Sheriff's Office/jail systems were struck by a separate ransomware attack in March 2026, putting cyber resilience, recovery, and infrastructure refresh at the center of the buying conversation.

Initiatives & Priorities

Cybersecurity recovery & resilience

After the October 2025 Lynx ransomware attack (resident breach notice issued March 2026) and a separate March 2026 ransomware hit on the Sheriff's Office/jail systems, DeKalb is rebuilding around threat monitoring, identity protection, incident response, and resilient system design. The clearest near-term buying center.

Infrastructure & cloud modernization

DoIT's Infrastructure & Support division runs server/cloud (Microsoft 365), networks, and data centers; the county has publicly highlighted cloud migration and was recognized for digital-innovation efforts. Hybrid infrastructure refresh and data-center modernization are open lanes.
