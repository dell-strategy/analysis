Overview

The Indiana Office of Technology (IOT) is the state's central IT agency, delivering shared infrastructure, networking, cloud, cybersecurity, and data services to dozens of executive-branch agencies from Indianapolis. IOT is best understood as a service organization rather than a large discretionary spender: it is a roughly 450-person agency with an operating budget reported around $250M, funded almost entirely through chargebacks — it bills the agencies it serves for the services they consume rather than drawing a large general-revenue appropriation. That model shapes the buying motion: most large agencies (Health, Transportation, Revenue, FSSA) keep their own IT teams and budgets, while IOT owns enterprise platforms, the state network, the security perimeter, and data services. Statewide commodity and IT purchasing is competed and held by the Indiana Department of Administration (IDOA) through Quantity Purchase Agreements (QPAs) that agencies — and many local governments — can buy from directly. Separately, Indiana has become a national data-center magnet, anchored by the LEAP Innovation and Research District in Lebanon, where Meta broke ground on a $10B+ AI data-center campus in February 2026 — context that frames Indiana's broader AI and infrastructure ambitions even though those private hyperscale builds sit outside IOT's own footprint.

Budget & Fiscal

IOT does not run on a large appropriation — it recovers its costs by billing the agencies it serves. The state's 2025–2027 biennial budget is roughly $46B (about $23.0B FY2026 / $23.2B FY2027), but IOT's own operating budget is reported around $250M, almost all of it chargeback-funded. A statewide fiscal squeeze is relevant: in 2025 the State Budget Agency ordered agencies to absorb a combined ~10% reduction (a 5% cut plus an additional 5% withholding) after a weak May 2025 revenue forecast.

Initiatives & Priorities

IN AI / human-centered AI adoption

Gov. Braun announced 'IN AI' on April 28, 2026 — a statewide push to make Indiana an AI-ready state, executed largely through the Central Indiana Corporate Partnership (CICP) for business adoption. Inside government, IOT has run a Microsoft Copilot pilot to evaluate productivity and data-protection. AI is a stated priority but governance and tooling are still maturing.

Cybersecurity & data protection

IOT owns the state's enterprise security perimeter under a long-tenured CISO and coordinates broadly through the Indiana Executive Council on Cybersecurity (IECC). Security remains a steady, board-level priority across endpoint, network, identity, and data-protection — a durable buying center.

Digital services & citizen experience

A new Chief Digital Experience Officer (2026) is leading state digital-service strategy, including the in.gov homepage chatbot as an early citizen-engagement tool. Modernizing front-door digital services is an emerging track.

LEAP district & data-center economy

Indiana's LEAP Innovation and Research District (Lebanon) anchors a wave of hyperscale AI data-center investment — Meta broke ground on a $10B+ campus in Feb 2026. These are private builds, not IOT projects, but they signal the state's AI-infrastructure direction and power/utility planning.
