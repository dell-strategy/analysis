Overview

The North Carolina Department of Administration (DOA) is the state's central business and administrative agency, providing the operational backbone of state government — property and construction, motor fleet, mail, surplus, and, most relevant to technology sellers, statewide procurement. Procurement runs through DOA's Division of Purchase & Contract (P&C), the central authority that establishes and manages statewide term contracts and sets purchasing rules for state agencies, with delegated authority extending to many local and education buyers. An important boundary to understand: P&C governs non-IT statewide term contracts, while IT statewide term contracts are owned by the NC Department of Information Technology (NCDIT), the state's central IT agency. For a hardware-and-infrastructure vendor, that means most technology buying flows through NCDIT vehicles, but DOA/P&C still shapes the surrounding procurement policy, the NC eProcurement system, and cooperative purchasing. The two agencies are actively collaborating in 2026 to streamline IT acquisition — so the DOA relationship is best understood as the procurement-policy and cooperative-purchasing context around the NCDIT technology motion, not the primary IT buying center itself.

Budget & Fiscal

DOA is an operational and procurement agency, not a large technology spender — its leverage is the contract and policy framework that purchases flow through, not its own appropriation. The bigger budget signal for a seller is the state's overall fiscal posture, which shifted sharply at the end of June 2026: after operating on the 2023 budget for more than 1,000 days, the General Assembly released and began passing a $34.4B biennial budget (FY2025–27), sending North Carolina's first full spending plan in over three years toward Gov. Stein. That unlocks agency appropriations — including technology and modernization line items — that had been frozen under continuation. Confirm the enacted final terms before forecasting.

Initiatives & Priorities

DOA–NCDIT procurement modernization

In June 2026 NCDIT named a Chief Procurement Transformation Officer (Shannon Casucci) and said it is collaborating with DOA to make IT acquisition more efficient, transparent, and customer-focused. This is the live procurement-reform track to watch — better processes and more interagency cooperation across both agencies.

Statewide AI governance (EO No. 24)

Gov. Stein's Sept 2025 Executive Order No. 24 stood up an AI Accelerator within NCDIT, an AI Leadership Council, and agency AI Oversight Teams. AI procurement and governance standards are forming now — a window for AI-infrastructure and responsible-AI positioning across state government.
