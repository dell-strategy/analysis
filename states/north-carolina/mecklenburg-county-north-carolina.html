Overview

Mecklenburg County is the most populous county in North Carolina, home to roughly 1.16 million residents and centered on Charlotte, the state's largest city and the Southeast's banking hub. It is a large, multi-department local government whose core mission is health and human services, public health, justice and criminal-justice services, tax and assessment, land use and environmental services, and park and recreation — services largely distinct from those the City of Charlotte provides. The County operates under a council-manager form of government: an elected Board of County Commissioners sets policy and adopts the budget, and a professional County Manager (Mike Bryant, sworn in June 30, 2025) runs day-to-day operations. Central technology is delivered by Information Technology Services (ITS), led by CIO Keith Gregg, which supplies enterprise infrastructure, application development, business-operations support, and IT security across County departments. For a Dell SLG seller, Mecklenburg is best understood as a single large local-government account, separate from the City of Charlotte, where ITS shapes technology direction but contracts are awarded centrally and material agreements must be approved by the elected Board of County Commissioners.

Budget & Fiscal

Mecklenburg County adopted a roughly $2.6B FY2027 operating budget on June 2, 2026 (effective July 1, 2026), about a 2% increase over FY2026's $2.54B, holding the property-tax rate at 49.27 cents per $100 of assessed valuation. The County does not publish a single headline IT figure, so technology spend is best tracked through the ITS department budget and capital/technology line items — and a large share of the topline funds Charlotte-Mecklenburg Schools, the Library, and Central Piedmont Community College rather than County operations, narrowing the directly technology-addressable pool.
