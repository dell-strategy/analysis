Overview

The Delaware Department of Technology & Information (DTI) is the state's central IT organization, chartered to deliver core technology services to other state organizations and to govern the technology direction and investments of the state. DTI operates the statewide network and security perimeter, enterprise infrastructure, cloud, and shared services that the Executive Branch agencies depend on, and it sets statewide IT policy and standards. It reports up through the Governor's administration — Governor Matt Meyer took office in January 2025. DTI is in the middle of a multi-year IT centralization effort: it has consolidated Executive-Branch agency IT under a shared-services chargeback model and stood up Secure End User Services (SEUS), a six-component enterprise stack (service desk, desktop, network & connectivity, voice, email & collaboration, and a security suite) with 24x7x365 support. For a Dell seller, DTI is both a direct buyer of infrastructure and end-user technology and the governance hub that shapes modernization demand across the executive branch, while contracting mechanics run through OMB's Government Support Services (GSS) and the MyMarketplace portal.

Budget & Fiscal

DTI's leverage is operational rather than a single large appropriation line: under IT centralization it runs enterprise infrastructure and end-user services as shared services and recovers much of its delivery cost from the agencies it serves via a chargeback model. Public top-line figures for the agency's own appropriation are limited; the macro pool is the state budget, and federal funds (ARPA, BEAD) underwrite specific modernization and broadband tracks.

Initiatives & Priorities

IT centralization & SEUS shared services

DTI is completing Executive-Branch IT centralization under a shared-services chargeback model, anchored by Secure End User Services (SEUS) — standard enterprise desktop, network & connectivity, voice, email/collaboration, service desk, and a security suite with 24x7x365 support. A direct end-user-compute and managed-services motion across agencies.

Modernization Shared Services End-User Compute
Generative AI policy & responsible-AI program

DTI issued an enterprise Generative AI policy governing use of consumer vs. enterprise GenAI tools (prohibiting public tools with confidential data and tools located outside the U.S.), and in March 2026 launched statewide "Responsible AI for Public Professionals" training for state employees. Delaware also has an AI Sandbox initiative (joint resolution, 2025) for high-impact sectors. Signals real demand for AI-ready, state-controlled compute.

Cybersecurity & state network defense

DTI owns statewide security standards, the state network perimeter, and incident response under the Chief Security Officer, plus a State & Local Cybersecurity Grant Program. The security buying center sits with DTI — position secure infrastructure, identity, and resilience here.

Mainframe & legacy modernization + broadband

DTI is modernizing mainframe and legacy systems (cloud-based grant-management replacement, improved availability, data integration, and cyber detection) while the Delaware Broadband Office runs BEAD to close the digital divide — construction expected to begin Fall 2026. Infrastructure refresh and hybrid-cloud plays map here.

Procurement

DTI is the central IT decision-maker and a direct buyer, but contracting mechanics largely run through OMB's Government Support Services (GSS) statewide contracts and the MyMarketplace portal, not one-off DTI RFPs in most cases. Two things to confirm first: that Dell is on the relevant GSS statewide and DTI-managed technology contracts, and that any cloud/AI offering meets DTI's security and GenAI-policy expectations before pursuing agency deals.
