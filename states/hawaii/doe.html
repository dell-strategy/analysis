Overview

The Hawaii State Department of Education (HIDOE) is unique in U.S. public education: it is the nation's only statewide single school district, governing every traditional public school across seven islands as a single organization rather than dozens of independent local districts. That structure means one superintendent, one board, and — critically for technology buyers — one centralized IT function. The Office of Information Technology Services (OITS), led by an assistant superintendent who reports through the deputy superintendent for operations, owns ed-tech, end-user devices, networks, data systems, and cybersecurity for roughly 257 schools and on the order of 165,000 students, plus a workforce of about 40,000. HIDOE leans heavily on Google's ecosystem (Workspace for Education, Chromebooks at scale, and Gemini/NotebookLM for staff AI), making it a fleet, connectivity, infrastructure, and security account more than a high-margin compute account. For a Dell SLG seller, the centralization is the opportunity: a single statewide decision center, a single procurement path, and a single set of standards — but also a single, deliberate buying motion that rewards being on the right contract vehicles early.

Budget & Fiscal

HIDOE is funded primarily by state general revenue, supplemented by federal funds and E-rate. Public reporting around the superintendent's 2025 reappointment cited an operating budget on the order of $2.8B; HIDOE's own materials have described an operating budget in the ~$2.1–2.2B range in recent fiscal years, with total revenue (all funds) higher. Treat the figures below as public approximations and confirm current-year appropriations against the enacted state budget.

Initiatives & Priorities

Statewide device fleet & 1:1 ecosystem

HIDOE operates a large, centrally standardized device fleet — heavily Chromebook/Google Workspace — across all islands. Refresh cycles, MDM/endpoint management, imaging, and asset lifecycle for ~165K students and ~40K staff are run as one program, not school-by-school.

Devices Endpoint
AI in education (Gemini / NotebookLM)

HIDOE has stood up an AI readiness program for staff and rolled out access to Google's Gemini and NotebookLM, plus an AI in Education Summit and an AI Readiness Toolkit. The motion is responsible-use and productivity first — frame AI as workflow and infrastructure enablement, not model spend.

AI Productivity
Cybersecurity & federal pilot funding

K-12 is a top ransomware and student-data target. The FCC Schools & Libraries Cybersecurity Pilot and E-rate cybersecurity-eligible services open funding for firewalls, endpoint protection, identity/MFA, and detection-and-response — a buying window for security-attached infrastructure.

Cybersecurity E-rate
Network & connectivity modernization

Connecting and securing schools across seven islands is a standing infrastructure challenge. WAN/LAN refresh, wireless, and resilient inter-island connectivity (E-rate Category One/Two eligible) underpin every other initiative.

Procurement

HIDOE buys through its own Procurement and Contracts Branch under Hawaii's centralized procurement code (HRS 103D / HAR Title 3), with solicitations posted on the state HIePRO eProcurement system. Two access paths matter: state cooperative purchasing under the State Procurement Office (SPO), and federal E-rate for connectivity and security. Confirm Dell's standing on the relevant SPO price/vendor lists and applicable cooperatives before pursuing a large refresh.
