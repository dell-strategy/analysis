South Dakota is a small state with an outsized cyber story and one of the most consolidated IT operating models in the country. The Bureau of Information & Technology (BIT) runs enterprise compute, data centers, networking, and security for nearly all of state government, higher ed, the constitutional offices, and the Unified Judicial System — meaning a handful of decisions in Pierre move the whole estate. The cyber center of gravity is Dakota State University in Madison: one of only a handful of schools holding all three NSA Centers of Academic Excellence designations, home to the Madison Cyber Labs (MadLabs) and a $90M, five-year research and workforce push, with a new Applied Research Lab opening in Sioux Falls in 2026. Governor Larry Rhoden has framed national security and defense — including cyber — as the state's 'next big industry,' and his FY2027 budget carries ~$10M for IT infrastructure plus a cloud-based cyber backup investment. For Dell, the two clearest angles are (1) the consolidated BIT estate — server refresh, data-center modernization, and security — and (2) the DSU cyber/AI research and applied-lab buildout.

Where Dell can play
  • Consolidated BIT estate refresh — server replacement, data-center modernization, and enterprise security across nearly all of state government from a single buyer.
  • Dakota State University cyber/AI buildout — Madison Cyber Labs and the Sioux Falls Applied Research Lab (opening 2026) need secure compute, lab infrastructure, and research-computing capacity.
  • Cloud-based cyber backup and resilience investment in the FY2027 budget — backup/recovery, secure storage, and redundancy.
  • AI and data modernization under a new CIO focused on improving state operations with AI and data.

Budget & Fiscal

South Dakota budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (FY runs July–June). Governor Rhoden's FY2027 proposal, delivered December 2, 2025, recommended roughly $7.46B in total funds with a ~$2.52B general fund; lawmakers adopted a budget of about $7.5B in March 2026 (a ~2.4% increase). There is no single consolidated 'state IT budget' line — technology spend runs through the Bureau of Information & Technology (BIT) and is distributed across agencies, but the FY2027 plan does call out targeted IT and cyber investments.

Initiatives & Policy

Dakota State University — Cyber & AI Powerhouse6

Expanding

DSU in Madison is a nationally recognized cyber school — one of only a handful holding all three NSA Centers of Academic Excellence designations (cyber defense, offense, research) and offering a PhD in Cyber Operations. The Madison Cyber Labs (MadLabs) is a 40,000-sq-ft hub with 15 labs, a secure (SCIF) facility, and a growing AI-and-cybersecurity graduate program. A $90M, five-year initiative (with NSA, state, and philanthropic backing) aims to double Beacom College graduates and stand up an Applied Research Lab in Sioux Falls opening in 2026. This is the strongest research-computing and secure-infrastructure account in the state.

Procurement & Competition

South Dakota technology procurement runs through two centralized authorities. The Bureau of Information & Technology (BIT) owns enterprise IT strategy, data centers, networking, and security for the state, while the Office of Procurement Management within the Bureau of Human Resources & Administration (formerly Bureau of Administration) is the central purchasing agency for goods and services, including NASPO ValuePoint cooperative contracts. The consolidated model means relatively few decision-makers control a broad estate.

Competitive landscape

South Dakota is a small, centralized market where relationships with BIT and the Office of Procurement Management matter more than volume. Differentiation comes from secure-infrastructure and research-computing fit (DSU), resilient backup/recovery for the funded cyber-backup priority, and a clean consolidated-refresh motion — rather than the scale plays that dominate larger states.
