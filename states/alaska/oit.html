Overview

The Alaska Office of Information Technology (OIT) is the state's consolidated central IT organization, sitting within the Department of Administration (DOA) and led by the State Chief Information Officer. Established by administrative order to centralize technology that had been scattered across departments, OIT operates Alaska's enterprise infrastructure — servers, licensing, networks, helpdesk, cloud platforms, and statewide applications such as myAlaska — and houses the state's cybersecurity organization under a Chief Information Security Officer. OIT sets statewide IT policy, standards, and the FY26-FY28 strategic plan, and embeds Chief Technology Officers and Department Technology Officers across agencies to deliver a 'one government' technology model. It is best understood as the enterprise relationship for selling into Alaska state government: OIT owns the central infrastructure and security direction, though large project funding and agency-specific buying still flow through the line departments and the state's budget process. Note that Alaska's marquee connectivity effort — the $1B-class BEAD broadband program — is administered separately by the Alaska Broadband Office under the Department of Commerce, not by OIT, so connectivity is context rather than an OIT procurement.

Budget & Fiscal

OIT is a centralized service organization funded largely through chargebacks and inter-agency receipts for the infrastructure, licensing, and support it delivers, rather than a single large general-fund line. Its FY2026 operating budget within the Department of Administration was roughly $64.6M, with the bulk in licensing, infrastructure, and servers. Initiatives & Priorities

Cloud migration & modernization

OIT's multi-phase 'move and modernize' effort has reached 1,100+ applications on Microsoft Azure over roughly 21 months without service disruption (per a June 2026 Microsoft case study), citing dynamic disaster and ransomware recovery and elastic capacity. The Permanent Fund Dividend system is being rebuilt cloud-native (targeted go-live 2026) and ~15,000 state employees now have M365 Copilot. A durable pull for hybrid-cloud, migration services, AI-ready platforms, and the on-prem footprint that anchors a hybrid estate.

Cloud Modernization Resilience
myAlaska + agentic AI

OIT issued a November 2025 RFI to embed 'agentic AI' into the myAlaska mobile app, with Microsoft AI feature integration in the FY26-FY28 plan — moving from a basic portal to autonomous, multi-step transactions that interact with legacy systems. Signals demand for AI-ready compute, data platforms, and integration services.

AI Digital Government
Mainframe & legacy modernization

The FY26-FY28 strategic plan includes a Mainframe Migration Project and statewide application lifecycle management. A multi-year track for migration, modern infrastructure, and the data platforms that replace legacy systems.

Modernization Data Center
Cybersecurity: Zero Trust, managed SOC & DLP

OIT's plan advances a Zero Trust roadmap, a managed Security Operations Center, and data loss prevention / data labeling governance under the State CISO. A centrally driven security theme spanning detection, identity, and resilience for state entities.

Procurement

Selling into Alaska state government runs on the Department of Administration's statewide contract vehicles and cooperative agreements, not one-off RFPs. The DOA Office of Procurement and Property Management (OPPM) owns statewide master agreements, and Alaska participates in NASPO ValuePoint (formerly WSCA-NASPO) cooperative contracts, including Cloud Solutions. Confirm Dell's coverage on the relevant Alaska statewide master agreements and NASPO ValuePoint vehicles first — it is the access path to far more than OIT alone, including agencies, the University of Alaska, boroughs, cities, and school districts.
