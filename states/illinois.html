Illinois enters FY2026 with the largest budget in its history (roughly $55.3B general funds) and a centralized technology operating model every SLG seller should know. First, the Department of Innovation & Technology (DoIT) runs a consolidated ~$1.1B enterprise IT shop for executive-branch agencies under new Secretary and State CIO Brandon Ragle (April 2025) — one front door for most state tech decisions. Second, Illinois has positioned itself as a national AI-policy leader: SB 315 (frontier-AI safety, effective Jan 1, 2027) plus a State Government AI Act mean agencies need governed, auditable AI. Third, a massive data-center buildout (201+ facilities, ~$15.8B invested) is colliding with grid and cost concerns — Pritzker is pausing new data-center tax incentives from July 1, 2026. For Dell, the buying motion runs through DoIT and the Chief Procurement Office via statewide master contracts and approved cooperatives (NASPO ValuePoint, Sourcewell, OMNIA), and a long-running statewide ERP modernization is reshaping back-office and security spend.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure demand from the University of Illinois / NCSA research-computing ecosystem (DeltaAI, NVIDIA GH200) and from agency AI adoption under new governance rules.
  • Statewide ERP modernization and the cybersecurity hardening attached to it — secure compute, storage, and lifecycle across consolidated DoIT systems.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across a record budget and a centralized device-buying motion (JPMC device master contracts).
  • Governed private / on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as Illinois operationalizes SB 315 and the State Government AI Act.

Budget & Fiscal

Illinois budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1 – June 30). The FY2026 enacted budget is roughly $55.3B in general-funds revenue against ~$55.1B in spending — the largest in state history, with a small projected surplus. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; most executive-branch technology spend is consolidated under DoIT's ~$1.1B enterprise budget, with additional technology and security dollars distributed across agencies and programs.

Initiatives & Policy

SB 315 — Frontier AI Safety Act5

Passed legislature; awaiting signature (effective Jan 1, 2027)

Illinois became the first state to mandate independent third-party safety audits of frontier AI developers, requiring annual safety frameworks, pre-deployment transparency, incident reporting, and whistleblower protections. Paired with the State Government AI Act, it signals a governed, auditable posture — an opening to lead with private/on-prem AI for sensitive state data rather than open public models.

Data-Center Buildout & Incentive Pause8

Pause from July 1, 2026

Illinois hosts 201+ data centers with ~$15.8B invested, driven by the Data Centers Investment Program incentives. Citing grid load, cost, and water concerns, Pritzker is pausing NEW data-center tax incentives starting July 1, 2026 (existing agreements honored). The infrastructure tailwind remains strong, but new greenfield economics shift — position around existing campuses, enterprise modernization, and research computing rather than net-new incentive plays.

Procurement & Competition

Illinois technology procurement is split between policy/operations and contracting authority. DoIT sets enterprise technology direction and operations for executive-branch agencies, while the independent Chief Procurement Office (CPO) and Central Management Services (CMS) run the contracting machinery. State agencies must first consider their own procurement or an available statewide master contract; BidBuy is the primary sourcing system, and a set of approved cooperatives extends pre-competed buying.

Competitive landscape

Illinois is a centralized, contract-driven market with strong incumbents on statewide master contracts and cooperatives. Differentiation comes from AI-infrastructure capability (research computing, governed on-prem AI), ERP/modernization-aligned security and compute, and correctly scoped cooperative coverage — not price alone.
