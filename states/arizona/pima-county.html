Overview

Pima County is the second most populous county in Arizona, with roughly 1.06 million residents centered on the Tucson metro area and stretching across a large desert and border region of southern Arizona. It is a full-service local government spanning public health, the Sheriff and justice system, transportation and regional flood control, public works, parks, and community services. Central technology is delivered by the Information Technology Department (ITD), which designs, implements, manages, and supports the County's information, network, communication, and application systems — including enterprise infrastructure, GIS, data management and analytics, collaboration tools, and information security. For a Dell SLG seller, Pima is best understood as a single mid-to-large local-government account where technology direction is concentrated in ITD but purchasing authority is centralized: contracts are run through the County's Procurement Department under the Pima County Procurement Code, and larger or longer-term awards must be approved by the elected Board of Supervisors. ITD sits operationally under a Deputy County Administrator, so the buying center spans the IT Director/CIO, the Procurement Director, and the County Administrator's office.

Budget & Fiscal

Pima County's Board of Supervisors adopted its final FY2027 budget on June 23, 2026 — roughly $1.81 billion in expenditures (about a 3.3% increase over FY2026), on ~$1.89B in total revenues. The County frames the plan around fiscal stability amid state cost-shifts and federal funding uncertainty, with priorities set by its Strategic Plan and the Prosperity and One Pima initiatives. Notable technology items include a $20M 'Middle Mile' broadband project extending fiber to underserved rural areas and $250K to add two ITD staff for federal/state vendor auditing. ITD spending is otherwise not a single visible line item — it is funded across the departments ITD serves — so demand should be mapped to the operating departments as well as to central IT.

Initiatives & Priorities

Data modernization and the 'Data Works' partnership

ITD has stood up a 'Data Works' model pairing the County IT Analytics and Data Governance team with department data teams (starting with Public Health under a federal PHIG grant), centered on a Snowflake data warehouse, workflow automation, and data governance. A clear opening for data-platform infrastructure, analytics, and governed-data conversations.

Enterprise infrastructure and continuity

ITD owns network, hardware, and software systems for the County and emphasizes continuity of operations with redundancy and security across critical systems. This is the natural home for ISG (compute/storage), hybrid-cloud, and disaster-recovery / business-continuity refresh.

GIS and data-driven services

Procurement

Pima County buys under its own Procurement Code (Title 11) through the County Procurement Department. The defining gate for a seller: contracts of five years or longer, and IGAs / grant awards / professional-services agreements over $100,000, must be awarded by the elected Board of Supervisors; the Procurement Director signs or prepares contracts for Board approval by value. Cooperative purchasing is explicitly authorized, so confirm which pre-competed vehicle gives Dell the cleanest path before pursuing a one-off solicitation.
