Overview

The City of Phoenix is the government of the fifth-largest city in the United States and the capital of Arizona, serving roughly 1.6 million residents across one of the nation's fastest-growing metros. City technology is run by the Information Technology Services Department (ITS), which delivers citywide IT under a federated model — supporting major operating departments including Phoenix Sky Harbor International Airport, Water Services, Police, and Fire alongside general-government services. The city's FY2026-27 budget is balanced, built on a General Fund of about $2.29 billion and supported by a preliminary five-year Capital Improvement Program (CIP) totaling $11.4 billion. Phoenix is a recognized smart-city and responsible-AI adopter: it operates an Office of Innovation, has published a Code of Conduct for the responsible use of generative AI, and has run AI-enabled pilots spanning traffic-signal optimization, water-system monitoring, and energy-efficient building systems. For a technology seller this is a large, growth-driven municipal IT buyer with stable IT leadership (CIO Steen Hambric), a newer CISO (Mitch Kohlbecker, 2025), and a returning City Manager (Ed Zuercher, 2025). Phoenix buys through its own central procurement — vendor registration via procurePHX and solicitations through its OpenGov-based Procurement Portal — and leverages cooperative purchasing under its city code.

Budget & Fiscal

Phoenix adopted a balanced FY2026-27 budget anchored by a General Fund of roughly $2.29 billion (up about 4.5% year over year), with a one-time surplus of about $162.5M directed to priorities such as housing, homelessness services, child-care affordability, and employee compensation. Technology spending is embedded across ITS operations and the capital program rather than reported as a single headline line — the preliminary five-year CIP totals $11.4 billion, with the first year alone at about $2.7 billion.

Initiatives & Priorities

Responsible / generative AI program

Phoenix has published a Code of Conduct for the Responsible Use of Generative AI and routes approved Gen AI uses through a technical review and committee-approval process. AI is being applied operationally — including an AI-assisted traffic-signal pilot across roughly 30 intersections and a Water Services pilot using AI sensors to monitor sewer pollutants. A live lane for governed AI infrastructure, data platforms, and use-case enablement.

AI Innovation
Smart-city and Office of Innovation pilots

The Office of Innovation (stood up in late 2021) co-creates pilots to modernize city processes, and Phoenix has been recognized as an IDC Smart Cities North America Awards finalist (2025). Work spans connected infrastructure, resident-engagement, and sustainability use cases — a recurring entry point for IoT, edge, and data-platform conversations.

Federated IT modernization across major departments

ITS supports a federated model spanning Sky Harbor Airport, Water Services, Police, and Fire. Infrastructure refresh, network, end-user compute, and managed services demand is distributed across these large operating departments as well as central ITS — position ISG and services against department-level capital, not only the central budget.

Active appetite for endpoint, network security, identity, and risk-visibility tooling across a large, multi-department estate.
