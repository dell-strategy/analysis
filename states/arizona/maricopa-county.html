Overview

Maricopa County is the most populous county in Arizona and the fourth most populous in the United States, with roughly 4.5 million residents across the Phoenix metro area. It is a large, multi-department government — more than 50 departments and over 13,000 employees — spanning public safety, courts, elections, public health, transportation, and environmental services. Central technology is delivered by the Office of Enterprise Technology (OET), which the County now publicly brands as Enterprise Technology & Innovation (ETI); it is the County's central enterprise technology provider, supplying infrastructure, application support, information security, GIS, and IT advisory services to the operating departments. For a Dell SLG seller, Maricopa is best understood as a single very large local-government account where the spending decisions are concentrated: OET/ETI shapes the technology direction, but contracts are awarded centrally through the Office of Procurement Services and must be approved by the elected Board of Supervisors. Public-safety functions (Sheriff, County Attorney, detention, courts) dominate the budget, and elections technology is an unusually high-profile workload given the County's national visibility.

Budget & Fiscal

Maricopa County adopted a FY2026 budget of roughly $3.9 billion. Public safety is the single largest claim on it (about half), and a large share of the General Fund is consumed by state-mandated costs — so the discretionary, technology-addressable pool is a fraction of the headline number. The County slightly reduced its primary property tax rate, signaling a cost-discipline posture rather than expansion.

Initiatives & Priorities

AI governance and adoption

OET/ETI has stood up a countywide AI usage policy, end-user training, and an AI subcommittee, and is adopting chatbots and productivity aids while managing security and data-privacy risk. An AI Governance Report (Oct 2025) tracks corrective actions. Lead with governed, secure AI infrastructure rather than point tools.

Elections technology and the new Tabulation & Election Center

A new ~260,000-sq-ft state-of-the-art Tabulation and Election Center is under construction (groundbreaking 2025; opening Summer 2027, operational for the 2028 cycle), set to more than double tabulation capacity. A high-visibility, security-sensitive workload with infrastructure and endpoint implications. Note: elections IT control is actively contested — a 2026 court fight between the Board of Supervisors and Recorder Justin Heap over who controls elections IT systems, servers, and staff remains unresolved on appeal, so confirm the current buying center before pursuing elections-tech deals.

Procurement

Maricopa County buys under its own Procurement Code (adopted under A.R.S. 41-2501) through the Office of Procurement Services. The defining feature for a seller: contracts and contract changes must be approved by the elected Board of Supervisors. Cooperative purchasing is explicitly authorized, so confirm which vehicle gives Dell the cleanest, pre-competed path before pursuing a one-off solicitation.
