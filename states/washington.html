Washington enters 2026 with two forces pulling in opposite directions. On one side, a record $77.9B 2025–27 operating budget (signed May 2025) plus a roughly $1.2B BEAD broadband buildout and the densest data-center corridor in the Northwest are real tailwinds. On the other, the state is in genuine fiscal stress: Ferguson's December 2025 supplemental closed a $2.3B shortfall with cuts and reserves, and in June 2026 OFM warned agencies to prepare for further 'significant' shortfalls — discretionary IT spend will be scrutinized. The buying motion runs through WaTech (Washington Technology Solutions) for enterprise services and strategy, and through DES master contracts (including NASPO ValuePoint) for hardware and services. AI governance is maturing fast: WaTech's generative-AI guidelines became statewide IT policy in December 2025, and the AG-led AI Task Force delivers its final report July 1, 2026. For Dell, the message is governed modernization on a budget — refresh, consolidation, and AI-ready infrastructure that pencils out against a deficit.

Where Dell can play
  • Agency modernization and lifecycle refresh under WaTech's 2025–2028 IT Strategic Plan and Enterprise Shared Tenant onboarding (endpoint, server, storage).
  • AI-ready, governed infrastructure as agencies operationalize WaTech's generative-AI policy and the AI Task Force's NIST AI RMF recommendations — private/on-prem AI for sensitive data.
  • Cybersecurity infrastructure and managed services tied to the Office of Cybersecurity (OCS) and the State & Local Cybersecurity Grant Program.
  • Higher-ed and research-computing demand at the University of Washington and WSU; plus the Quincy/Central Washington data-center corridor.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with TCO and consolidation: tie Dell refresh, server, and storage to WaTech's enterprise-first strategy and Enterprise Shared Tenant onboarding in a budget-constrained year.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as agencies operationalize WaTech's AI policy and the AI Task Force's NIST AI RMF recommendations.
  • Map cybersecurity offerings to OCS priorities and the State & Local Cybersecurity Grant Program so local-gov deals ride federal grant dollars.
  • Validate Dell's DES master-contract and NASPO ValuePoint coverage and resellers are current and correctly scoped for state, local, K-12, and higher-ed buyers.
  • Engage WaTech and OFM early on gated IT projects, and pursue UW/WSU research-computing and the Central WA data-center corridor for AI-infrastructure demand.

Budget & Fiscal

Washington budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle running July–June, with supplemental adjustments in the off-year. The 2025–27 operating budget (SB 5167) is $77.9B; the capital budget is $7.3B. There is no single 'state IT budget' line — technology spend is distributed across agencies and reported in OFM's IT budget detail, with major projects subject to WaTech/OFM gated-funding oversight. The fiscal backdrop matters: a $2.3B shortfall was closed in the December 2025 supplemental, and OFM has warned of further shortfalls in the 2026 session.

Procurement & Competition

Washington splits its technology motion between two central bodies. WaTech (Washington Technology Solutions) is the enterprise IT authority — it sets statewide strategy and policy, runs core services (network, data center, Azure, Enterprise Shared Tenant), and provides gated oversight of major IT projects with OFM. The Department of Enterprise Services (DES) runs statewide master contracts for goods and services, including cooperative agreements (NASPO ValuePoint, Sourcewell) that more than 1,800 state, local, tribal, K-12, and higher-ed purchasers can use.

Competitive landscape

Washington is a Microsoft-anchored, contract-driven market — Azure and the Enterprise Shared Tenant shape the cloud baseline, and incumbents hold DES master contracts across compute, networking, and services. With the state under fiscal pressure, differentiation comes from consolidation/TCO, governed AI-ready infrastructure, and security-grant-aligned offerings rather than price alone.
