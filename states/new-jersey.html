New Jersey enters mid-2026 under a new administration with a clear technology identity. Governor Mikie Sherrill (D), sworn in January 20, 2026 as the state's 57th governor, inherits the NJ AI Hub — a Princeton-anchored ecosystem with CoreWeave and Microsoft as founding partners that aims to make New Jersey the East Coast's leading AI center. The state runs on a single-year fiscal cycle; the FY2026 budget signed by former Governor Murphy totals $58.78B. Three shifts matter for SLG sellers: the AI Hub and surrounding 'AI corridor' along Route 1 are pulling in compute and talent; Sherrill has unveiled a first-in-the-nation framework to regulate AI data centers (making operators fund their own power and grid upgrades) amid ~20% electric-bill increases driven largely by data-center demand; and central IT leadership has settled under new CTO Kevin Dehmer (who succeeded the retired Chris Rein), with NJOIT (50+ departments) and the NJCCIC cyber fusion center as the key statewide buyers. For Dell, the AI-infrastructure angle is the headline, balanced against an energy-and-governance climate that rewards efficient, governed compute.

Budget & Fiscal

New Jersey budgets on an annual (single-year) cycle running July 1 to June 30. The FY2026 Appropriations Act, signed by then-Governor Murphy on June 30, 2025, appropriates $58.78B in State funds (plus roughly $31B in federal funds). There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is distributed across agencies and is centrally coordinated through the Office of Information Technology (NJOIT), with cyber funded through Homeland Security / the NJCCIC. Governor Sherrill delivered her first budget address in early 2026 emphasizing affordability, energy, and accountability for Big Tech and data centers.

Initiatives & Policy

NJ AI Hub2

Operational / scaling

Announced in January 2025 by then-Governor Murphy and Princeton President Eisgruber, with Microsoft and CoreWeave as founding partners and $72M+ committed across the state (NJEDA), Princeton, and industry. Located at 619 Alexander Road in West Windsor along the Route 1 corridor, the Hub advances three pillars: AI research, commercialization/acceleration, and AI education/workforce. In December 2025 it selected Plug and Play to operate a regional AI Accelerator. The strongest tailwind in the state for Dell AI-infrastructure and research-computing demand.

AI Data Center Regulatory Framework7

Proposed (June 2026)

Governor Sherrill in June 2026 unveiled what her administration calls the country's first comprehensive statewide framework for regulating AI data centers. The four-part plan would make operators fund their own energy supply and grid upgrades, publicly report energy/water use, negotiate community benefit agreements, and use union labor — shifting costs off ratepayers. Related bills (e.g., S680) tie clean-energy mandates and large-load tariffs to data centers. The climate rewards efficient, governed, lower-power compute and creates uncertainty for raw hyperscale buildout.

NJCCIC & Civilian Cyber Resilience Corps5

Active / expanding

The New Jersey Cybersecurity and Communications Integration Cell (NJCCIC), the state's cyber fusion center within Homeland Security, published a new 2026–2030 strategic plan and in December 2025 launched the Civilian Cyber Resilience Corps — a statewide volunteer initiative to help state, local, and critical-infrastructure entities prevent, withstand, and recover from attacks. Drives demand for secure infrastructure, endpoint protection, and managed services across state and local government.

Enterprise GenAI for State Government6

In production

Under EO 346 (2023) New Jersey stood up an AI Task Force and directed NJOIT to govern state AI use. The Office of Innovation launched the NJ AI Assistant — a safe 'sandbox' GenAI platform for state employees — and one of the nation's first comprehensive GenAI training programs. As agencies move from pilot to scale, governed and auditable AI on sensitive data is an opening for private/on-prem compute rather than open public models.

Procurement & Competition

New Jersey centralizes IT direction in the Office of Information Technology (NJOIT), which oversees infrastructure for 50+ state departments and agencies, while purchasing is run by the Department of the Treasury's Division of Purchase & Property (DPP) through the NJSTART eProcurement marketplace. Most technology buys run through DPP statewide contracts and NASPO ValuePoint cooperative agreements (including Cloud Solutions via a Carahsoft participating addendum).

Competitive landscape

New Jersey is a contract-driven, cooperative-friendly market where incumbents hold NJSTART and NASPO agreements across compute, networking, and services. Differentiation comes from AI-infrastructure capability tied to the AI Hub and research universities, energy-efficient/governed compute that aligns with the state's data-center scrutiny, and cyber-ready offerings mapped to NJCCIC — more than on price alone.
