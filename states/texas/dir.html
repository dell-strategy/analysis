Overview

The Texas Department of Information Resources (DIR) is the state's central IT agency — it sets statewide technology policy, owns the procurement vehicles most Texas public entities buy through, and delivers shared services (data center, cloud, network, security). DIR is best understood as the conduit for Texas public-sector technology rather than a large end spender: the DIR Cooperative Contracts program drove more than $3.4B in customer purchases over the last three fiscal years, and eligible state agencies, local governments, K-12, and higher-ed can buy off those pre-competed contracts without running their own RFP. DIR also operates Shared Technology Services (STS) — consolidated data-center and cloud capacity — and enforces TX-RAMP, the cloud-security certification gate. One structural change is in motion: cybersecurity functions are migrating out of DIR into the new Texas Cyber Command, a transition slated to complete by December 31, 2026.

Budget & Fiscal

DIR isn't a large direct spender — its leverage shows up as the volume flowing through its vehicles rather than its own appropriation. The state's 2026–27 biennial budget is $337.4B (largest in Texas history), and DIR's Cooperative Contracts program moved $3.4B+ in customer purchases over the last three fiscal years.
