Overview

The Texas Department of Family & Protective Services (DFPS) runs the state's Child Protective Investigations, Child Protective Services, Adult Protective Services, and Statewide Intake (the abuse/neglect hotline and online reporting). It is one of the most data- and case-management-intensive agencies in Texas: caseworkers, investigators, and external Community-Based Care contractors all transact through IMPACT, the statewide case-management system whose core dates back decades. DFPS is mid-leadership-transition and mid-modernization at once — Audrey O'Neill became Commissioner in September 2025, and the agency's strategic plan, IMPACT replacement effort, and IT roadmap all push toward modern case management, stronger data governance, AI tooling, and tighter cybersecurity. For a Dell SLG seller, DFPS is a mission-driven, federally-co-funded modernization account where the buying centers are the IT division (infrastructure, end-user compute, data) and the IMPACT replacement program, reached primarily through DIR Cooperative Contracts and TX-RAMP-cleared cloud.

Budget & Fiscal

DFPS is a large human-services agency: its FY2026-27 baseline request totaled roughly $4.6B in all funds (~$3.1B state). Most of that is direct services and foster care, but the agency also funds a sizable internal IT operation and a multi-year case-management replacement that draws on both state appropriations and federal reimbursement.

Initiatives & Priorities

IMPACT case-management replacement

DFPS is planning a multi-year replacement of its legacy IMPACT case-management system with a federally compliant, modern platform, paired with business-process redesign and better data sharing with Community-Based Care contractors. Planning is in FY2026-27; build is projected for ~FY2028-2032. The infrastructure, data, and integration layers underneath are the seller-relevant surface.

Case Management Modernization
AI tooling & data governance

The 2027-2031 strategic plan (approved June 2026) commits to integrating AI tools and strengthening data governance for accurate, secure, responsible data use. DFPS IT leadership has publicly floated an AI model usable across state agencies — an opening for AI-ready infrastructure and data-platform conversations.

Cybersecurity & continuity

The strategic plan calls out strengthening cybersecurity safeguards, mitigating operational risk, and ensuring continuity of operations — relevant given the sensitivity of child-welfare and APS case data. CISO-owned program; aligns with statewide TX-RAMP and security standards.

Legacy debt & digital intake

DFPS is modernizing applications dating to the 1980s-90s toward mobile and cloud-capable experiences, and expanding online/Statewide Intake reporting channels. Continual transformation of technical debt is the CIO's stated strategy — steady infrastructure-refresh and end-user-compute demand.

Infrastructure Modernization

Procurement

DFPS buys IT primarily through the statewide vehicles rather than running everything as standalone RFPs. The fastest paths are DIR Cooperative Contracts (where Dell can sell hardware, software, and services without the agency re-competing) and agency solicitations for larger programs like the IMPACT replacement. Any cloud or as-a-service offering touching DFPS case data must clear TX-RAMP first.
