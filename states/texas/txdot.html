Overview

The Texas Department of Transportation (TxDOT) plans, builds, operates, and maintains the state highway system — one of the largest transportation networks in the country, spanning roughly 80,000 centerline miles across 25 districts. Headquartered in Austin, it is a heavy edge and field organization: traffic management centers, roadside ITS and sensor infrastructure, signals, cameras, connected-vehicle pilots, and a large back-office data and applications estate. Its capital program runs through the annually updated Unified Transportation Program (UTP), a 10-year 'road map' that the draft 2026 edition put at roughly $101.6B — the third straight year above $100B. On the technology side, TxDOT's Information Technology Division supports more than 700 IT professionals and the agency has moved aggressively on AI: its AI Strategic Plan, launched December 2024 and updated in February 2026, frames AI as a 'force multiplier' embedded in workflows under a 'human-led, AI-supported' governance model. For a Dell SLG seller, TxDOT is an infrastructure-, edge-, and data-rich account whose buying runs through TxDOT's own procurement plus DIR Cooperative Contracts, gated by TX-RAMP and TxDOT's internal IS-RAMP requirements.

Budget & Fiscal

TxDOT's scale shows up in its capital program, not a small IT line. The draft 2026 UTP was about $101.6B over 10 years (roughly $7.5B of it new funding), and Governor Abbott has separately framed a broader transportation investment of over $146B. Embedded in the capital plan is a meaningful traffic-technology / ITS component — the kind of sensor, edge, and data infrastructure a compute and storage vendor maps to.

Initiatives & Priorities

AI Strategic Plan — pilots to operations

Launched Dec 2024 and updated Feb 2026, TxDOT is moving AI from isolated pilots into everyday operations under a 'human-led, AI-supported' model. It has evaluated 200+ use cases via a Readiness Scorecard, with live examples like AI incident detection in Austin. Strong fit for AI-infrastructure, data-platform, and edge-compute conversations.

AI Data Edge
Connected & autonomous vehicles / ITS

TxDOT runs a Connected and Autonomous Vehicle Task Force and a growing intelligent-transportation-systems footprint — roadside sensors, traffic management centers, signal tech, and connected-vehicle pilots. The draft 2026 UTP earmarks $264M+ in new traffic-management technology. This is an edge, sensor-data, and real-time-processing buildout.

Innovation & testing platforms

Programs such as the Texas Innovation Alliance (analytics and AI for smart transportation) and proposed neutral testing/proving-ground concepts position TxDOT as a public-private innovation buyer — a route for piloting compute, data, and AI solutions before scaling.

Innovation Analytics
Cybersecurity & data protection (IS-RAMP)

TxDOT operates its own Information Security Risk and Authorization Management Program (IS-RAMP) and a controls-standards catalog, with a CISO who reviews and approves technology proposals. Security and TX-RAMP posture is a gate to clear on any data-touching deal.

Procurement

TxDOT buys both through its own agency procurement and through statewide vehicles. Two early checks for any technology play: that Dell's DIR Cooperative Contracts are active and correctly scoped for TxDOT, and that any cloud / as-a-service offering meets TX-RAMP and TxDOT's internal IS-RAMP security requirements.
