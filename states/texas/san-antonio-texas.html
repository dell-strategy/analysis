Overview

The City of San Antonio is the government of the 7th-largest city in the United States, serving roughly 1.5 million residents and operating an adopted FY2026 budget of about $4 billion. City technology is run by the Information Technology Services Department (ITSD), which provides IT services across more than 40 departments and 500-plus city facilities — spanning cybersecurity, network infrastructure, enterprise applications, end-user support, public-safety systems, data analytics, smart-city strategy, and digital transformation. San Antonio is widely branded 'Cyber City USA': it holds the largest concentration of cyber assets in the country outside the Washington, D.C. region, home to NSA Texas (about 3,000 employees), the 16th Air Force (AFCYBER) at Joint Base San Antonio, and now the new Texas Cyber Command — the nation's largest state-run cybersecurity agency, headquartered in San Antonio at UT San Antonio. For a technology seller this is a large municipal IT buyer embedded in a dense federal/state cyber ecosystem, with fresh IT and security leadership in 2026 (a new CIO and CISO) and a city budget that put $130M+ behind technology. San Antonio buys through its own central purchasing process and cooperative vehicles, including the Texas DIR Cooperative Contracts.

Budget & Fiscal

San Antonio adopted a balanced ~$4B FY2026 budget that allocates more than $130M to technology-related initiatives. The Information Technology Services Department (ITSD) itself is funded at about $94M, with additional technology dollars flowing through the capital improvement program and the Office of Innovation rather than ITSD alone.

Procurement

San Antonio buys two main ways, and a seller should confirm both. It runs its own central purchasing process (formal/informal solicitations, vendor registration) and it uses cooperative contracts — including the Texas DIR Cooperative Contracts — to buy technology at pre-negotiated statewide pricing without running a separate RFP.
