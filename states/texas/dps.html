Overview

The Texas Department of Public Safety (DPS) is the state's primary law-enforcement and homeland-security agency. It runs the Texas Rangers, the Texas Highway Patrol, the statewide driver license and ID program, crime records and forensics, border-security operations, and the Texas Fusion Center — the 24/7 intelligence hub that serves as the state repository for homeland-security information. With roughly 11,000 commissioned and non-commissioned personnel, DPS combines a heavy mobile and field-operations profile (patrol vehicles, in-car and body-worn systems, mobile data) with substantial back-office data-center, records, and biometrics systems. Its technology estate spans the Multimodal Biometric Identification System (MBIS) for fingerprint, mugshot, and iris matching; the driver license issuance and online-services platforms (including the MyTexas app); CJIS-governed criminal-justice data; and real-time intelligence/analytics for the fusion center. For a Dell SLG seller, DPS is a high-value account that touches edge/mobile, secure data center, storage, biometrics, AI/analytics, and cybersecurity — most of it procurable through DIR Cooperative Contracts and gated by TX-RAMP and CJIS requirements.

Budget & Fiscal

DPS is a large direct spender. The FY2026–27 biennial budget (SB 1, 89th Legislature) funds DPS at roughly $3.8B, with an IT capital line of about $134M and record continued border-security funding driving much of the field, surveillance, and intelligence spend.

Initiatives & Priorities

MBIS biometrics modernization (cloud)

DPS's Multimodal Biometric Identification System matches fingerprints, mugshots, and irises for arrest and applicant submissions. DPS has solicited cloud-based maintenance, support, disaster recovery, and enhancement services — a clear infrastructure, storage, and resiliency opening tied to a mission-critical system.

Driver license modernization & digital services

DPS rolled out a new, harder-to-counterfeit DL/ID card design (Aug 2025) and expanded online application, renewal, and replacement via the MyTexas app. Texas has not yet launched a mobile/digital driver license, an area of active national expansion and a likely future modernization track.

Texas Fusion Center & real-time intelligence

The fusion center's Real-Time Watch Center operates 24/7 as the state's homeland-security information hub, consolidating threat and suspicious-activity data across federal, state, and local partners. Real-time data ingestion, analytics, and AI for threat detection map directly to high-performance compute, storage, and edge needs.

Procurement

DPS buys both through statewide vehicles and via its own agency solicitations. The most efficient path for IT and infrastructure is DIR Cooperative Contracts; larger or specialized systems (e.g., MBIS, driver license platforms) run as agency RFOs/RFPs. Any cloud or as-a-service offering must clear TX-RAMP, and criminal-justice data carries CJIS security obligations.
