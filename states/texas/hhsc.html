Overview

The Texas Health & Human Services Commission (HHSC) is the state's largest health-and-human-services agency — it administers Medicaid and CHIP for more than four million Texans, runs SNAP and TANF eligibility, operates state psychiatric and supported-living facilities, and regulates much of the state's health-care sector. Its 2026–27 all-funds appropriation is roughly $94B (about a quarter of the entire state budget), with Medicaid client services the dominant line. For a technology seller, the relevant story is the application estate behind that scale: HHSC has cited roughly a $2B IT footprint, 488 client-critical applications (about 128 of them legacy), and a workforce north of 50,000. A multi-year modernization push — eligibility-system overhaul, cloud migration, data, AI, and cybersecurity — is the through-line, much of it forced by federal scrutiny over Medicaid and SNAP processing delays. HHSC buys both through DIR Cooperative Contracts and via its own large competitive procurements, and the agency is under new executive leadership as of January 2026.

Budget & Fiscal

HHSC is genuinely large as both a program agency and an IT spender — unlike DIR, it owns a big direct technology budget. Its 2026–27 all-funds appropriation is roughly $94B, with Medicaid the dominant driver; the agency has separately cited an IT footprint of about $2B and requested significant additional dollars for eligibility modernization and staffing.

Initiatives & Priorities

TIERS eligibility-system modernization

HHSC is overhauling the Texas Integrated Eligibility Redesign System (TIERS) — the platform that determines Medicaid, SNAP, and TANF eligibility — including a phased migration of dozens of applications and services into the cloud (AWS). Goals: cut contract fees, improve security and data capability, and add disaster-scale elasticity. The single biggest modernization vehicle to track.

Eligibility Cloud Modernization
Legacy application modernization

With ~128 of 488 client-critical applications classified as legacy, HHSC is pursuing cloud adoption, automated/AI-assisted app development, and re-platforming across the estate. Infrastructure refresh, hybrid cloud, and managed services all have a lane here.

Modernization Infrastructure
AI and data

HHSC stood up a Chief AI Officer / Deputy CIO for AI role (James Huang, since April 2025) and adopted the agency's first AI policy. Priorities include agentic AI, AI-assisted coding, responsible-AI governance, and stronger data capabilities to support human decision-making in benefits delivery.

Cybersecurity and cloud security

HHSC operates a CISO office aligned to NIST 800-53 and DIR standards, with focus on cloud security posture management across Azure and AWS. In March 2026 it issued a connected-medical-device security directive and is coordinating inventories with the new Texas Cyber Command.

Procurement

HHSC buys two ways, and a seller should confirm both paths. It uses DIR Cooperative Contracts and DIR SOW solicitations for technology products and services, and it runs its own large competitive procurements (RFP/RFQ) for complex, agency-specific systems. Any purchase over $25,000 is posted to the Texas Electronic State Business Daily (ESBD).
