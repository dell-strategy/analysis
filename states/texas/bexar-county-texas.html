Overview

Bexar County is the fourth-most-populous county in Texas, home to roughly 2 million residents and the City of San Antonio. Its central technology organization is Bexar County Information Technology (BCIT), the internal IT provider for county offices and departments — from the Auditor to the Sheriff — delivering computing and communications infrastructure, end-user services, enterprise applications, and cybersecurity on a 24/7 basis. Governance runs through a five-member Commissioners Court (the County Judge plus four precinct Commissioners), which adopts the budget and approves contracts of size; day-to-day administration runs through the County Manager, to whom the CIO reports. For a Dell SLG seller, the practical map is: BCIT is the technology buying center, the County Manager and Budget & Finance shape what gets funded, and Commissioners Court is the approval gate. Bexar is also distinctive for its setting — San Antonio holds one of the largest concentrations of cyber talent in the U.S. (Sixteenth Air Force, NSA, UTSA, and federal partners), and the new state-level Texas Cyber Command is standing up locally — which raises both the security expectations and the partnership options around the county.

Budget & Fiscal

Bexar County Commissioners Court unanimously adopted a ~$2.8B FY2026 budget (fiscal year Oct 1, 2025–Sep 30, 2026) without raising the property-tax rate, holding it at $0.299999 per $100. Technology is a focused line within that: the proposed FY25-26 budget carried $150M+ in technology-related spending, with BCIT itself slated for a ~$45.6M operating budget — a ~$6.2M increase year-over-year for infrastructure, cybersecurity, end-user services, and enterprise applications.

Initiatives & Priorities

Bexar 2.0 modernization

A ~$27.3M initiative to improve county employee and resident remote-work capability through IT-infrastructure investment, automation, and cloud-based document storage — the county's largest named technology project and a clear window for client refresh, infrastructure, and as-a-service.

Core infrastructure refresh

BCIT is upgrading its Cisco HyperFlex compute/storage platform (vendor support ended September 2025), which underpins most county applications not hosted in the cloud. A direct ISG and data-center modernization opportunity tied to an end-of-support forcing function.

Data Center Infrastructure
IoT / network security

A BCIT IoT Network Security project secures roughly 14,000 daily Internet-of-Things devices on the county network — a recurring security and endpoint-visibility opportunity, reinforced by a modernized data center adopting a cloud-first, IP-monitoring security posture.

AI governance (Admin Policy 5.3)

Commissioners Court unanimously adopted Administrative Policy 5.3 to govern AI use across departments, directing BCIT to vet vendors, run an employee-awareness campaign, and coordinate with partners handling sensitive data — an early governance gate that shapes how AI tooling can be sold in.

Procurement

Bexar County buys through Bexar County Purchasing, with awards of size approved by Commissioners Court. As a local government it is also eligible to buy off pre-competed cooperative vehicles — most importantly the Texas DIR Cooperative Contracts — which let it acquire technology at state pricing without running a full county RFP.
