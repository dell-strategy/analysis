Overview

The Texas Workforce Commission (TWC) is the state's workforce agency — it administers unemployment insurance (UI) benefits and employer UI tax, oversees the statewide network of Workforce Solutions boards and career centers, and runs Vocational Rehabilitation (VR) services that help Texans with disabilities prepare for and find work. TWC is one of Texas's most data- and transaction-intensive agencies: it processes high volumes of unemployment claims, employer wage filings, and benefit payments, and those workloads still run on aging legacy systems that the agency has publicly committed to replacing. Its largest funding stream is federal (workforce and UI administration grants pass through TWC), and its profile rose sharply during the pandemic when claims volume exposed the strain on legacy infrastructure. For a Dell SLG seller, TWC is a modernization story — UI-systems replacement, cloud migration, data and analytics, and the compute/storage and security underneath them — bought through the standard Texas vehicles.

Budget & Fiscal

TWC is funded predominantly by federal dollars, with state general revenue largely going to match programs like Vocational Rehabilitation. The 2026-27 budget recommendations included roughly $4.9B in (non-COVID) federal funds for the biennium, a net increase over 2024-25, set against Texas's $337.4B 2026-27 state budget.

Initiatives & Priorities

Texas Unemployment System (TxUS) modernization

TWC is replacing its legacy UI tax mainframe and public web applications (UTR, UTS, QuickFile, Magnetic Media) with a consolidated, modern TxUS platform, with the tax phase tracking toward go-live in 2026. A multi-phase replacement of mission-critical systems — the central modernization motion to track.

Modernization Unemployment Systems
Cloud migration of core systems

TWC has been moving workloads — including its primary public website — to cloud environments and is replacing legacy mainframe/web systems with modern, cloud-based platforms. Compute, storage, hybrid-cloud, and managed-services demand sits underneath this shift.

Cloud Infrastructure
Data, scale & resilience for UI workloads

UI benefits, tax, and appeals are high-volume, surge-prone workloads where capacity and uptime are mission-critical (a lesson from pandemic-era claims spikes). Data platforms, analytics, and resilient infrastructure are a recurring need.

Vocational Rehabilitation service delivery

VR is a major program area with its own caseload, eligibility, and reporting systems serving Texans with disabilities — a distinct buying area for case management, accessibility, and supporting infrastructure.

Procurement

TWC buys technology through Texas's standard public-sector mechanisms: DIR Cooperative Contracts for pre-competed IT, agency-run RFPs/solicitations for larger or program-specific systems, and TX-RAMP certification as the gate for any cloud service. Confirm Dell's DIR co-op coverage and the right TX-RAMP level before pursuing cloud/as-a-service work.
