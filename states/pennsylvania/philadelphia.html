Overview

Philadelphia is the sixth-largest city in the United States — roughly 1.55 million residents — governed by a strong-mayor system under Mayor Cherelle L. Parker and a 17-member City Council. Enterprise technology direction lives with the Office of Innovation & Technology (OIT), the City's central IT agency: it sets the citywide technology strategy, runs shared infrastructure and networks, delivers cybersecurity, and manages major technology projects for City departments. OIT is led by a Chief Information Officer who reports into the Parker administration, with a Chief Information Security Officer, Chief Data Officer, and several deputy CIOs underneath. The City is best understood as a centralized-but-departmental buyer: OIT owns enterprise infrastructure, security, and platforms, while large operational departments (Police, Fire, Revenue, Licenses & Inspections, the Water Department) carry their own systems and modernization needs. Procurement is gated centrally — competitively bid supplies and services run through the City's Procurement Department and the PHLContracts portal, with cooperative ('piggyback') contracts available to speed sourcing. For a Dell SLG seller, this is one of the largest single municipal opportunities in the Mid-Atlantic; the entry point is OIT's enterprise programs plus the Procurement Department's vehicles, not a single discretionary IT wallet.

Budget & Fiscal

Philadelphia's FY2027 budget — Mayor Parker's ~$6.97B 'One Philly, One Future' proposal, adopted by City Council as a ~$7.1B operating budget in June 2026 — carries forward the administration's priorities of public safety, clean & green, housing (the H.O.M.E. Initiative), economic opportunity, and education. Council rejected Parker's proposed ride-share, hotel/short-term-rental, and retail-delivery taxes, redirecting funds to close a School District shortfall instead. OIT's own FY2027 General Fund budget was proposed at roughly $158.7M — a small (~$2M) decrease from FY2026 as one-time FY26 costs rolled off — with ~$100M in Class 200 (contracts/services) that funds enterprise-wide IT, cybersecurity, and the City's public safety radio system. IT spend beyond OIT is distributed across departments rather than concentrated in one line.

Initiatives & Priorities

Citywide AI guidelines & responsible use

The City is developing AI-use guidelines for employees, with OIT working alongside the Law Department, HR, and the Chief Administrative Officer, plus a promised cross-departmental AI governance committee. Officials targeted a framework for late-winter/early-spring 2026, but as of mid-2026 no formal guidelines had been publicly released — while operational AI use expands (SEPTA/city AI-camera transit-lane enforcement went live with fines in spring 2026; machine learning triages 311 requests). An active AI-governance, data-platform, and AI-infrastructure opening.

Cybersecurity expansion

Expanding cybersecurity capability is the stated driver of OIT's FY2026 budget increase. The CISO owns citywide security strategy across networks, programs, and infrastructure — a funded, executive-priority entry point for security and resilience plays.

Digital platform modernization & secure SSO

OIT modernized phila.gov and is re-platforming to expand login.phila.gov for secure resident Single Sign-On (SSO), advance mobile development, and meet new digital-accessibility standards taking effect in April 2026 — a modernization and infrastructure-refresh track.

Digital equity (DigitalEquityPHL / PHLConnectED)

Backed by an $11.9M federal NTIA Digital Equity award (Jan 2025), the City is funding free/low-cost internet and devices, digital-skills training, Digital Navigators, and a network of eSports & Digital Access Centers in parks and rec facilities — device, connectivity, and endpoint demand at community scale.

Procurement

Selling to Philadelphia means navigating central procurement, not a single buying desk. Competitively bid supplies, equipment, and non-professional services run through the City's Procurement Department and are awarded to the lowest responsive, responsible bidder under the Home Rule Charter; professional services route through eContract Philly. The City also 'piggybacks' onto cooperative contracts established by other entities. Confirm which department owns the requirement, then map the PHLContracts path and any usable co-op vehicle.
