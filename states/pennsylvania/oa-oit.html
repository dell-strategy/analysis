Overview

The Office for Information Technology (OA-OIT) sits inside the Governor's Office of Administration and is the Commonwealth's central IT authority — it oversees the investment in and performance of IT across all executive agencies under the Governor's jurisdiction and sets the policies, standards, and security guidelines those agencies follow. OA-OIT runs enterprise shared services (infrastructure, cloud, data, identity), statewide cybersecurity, and the digital-experience portfolio, and it is the most visible state-government AI program in the country following Pennsylvania's OpenAI/ChatGPT Enterprise pilot. Leadership turned over in late 2025 and was stabilized in early 2026: Bry Pardoe became Commonwealth CIO in October 2025, and in February 2026 Secretary of Administration Neil Weaver made four interim OIT leaders permanent, including a new state CISO. Note the buying nuance — large agency IT budgets (DHS, PennDOT, etc.) live with those agencies; OA-OIT is the policy, standards, and shared-services center, while most cooperative purchasing flows through the separate Department of General Services (DGS) and its COSTARS program.

Budget & Fiscal

OA-OIT's leverage is in statewide direction, shared services, and security policy rather than a single large appropriation. Governor Shapiro's FY2026-27 proposed budget is $53.26B in General Fund spending (up ~5.4%), and OA's IT story this cycle is modernization savings plus a multi-year cybersecurity build-out.

Initiatives & Priorities

Generative AI — ChatGPT Enterprise expansion

After a first-in-the-nation OpenAI pilot (175 employees, 14 agencies) that reported ~95 min/day saved, Pennsylvania expanded ChatGPT Enterprise to 3,000+ employees across 35 agencies in early 2026, with more in training. AI is a top-of-house priority — align infrastructure, data, and endpoint plays to it.

Cybersecurity modernization

OA is pursuing a multi-year cyber modernization strategy, reinforced in the FY2026-27 proposal, to harden state systems against ransomware and data theft. A clear lane for secure infrastructure, endpoint, and zero-trust offerings under the new CISO.

Cloud & IT cost optimization

OA credits ~$37M in three-year savings to right-sizing IT contracts, expanding cloud, and in-housing work. Expect ROI-driven, consolidation-minded buying — position hybrid cloud and as-a-service against measurable cost outcomes.

Cloud Cost Savings
Digital experience (CODE PA)

The Commonwealth Office of Digital Experience — led since Feb 2026 by Executive Director Sara Hall — marked three years (~$10M saved, five national awards) and in spring 2026 began rolling out four enterprise platforms: a centralized permits/licensing system, a grants one-stop shop, a modernized payment platform, and a single resident login. A direct pull-through for the data, identity, and infrastructure beneath citizen-facing services.

Procurement

Selling into Pennsylvania state government runs through pre-established contracts, not one-off RFPs per agency. Most cooperative purchasing is administered by the Department of General Services (DGS), not OA — confirm Dell's coverage on the right DGS statewide and COSTARS contracts, and register in the PA Supplier Portal.
