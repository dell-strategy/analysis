Michigan is in transition heading into 2026, and three things matter most for an SLG seller. First, technology is almost entirely centralized in one department — the Department of Technology, Management & Budget (DTMB) — which owns IT, procurement, and budget, so the buying motion is unusually consolidated. Second, Michigan has become a national AI-infrastructure destination: the OpenAI/Oracle/Related Digital 'Stargate' campus in Saline Township (~1.4GW, called the largest one-time investment in state history) plus new enterprise data-center sales-tax exemptions extended to 2050/2065 are pulling hyperscale and enterprise compute into the state. Third, leadership is in flux — DTMB has an acting director and acting CIO as of early 2026, and a new state Chief Security Officer arrived in December 2025 — so relationships and account mapping are being reset. Dell's clearest plays are AI-infrastructure and research-computing demand, endpoint and lifecycle refresh across a centralized estate, and governed AI as agencies operationalize DTMB's 2025 AI guidelines.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure and 'AI factory' demand tied to the Stargate buildout and broader data-center boom (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • Centralized DTMB estate refresh — endpoint, client, server, and lifecycle across consolidated state IT.
  • Governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as agencies stand up DTMB's AI guidelines and any generative-AI pilot.
  • University research computing (University of Michigan, Michigan State) as independent large-scale AI-infrastructure buyers.

Dell Action Plan

  • Lead with AI infrastructure: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory offerings to the Stargate buildout, the data-center tax incentives, and university research-computing buyers.
  • Re-map DTMB now — engage the acting director, acting CIO, and new CSO early while relationships are being reset under a centralized authority.
  • Validate Dell's DTMB statewide-contract and MiDEAL coverage and resellers are current and correctly scoped for both state and local/education buyers.
  • Position governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as agencies operationalize DTMB's AI guidelines and any generative-AI pilot.
  • Frame modernization around ROI given the ~$1.8B budget gap — endpoint/lifecycle refresh and resilience services that pay back quickly.

Budget & Fiscal

Michigan budgets on an annual fiscal cycle ending September 30. Governor Whitmer's eighth and final executive recommendation, presented February 11, 2026, proposes roughly $88.1B for FY2027, against a projected ~$1.8B gap driven by softening revenue, rising health costs, and federal fiscal impacts. There is no single 'state IT budget' line; technology spend is centralized in DTMB and distributed across agency services, with a dedicated Information Technology Investment Fund for modernization.

Initiatives & Policy

Stargate & AI Data-Center Boom7

Under construction

The OpenAI/Oracle/Related Digital 'Stargate' campus in Saline Township (~1.4GW power, multi-billion-dollar, called the largest one-time investment in state history) advanced to active construction in mid-2026 — the June 1 'The Barn' milestone marked three ~550,000 sq ft halls (~1.65M sq ft total), completion targeted end-2027, despite local opposition. It anchors a wider data-center wave drawn by Michigan's expanded sales/use-tax exemptions. This is the strongest tailwind for Dell AI-infrastructure and ISG demand in the state.

Enterprise Data-Center Tax Exemption2

In effect

Public Act 207 of 2024 (signed Jan 2025) extends the data-center equipment sales/use-tax exemption through 2050 for qualified centers and creates a new enterprise exemption through 2065 for brownfield/former-powerplant sites. Qualification requires $250M+ investment and 30+ jobs at 150% of regional median wage — structurally favoring large, hardware-intensive buildouts.

Procurement & Competition

DTMB is the central technology procurement authority and consolidates IT, procurement, facilities, and budget under one executive department — a structure cited as a national best practice. State technology buys run through DTMB-managed statewide contracts, and the MiDEAL extended purchasing program lets local governments, K-12, universities, community colleges, tribes, and nonprofit hospitals buy off those same contracts without running their own RFP.
