North Dakota is the most consolidated state-government technology environment in the U.S. — and for a Dell seller that changes everything about how you cover the account. North Dakota Information Technology (NDIT) centrally delivers IT for nearly the entire public sector: state agencies, higher education (NDUS), K-12, counties, cities, the courts and the legislative branch all sit on the state-owned STAGEnet network and largely buy through NDIT. That means one CIO, one CISO, and one set of standards drive a 'whole-of-state' motion across ~250,000 devices. Two macro themes define 2025-26: (1) a statewide cybersecurity and K-20 cyber-education model (the PK-20W / 'PoweredByND' approach) that NDIT runs for the entire state, and (2) an energy-driven AI data-center buildout — Applied Digital's Polaris Forge campuses near Ellendale and Harwood (leased to CoreWeave, supporting Nvidia Blackwell), plus flared-gas and crypto-to-AI conversions — turning cheap power and cold climate into national AI-infrastructure capacity. For Dell, the public-sector buying motion is unusually centralized through NDIT and OMB State Procurement, while the private AI-factory boom is a separate, fast-moving ISG opportunity.

Where Dell can play
  • Consolidated whole-of-state refresh: with NDIT buying client, datacenter, and network for nearly all public entities, a single relationship scales across agencies, NDUS, K-12 and local government.
  • AI-infrastructure / 'AI factory' demand tied to the Applied Digital Polaris Forge buildout and other energy-powered data centers (PowerEdge, networking, liquid cooling, services).
  • Whole-of-state cybersecurity: secure compute, endpoint, and infrastructure aligned to NDIT's centralized CISO function and PK-20W cyber program.
  • Higher-ed and research computing across the North Dakota University System, including UND's UAS / autonomy and aerospace programs in Grand Forks.

Dell Action Plan

  • Treat NDIT as the keystone account: because nearly all public-sector IT runs through one agency on STAGEnet, a single, well-positioned relationship scales across agencies, NDUS, K-12 and local government.
  • Lead AI-infrastructure with the data-center boom: tie Dell ISG / AI Factory, networking and liquid-cooling offerings to Applied Digital's Polaris Forge campuses and other energy-powered builds (a commercial-ISG motion alongside the public-sector one).
  • Align to the whole-of-state security mandate: position secure compute, endpoint and infrastructure to NDIT's centralized CISO and the PK-20W cyber program.
  • Clear the procurement gates early: confirm Dell coverage on OMB cooperative state contracts, plan for NDIT's IT Review, and pass the mandatory third-party (cloud/data-hosting) security assessment before pursuing as-a-service deals.
  • Engage NDUS / UND research computing for UAS, autonomy and aerospace workloads concentrated in Grand Forks.

Budget & Fiscal

North Dakota budgets on a two-year (biennial) cycle; the current biennium runs July 1, 2025 through June 30, 2027 and was set by the 69th Legislative Assembly (adjourned May 3, 2025). NDIT operates largely as an internal-service / rate-funded enterprise: in April of each even-numbered year it sets service rates for the upcoming biennium, and those rates generally hold for the two years because agencies cannot request additional funds mid-biennium. There is no single public 'state IT budget' line; technology spend flows through NDIT rates and through individual agency, higher-ed, and local budgets that buy NDIT services.

Initiatives & Policy

Whole-of-State Consolidation (NDIT + STAGEnet)6

Mature / ongoing

North Dakota runs one of the most consolidated public-sector IT models in the nation. NDIT centrally delivers technology for state agencies, the university system, K-12, counties, cities, the courts and the legislature, all riding the state-owned STAGEnet 100GB fiber network (~250,000 devices). For Dell this means a single, scalable relationship can touch nearly every public entity in the state — but it also means standards and approvals funnel through one agency.

Whole-of-State Cybersecurity & PK-20W7

National model

NDIT runs cyber defense and risk management for the entire public sector under a centralized CISO, and the PK-20W initiative makes North Dakota the first state to require computer-science and cybersecurity education at every K-12 grade level. The state's layered, zero-trust posture defends against billions of threats annually. A consolidated security mandate creates demand for secure compute, endpoint, and infrastructure across agencies, schools and local government.

Universal Broadband / Fiber by 20284

On track

North Dakota is on track to become the first state where every home and business can access high-speed fiber by 2028, built largely through telecom cooperatives and private carriers. CIO Corey Mock has named connectivity — fiber, plus a statewide interoperable first-responder radio network targeted for 2027 — as a top priority. Edge connectivity expands the addressable footprint for managed, public-sector technology.

UAS / Autonomy & Aerospace (Grand Forks / UND)9

Expanding

Grand Forks and the University of North Dakota anchor a national UAS / drone ecosystem: the Northern Plains UAS Test Site, the Vantis BVLOS network (first nonfederal entity to use unfiltered FAA radar data), and Project ULTRA defense cargo flights between Grand Forks AFB and Cavalier Space Force Station. These autonomy, sensor and aerospace workloads drive research-computing and edge-compute demand within NDUS.

Procurement & Competition

Public-sector technology buying is centralized through NDIT and OMB's State Procurement Office. NDIT adopts OMB Central Services Division purchasing procedures (the State Procurement Manual) as the standard for all technology purchases, and agencies must initiate an IT Review for buys above approval limits. Per N.D.C.C. 54-59-22, agencies (unless exempted) must obtain application and hosting services through NDIT. Cooperative state contracts established by the State Procurement Office are usable by a broad set of eligible entities — cities, counties, townships, public education, tribal entities and transit authorities.

Competitive landscape

North Dakota is a relationship- and standards-driven market: because NDIT centralizes delivery and approvals, the path runs through a small number of decision-makers and a mandatory IT Review / third-party assessment process. Differentiation comes from whole-of-state scale, AI-infrastructure capability tied to the data-center boom, and security-aligned offerings rather than price alone. The private AI data-center buildout (Applied Digital and peers) is a separate, faster commercial-ISG motion outside the public-sector gates.
