South Carolina budgets annually (fiscal year July–June) and runs a centralized technology model: the Division of Technology Operations (DTO) inside the Department of Administration delivers enterprise IT, while the Division of Procurement Services / Materials Management Office owns statewide contracts. Three things matter to a Dell seller right now. First, an historic AI-infrastructure buildout is landing — Google committed roughly $9B through 2027 across Berkeley and Dorchester counties, and Meta is building an ~$800M AI-optimized data center in Aiken County. Second, the state is operationalizing AI under CIO Nathan Hogue's 'three Ps' framework (protect, promote, pursue), an AI Center of Excellence that vets proposals, and the citizen-facing 'Bradley' AI assistant (with Tyler Technologies). Third, centralized cybersecurity spans 80+ agencies, with a Deloitte-run security operations center, next-gen firewalls, and legacy modernization opening the door to cloud and AI. The buying motion runs through SC statewide term contracts; greenfield data centers and agency modernization are the strongest Dell tailwinds.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure demand tied to the Google ($9B) and Meta (~$800M) data-center buildout — PowerEdge, networking, and services.
  • Legacy application and infrastructure modernization across 80+ agencies as the state unlocks 'trapped' data for cloud and AI.
  • Governed, on-prem / private-cloud AI for sensitive citizen data as agencies move proposals through the AI Center of Excellence.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across a centralized enterprise-IT estate run by DTO.

Budget & Fiscal

South Carolina enacts an annual budget on a July–June fiscal year. The FY2025-26 General Appropriations Act (H.4025) totals roughly $41.6B in all funds, of which about $13.2B is general fund. There is no single published 'state IT budget' line — technology spend is distributed across agencies, with enterprise services centralized in the Division of Technology Operations and statewide buying run through Procurement Services. Governor McMaster's FY2026-27 executive budget (announced January 2026) totals ~$42.8B all funds / ~$14.0B general fund and is now moving through the General Assembly.

Procurement & Competition

South Carolina runs a centralized procurement model under the Consolidated Procurement Code. The Division of Procurement Services (within the Department of Administration) is the state's central procurement authority; its Materials Management Office and an Information Technology Management Office establish open-ended statewide term contracts that leverage the collective buying power of state agencies and political subdivisions. Enterprise IT delivery is centralized in the Division of Technology Operations (DTO).
