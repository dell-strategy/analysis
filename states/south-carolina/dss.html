Overview

The South Carolina Department of Social Services (SCDSS) is the state's primary human-services agency, responsible for child welfare (abuse/neglect intake, investigations, and foster-care case management), public benefits (SNAP, TANF, and related economic-services programs), and child support enforcement. It is one of South Carolina's most data-intensive agencies: its eSAM benefits mainframe serves more than 300,000 households and roughly 630,000 individuals and processes on the order of $1.5B in annual benefit payments, while child-welfare casework runs on CAPSS, the state's SACWIS-class case-management system. SCDSS operates under federal oversight from the long-running Michelle H. v. McMaster child-welfare settlement, which has driven a multi-year child-welfare transformation and continuing data-quality and workload requirements. For a Dell SLG seller, SCDSS is a large, modernization-driven buyer with two active technology fronts — replacing/modernizing the aging benefits mainframe and improving the integrity and tooling of child-welfare case data — reached primarily through South Carolina's central statewide IT contracts rather than agency-only RFPs.

Budget & Fiscal

SCDSS is a direct spender funded through South Carolina's annual General Appropriations Act plus large federal streams (SNAP, Title IV-E child welfare, child-support, and CCDF child care). For FY2027, Director Tony Catone presented a request of roughly $114.6M to expand child-welfare supports and child care and to continue modernizing the benefits mainframe — a proposed ask, not a final enacted appropriation, so confirm the signed budget before forecasting.

Initiatives & Priorities

eSAM benefits-mainframe modernization

SCDSS is pursuing a multiyear replacement/modernization of the aging eSAM mainframe that determines SNAP/TANF eligibility for 300K+ households and processes ~$1.5B in annual payments. The agency has requested successive installments (most recently $18.6M nonrecurring state match) and expects a new system to come online over the next couple of years — a long runway for cloud, data, integration, and migration work.

Modernization Benefits Cloud
Child-welfare transformation & CAPSS data integrity

Under the Michelle H. settlement and court monitors, SCDSS is reforming foster care and improving the quality and integrity of data in CAPSS, its SACWIS case-management system. Documentation gaps and data-entry integrity have been flagged publicly — a surface for case-management tooling, data quality, integration, and analytics.

Child Welfare Data Modernization
Contact-center & benefits citizen experience

SCDSS modernized 19 contact centers and its IVR with Amazon Connect and added real-time SNAP status notifications and video quality-control interviews; SNAP application timeliness rose from ~84% to 95%+ in 2025. Reliability, capacity, and digital experience remain priorities — an infrastructure and benefits-experience play.

Cybersecurity & data protection

SCDSS holds highly sensitive child-welfare, benefits, and child-support data and shares it across agencies and with federal partners. Statewide security sits under the SC Dept of Administration's Division of Information Security (SCDIS), whose SCDIS-200/210 standards apply — data protection, identity, and compliance are persistent requirements alongside both modernization tracks.

Procurement

Selling to SCDSS runs through South Carolina's centralized procurement framework under the Consolidated Procurement Code, administered by the Office of State Procurement (OSP) / Division of Procurement Services. Confirm the right path early: most commodity IT flows through statewide term contracts (with a dedicated IT term-contracting team), while larger or specialized programs — like the eSAM modernization or child-welfare systems — run as their own competitive agency solicitations, sometimes via sole-source determinations.
