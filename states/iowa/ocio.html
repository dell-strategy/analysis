Overview

The Iowa Office of the Chief Information Officer (OCIO) is the state's central IT agency — it leads statewide technology strategy, delivers enterprise shared services to executive-branch agencies, runs statewide cybersecurity, and administers Iowa's broadband grant program. Since a 2022 reorganization the OCIO has been organized under the Iowa Department of Management as its Division of Information Technology (DoIT), and its public web presence is migrating to dom.iowa.gov; the OCIO name and brand remain in active use. Per its FY2022 annual report the office served all executive-branch agencies plus numerous counties, cities, and schools, owned 852 information systems and supported 2,932 systems, and is funded primarily by rates charged to agencies and local subdivisions for the services they consume rather than a large general-fund appropriation. A defining trait for a Dell SLG seller: Iowa's IT model leans heavily on outsourcing and managed services — IT staff augmentation/managed services through CAI (Computer Aid), endpoint managed services through Insight, and cloud/consulting master agreements (e.g., Resultant for Google Cloud, Capgemini) — so OCIO is as much a contract-and-vendor-management hub as it is a direct operator. The CIO is appointed by and reports to the Governor and is subject to Iowa Senate confirmation.

Initiatives & Priorities

Enterprise generative AI policy

Iowa adopted a statewide Enterprise Generative AI policy (effective March 31, 2025), administered by the Department of Management, setting minimum requirements, prohibited uses, mandatory human review of AI output, and a ban on free/unapproved AI tools without written authorization. Signals active AI governance — position AI-ready infrastructure and compliant solutions to the policy's guardrails.

AI Governance
Broadband — Empower Rural Iowa / BEAD

OCIO/DoIT administers the Empower Rural Iowa Broadband Grant Program; prior rounds awarded roughly $500M to reach 100,000+ locations, and a 2025 round opened up to $400M via the federal BEAD program. A large, multi-year build-out adjacent to infrastructure, networking, and edge needs.

Broadband Federal Funds
Whole-of-state cybersecurity

The state runs enterprise information security under the CISO, coordinates incident response, and (with Iowa Homeland Security & Emergency Management and Iowa State's CyIO) channels the federal State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program to local governments. Security is its own buying center and a whole-of-state priority.

Cybersecurity Whole-of-State
Managed-services / outsourced delivery model

Iowa delivers much of its IT through master agreements with managed-service and staff-augmentation vendors (CAI for IT managed services/staff aug, Insight for endpoint managed services, Resultant/Capgemini for cloud and consulting). Modernization runs through these vehicles — map ISG, endpoint, and lifecycle/managed offerings to them.
