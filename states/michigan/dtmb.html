Overview

The Department of Technology, Management & Budget (DTMB) is Michigan's central support agency — it consolidates information technology for nearly all of state government, runs central procurement, and houses the State Budget Office. State IT is almost entirely centralized inside DTMB rather than spread across agencies, which makes it the single most important enterprise relationship for selling technology into Michigan. DTMB's CIO organization spans the Office of the Chief Technology Officer, Agency Services (the IT teams embedded with each department), Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Protection (CIP), and the Center for Shared Solutions. On the buying side, DTMB's Central Procurement Services is the state's purchasing authority and operates MiDEAL, the extended-purchasing co-op that lets Michigan cities, counties, school districts, universities, community colleges, and nonprofit hospitals buy off state contracts without running their own solicitations. DTMB is also where statewide AI and cyber policy is set — it has published the state's AI strategy and adoption guidelines and is the proposed home for a new AI governing board.

Budget & Fiscal

DTMB's leverage is structural: because Michigan centralizes IT here, most state technology spend and most public-sector contract access flow through this one department. Its own modernization money is concentrated in the Information Technology Investment Fund (ITIF), which rose to $50.0M in FY2025-26 from $35.0M the prior year — the pool that funds legacy-system modernization and transformation projects.

Initiatives & Priorities

Statewide AI strategy & generative-AI rollout

DTMB published Michigan's AI strategy and formal adoption/usage guidelines, and began rolling out a Retrieval-Augmented-Generation public chatbot — starting with Unemployment Insurance content — with the stated goal of a unified assistant across the state's ~110 Michigan.gov sites. Strong pull for AI-ready infrastructure, data, and GPU/compute.

AI Modernization
AI governance board (HB 5899)

Legislation would create a three-member AI governing board inside DTMB and a generative-AI pilot for state employees/agencies, with the pilot required to be operational by Jan 1, 2027 and a public evaluation report to the legislature. It cleared the House Communications & Technology Committee unanimously on May 19, 2026 and moved to Rules. Watch as the policy gate that will shape what AI offerings are buyable.

AI Policy
Legacy IT modernization (ITIF)

The Information Technology Investment Fund (now $50.0M) bankrolls modernization of aging state systems and service-delivery transformation. The recurring funding line to map opportunities against — infrastructure refresh, cloud, and application modernization.

Modernization Infrastructure
Enterprise cybersecurity (CIP)

DTMB's Cybersecurity and Infrastructure Protection organization runs enterprise security; Michigan's cyber program is consistently ranked among the strongest of any state. Continued investment in resilience, endpoint, and identity is a durable theme.
