Vermont is a small, centralized market where most state technology decisions run through a single agency — the Agency of Digital Services (ADS), led by Secretary & CIO Denise Reilly-Hughes. Three shifts matter for a Dell seller in 2026. First, ADS is executing a multi-year 'One ADS' strategy that moves the state to a service-based (internal service fund) IT budgeting model, backed by a $15M appropriation, to consolidate fragmented systems and bill agencies for shared services. Second, Governor Scott's new AI Economic Task Force (Executive Order 01-26, May 2026), co-chaired by Reilly-Hughes, sits alongside the state's existing Council on Artificial Intelligence (Act 132 of 2022) and is tasked within 90 days with naming priority opportunities where AI can help state employees. Third, a $70M+ Workday HR/finance modernization is mid-flight — recently shifted from phased to all-at-once rollout — and Vermont's federally funded fiber broadband buildout is pushing access toward 99% statewide. Buying runs through Buildings & General Services (BGS) Office of Purchasing & Contracting and ADS vendor procurement, increasingly via the VT Buys platform and statewide IT contracts.

Where Dell can play
  • Infrastructure consolidation under 'One ADS' and the service-based IT funding model — compute, storage, networking, and lifecycle for shared state services.
  • Governed AI enablement as the AI Economic Task Force and Council on AI define state use cases — an opening for private/on-prem AI on sensitive workloads.
  • Cybersecurity for state and local government via the Cybersecurity Advisory Council and federal SLCGP grants — secure compute, endpoint, and resilience.
  • Client/endpoint and data-center refresh tied to the Workday modernization and a record all-funds budget.

Dell Action Plan

  • Build the relationship with ADS (Secretary/CIO Reilly-Hughes and team) as the single front door — Vermont's IT decisions are centralized there.
  • Tie infrastructure, compute, and lifecycle offerings to the 'One ADS' consolidation and service-based funding model rather than one-off agency deals.
  • Lead with governed, private/on-prem AI for sensitive workloads as the AI Economic Task Force and Council on AI define state use cases.
  • Pursue state and local cybersecurity through the Cybersecurity Advisory Council and SLCGP grant funding — secure compute, endpoint, and resilience.
  • Confirm Dell and reseller coverage on current statewide IT contracts (ITS75 / IT Retainer) and register/stay current on the VT Buys platform.

Budget & Fiscal

Vermont budgets on an annual fiscal-year cycle (July 1–June 30). The FY2026 Appropriations Act (H.493 / Act 27) totals roughly $9.1B across all funds, with about $2.48B in General Fund appropriations. There is no single consolidated 'state IT budget' line — the state spends well over $140M a year on government IT, distributed across agencies, with ADS now moving toward a service-based internal service fund that bills agencies for shared technology.

Initiatives & Policy

One ADS — Service-Based IT Strategy2

In progress

ADS is executing a multi-year 'One ADS' strategy to consolidate fragmented state technology and move to a service-based (internal service fund) budgeting and billing model, backed by a $15M appropriation. The goal is greater reliability, transparency, and predictable cost — a consolidation motion that creates demand for standardized infrastructure, shared compute/storage, and lifecycle services.

AI Economic Task Force & Council on AI5

Established May 2026

Governor Scott's Executive Order 01-26 (May 18, 2026) created the AI Economic Task Force, co-chaired by ADS Secretary/CIO Reilly-Hughes, with working groups on Economic Acceleration, Small Business Competitiveness, and Community Resilience. Within 90 days it must name up to five priority opportunities for AI to help state employees. It complements the existing Council on Artificial Intelligence (Act 132 of 2022) that governs state AI use — an opening for governed, auditable AI.

Workday HR & Finance Modernization4

Mid-implementation

Vermont is replacing legacy human-resources and finance systems with the Workday cloud platform at a cost exceeding $70M. In April 2026 officials postponed the HR (HCM) go-live and moved to a single 'unified launch' now targeted for 2028 after finding the HR and finance components more interconnected than expected; legislators flagged the lack of an upfront gap analysis and are scrutinizing the project — raising cost, risk, and oversight attention. While the platform is SaaS, the slipping timeline opens room for advisory/integration and the surrounding infrastructure and endpoint refresh.

Cybersecurity Advisory Council & SLCGP6

Active

Vermont's Cybersecurity Advisory Council is extending protection beyond state government to critical-infrastructure and local entities, using a tiered plan (training/planning, risk management, information sharing). Federal State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (SLCGP) funds, administered with Ready Vermont, help municipalities meet baseline security — a channel for secure compute, endpoint, and resilience offerings into the SLG market.

Broadband Buildout — VCBB & CUDs7

Accelerating

The Vermont Community Broadband Board (VCBB) and locally governed Communications Union Districts (CUDs) are building fiber into rural Vermont with a ~$229M, BEAD-anchored program; NEK Broadband alone won ~$93M to reach ~10,000 addresses. The goal is ~99% statewide access. Primarily a fiber/ISP motion, but the expanding state and CUD digital footprint drives downstream network, edge, and security needs.

Procurement & Competition

Vermont technology buying is centralized. Statewide purchasing policy and contracting run through the Buildings & General Services (BGS) Office of Purchasing & Contracting, while the Agency of Digital Services (ADS) owns technology strategy, standards, and IT vendor procurement. The state is standardizing sourcing on the VT Buys eProcurement platform, and maintains statewide IT contracts that agencies buy from without running their own solicitations.

Competitive landscape

Vermont is a small, relationship-driven, centralized market. Volume is modest versus large states, so differentiation comes from fit with ADS consolidation, governed AI, and state/local cybersecurity — plus presence on current statewide IT contracts and VT Buys — rather than price alone. Incumbent resellers and SaaS platforms (e.g., Workday for ERP) hold key footholds.
