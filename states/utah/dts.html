Overview

The Utah Department of Technology Services (DTS) is the state's consolidated central IT agency and one of the most centralized models in the US — it provides enterprise technology services (infrastructure, networking, security, application development, and end-user support) to nearly all executive-branch agencies rather than letting each agency run its own IT shop. DTS is a division of the Department of Government Operations and operates under the State Chief Information Officer (CIO). Because consolidation is so deep, DTS is the buying center for most statewide technology decisions: agency CIOs and IT staff are embedded under DTS, so a seller's relationship with DTS reaches across the executive branch instead of one agency at a time. DTS is also visibly AI-forward, building enterprise AI infrastructure and governance for agencies in step with Utah's broader pro-human posture and the state's Office of Artificial Intelligence Policy (created by the 2024 AI Policy Act, SB-149). For a Dell SLG seller, DTS is the single highest-leverage relationship in Utah state government.

Budget & Fiscal

DTS is funded primarily as an internal service fund (ISF): it recovers costs by charging agencies rates for the services it delivers rather than drawing one large general-fund appropriation. The Legislature authorizes those rates each session, so the budget story is about the rate structure and the agency dollars flowing through it, not a single line item.

Initiatives & Priorities

Enterprise AI program

DTS is standing up an AI program — providing infrastructure, platforms, data governance, and ethics/standards so agencies can adopt generative AI safely. It is actively evaluating and rolling out gen-AI use cases across the executive branch, aligned with Utah's pro-human AI posture.

AI Infrastructure
Statewide IT consolidation

Deep consolidation is the defining DTS feature — enterprise infrastructure, networking, and shared services span nearly all executive-branch agencies. Modernization and refresh decisions are made centrally, which is where infrastructure and platform plays land.

Modernization Data Center
Cybersecurity & state Cyber Center

DTS runs statewide security under the CISO, including the Utah Cyber Center, setting security standards and operating SOC/incident-response capabilities for served agencies. Security requirements gate most infrastructure and cloud purchases.

Cybersecurity
Cloud & digital services

DTS provides hybrid cloud, hosting, and digital/online-service delivery for agencies. Cloud and as-a-service offerings flow through DTS standards and statewide cooperative contracts rather than agency-by-agency buys.

Procurement

Technology buying in Utah runs through the Division of Purchasing and General Services on statewide and cooperative contracts, with DTS as the consolidated technical buyer/operator. Two access paths matter: holding the right state and cooperative contracts, and being positioned on DTS's enterprise standards and managed-services catalog.
