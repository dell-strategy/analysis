Overview

The Louisiana Division of Administration (DOA) is the management and finance arm of the executive branch — it drafts the state budget, manages facilities and capital outlay, and houses two functions that matter most to a technology seller: the Office of Technology Services (OTS), the state's consolidated central IT organization, and the Office of State Procurement (OSP), which owns statewide contracts and cooperative purchasing. OTS was created by the Legislature in 2014 to consolidate IT out of the agencies and into a single shared-services provider; it now supports roughly 40,000 executive-branch users and runs the state's data centers, network, security operations, and enterprise applications. Critically, OTS is an ancillary agency that recovers its costs through a fee-for-service model — its operating budget flows through inter-agency transfers and self-generated funds rather than a direct general-fund appropriation, so its scale shows up in the services it bills back to agencies rather than a headline line item. For a Dell SLG seller, DOA/OTS is the central buying and standards-setting center for state-government technology in Louisiana, sitting alongside OSP's procurement vehicles as the path to most executive-branch IT spend.

OTS issued a statewide Artificial Intelligence Acceptable Use Policy effective Sept. 29, 2025, setting guidelines for responsible AI use by state agencies and employees. AI is an active, governed track — lead with governed, secure AI infrastructure rather than ungated tooling.
