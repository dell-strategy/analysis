Overview

The Arkansas Division of Information Systems (DIS) is the state's central IT and shared-services organization, providing the statewide network backbone (data, voice, video), data-center and hosting, and shared technology services for state agencies, and increasingly setting statewide technology and security policy. Following Gov. Sarah Huckabee Sanders' June 12, 2025 executive order and a statewide IT assessment, DIS was reorganized and rebranded as the Office of State Technology (OST), reporting to the Department of Shared Administrative Services (SAS) — the renamed successor to the Department of Transformation & Shared Services (TSS), effective around July 1, 2025. Public references still use 'DIS' and 'TSS' interchangeably with the new OST/SAS names during the transition, so confirm the current entity name on any document. DIS/OST is funded largely through cost recovery (non-revenue receipts) on services it provides to state, local, and federal customers rather than a single large general-revenue line, and it is the natural buying center for statewide infrastructure, hosting, cloud, and now consolidated cybersecurity. For a Dell seller, DIS/OST is best understood as both the standards-and-security layer above agency IT and the shared-services delivery arm — the path into statewide infrastructure modernization.

Budget & Fiscal

DIS isn't the state's largest direct spender, but it sits at the center of Arkansas IT economics. The division's own appropriation runs roughly $102M/year (largely cost-recovery from customer agencies), while the 2025 statewide assessment found Arkansas spends on the order of $680–700M/year on technology overall — heavily weighted toward applications rather than infrastructure, and only ~$6M on cybersecurity versus a ~$60M peer-state average. The reorganization is pitched as saving $65–130M/year through consolidation and stronger vendor negotiation.

Initiatives & Priorities

Statewide IT centralization & reorganization

Gov. Sanders' June 2025 executive order, backed by a ~956-page assessment, consolidates IT administration and governance, institutes statewide project management, and consolidates duplicative services. DIS was rebranded as the Office of State Technology (OST) under SAS. The consolidation drive is the central opening — confirm which entity owns a given program before forecasting.

Modernization Reorg
Centralized cybersecurity program

A new State Cybersecurity Office (SCSO) centralizes cyber risk management across agencies, with the assessment flagging Arkansas's ~$6M cyber spend as far below peers. Closing that gap is a stated priority — a clear opening for security infrastructure, endpoint, and resilience offerings.

Cybersecurity
AI adoption & governance

Arkansas is standing up an AI & Analytics Center of Excellence, recruiting its first Chief AI Officer, and OST issued statewide AI acceptable-use policy under Act 848 (2025). Gov. Sanders' AI report targets productivity, Medicaid/SNAP program integrity, and an AI-enabled services portal — opening AI-infrastructure and platform conversations.

eProcurement & shared-services modernization

The state is migrating to a new SAP Ariba eProcurement system (targeted July 2026) and consolidating shared technology services. Infrastructure refresh, hosting/cloud, and managed services across agencies are the recurring delivery openings.
