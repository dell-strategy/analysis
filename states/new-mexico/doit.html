Overview

The New Mexico Department of Information Technology (DoIT) is the executive branch's central IT agency, headquartered in Santa Fe. It delivers enterprise services to state agencies — telecommunications, network and internet access, server and application hosting, the statewide SHARE ERP system, and public-safety radio (the Digital Trunked Radio System) — and is organized into six authorized programs: Program Support, Compliance and Project Management, Enterprise Services, Equipment Replacement, Broadband, and Cybersecurity. DoIT funds most of its operations through cost recovery: it charges customer agencies published rates for services rather than running on a large general-fund appropriation, and uses non-reverting equipment-replacement funds to refresh aging infrastructure. Two policy offices are administratively attached to DoIT but operate with their own mandates — the Office of Broadband Access & Expansion (OBAE), which is executing New Mexico's federal broadband buildout, and the Office of Cybersecurity (OCS), whose CISO now reports to a Cybersecurity Advisory Committee rather than to the CIO. For a Dell seller, DoIT is both an enterprise-infrastructure buyer for the state's data-center and network estate and the central authority that shapes how other agencies procure IT.

Budget & Fiscal

DoIT is funded primarily through cost recovery, not a large direct appropriation: it bills customer agencies published rates for enterprise services and sustains its non-reverting equipment-replacement funds from those fees. Its FY27 appropriation request (submitted September 2025) aligned spending to FY25 actuals and accommodated a 5% rate increase for Enterprise Services approved by the rate committee. The most consequential dollars in DoIT's orbit are pass-through and grant funds — most visibly the $382M federal broadband award flowing through OBAE.

Initiatives & Priorities

BEAD broadband buildout via OBAE

New Mexico's $382M federal BEAD plan (approved by NTIA in Jan 2026) funds 31 grants to connect 42,500+ unserved/underserved locations across 32 of 33 counties, using a tech-neutral mix of fixed wireless, fiber, and LEO satellite. OBAE, attached to DoIT, runs the program. Adjacent opportunity for network, edge, and middle-mile infrastructure rather than a direct DoIT data-center play.

Statewide cybersecurity under the Office of Cybersecurity

New Mexico's Cybersecurity Act stood up an independent Office of Cybersecurity whose CISO reports to a Cybersecurity Advisory Committee, with scope extended to local, county, tribal, and education entities. The state expects roughly $13M over four years via the State and Local Cybersecurity Grant Program (80% to locals) and is pursuing statewide procurement vehicles for shared security tooling.

Enterprise services & infrastructure modernization

DoIT's Enterprise Services program is the core motion for Dell: consolidated hosting, network, cloud, and the equipment-replacement refresh cycle for aging state infrastructure. The 5% FY27 rate increase signals continued reinvestment in the shared estate.

Procurement

New Mexico buying runs through two layers: the General Services Department (GSD) State Purchasing Division, which establishes statewide price agreements open to state and local government entities, and DoIT, which reviews IT projects and professional-services procurement.
