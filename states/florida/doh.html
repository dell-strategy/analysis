Overview

The Florida Department of Health (DOH) is the state's lead public-health agency, headquartered in Tallahassee and operating through a county health department in all 67 Florida counties. Its mission spans epidemiology and communicable-disease control, emergency preparedness and bioterrorism response, immunizations, vital records (birth/death certificates), environmental health, school health, and chronic-disease prevention for a population of 23M-plus residents plus a large visitor base. DOH is led by the State Surgeon General (who also serves as Secretary of Health) reporting to the Governor, and employs more than 17,000 people. For a technology seller, the relevant story is data- and records-intensive: DOH operates the Health Management System (HMS), an ONC-certified electronic health record (certified 2019, built on Microsoft Azure infrastructure) used across the county health departments, plus surveillance, vital-statistics, and prescription-drug-monitoring (E-FORCSE) systems. DOH does not run its own statewide IT procurement authority — technology buying flows through the Department of Management Services (DMS) state term contracts and the state's central IT-governance body, the Florida Digital Service (which remains housed under DMS after the 2026 legislative effort to move it into a new DIGIT division died in the Senate).

Budget & Fiscal

DOH is a sizable program agency but not a central IT buyer — its technology dollars ride on DMS vehicles and specific appropriations rather than a large standalone IT line. Governor DeSantis signed the $117.6B FY2026-27 budget on June 29, 2026 (after ~$810M in line-item vetoes), sustaining the ~$4.1B health-care investment framed in the 'Floridians First' proposal and fully funding Medicaid/KidCare. The proposal earmarked funds to evaluate and replace the EHR/practice-management software used by the county health departments; confirm the final enacted line before forecasting, since health-IT modernization was not called out in the signing announcement. The prior FY2025-26 budget cut about 601 DOH positions (~4.5% of the workforce), tightening the operating envelope.

Initiatives & Priorities

County health-department EHR / HMS modernization

The FY26-27 budget proposal earmarks ~$6.6M to evaluate and implement new electronic health record and practice-management software across the 67 county health departments, an upgrade meant to integrate patient records and streamline operations statewide. DOH already runs the ONC-certified Health Management System (HMS) on Microsoft Azure; a refresh or replacement is the clearest near-term modernization vehicle and the natural anchor for infrastructure, migration, and data conversations.

Disease surveillance & vital-records systems

DOH operates statewide surveillance, vital-statistics, and prescription-drug-monitoring (E-FORCSE) systems that ingest and exchange high volumes of clinical and registry data across 67 counties. Sustained demand for resilient, scalable infrastructure, faster data ingestion/analytics, and interoperability across legacy and modern platforms.

Data integration & analytics

Linking patient demographics, clinical reconciliation, family-health history, and exportable health information across the county system points to ongoing data-platform and analytics needs. Any AI/advanced-analytics offering must clear data-privacy and federal health-records (HIPAA/ONC) gates given the sensitivity of the data.

Cybersecurity alignment to state standards

DOH must align to the statewide Florida Cybersecurity Standards (Rule 60GG-2, F.A.C., NIST-based) administered by DMS, including access management, encryption, and incident reporting. Health-data sensitivity — underscored by the RansomHub breach of DOH's Vital Statistics system and ongoing victim notifications — keeps security posture, resilience, and asset visibility in active scope.

Procurement

Selling into DOH means navigating two layers: the agency's own competitive procurements and Florida's centralized IT-purchasing machinery. DOH buys mission-specific systems through its own RFP/ITN solicitations, but most technology goods and services flow through Department of Management Services (DMS) state term contracts and IT enterprise agreements, governed by the state's central IT body — the Florida Digital Service, which remains under DMS (led by State CIO Warren Sponholtz) after the 2026 SB 480 / DIGIT restructuring bill died in the Senate.
