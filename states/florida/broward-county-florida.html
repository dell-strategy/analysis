Overview

Broward County is the second-most-populous county in Florida — more than 2 million residents across Fort Lauderdale and 30+ municipalities — and the 17th-largest county in the United States. County government operates under a County Administrator (CEO model) with an elected nine-member Board of County Commissioners. Day-to-day IT is run by Enterprise Technology Services (ETS), a division of roughly 165 IT professionals organized around application services, infrastructure, project management, IT business services, and financial services. ETS is the central technology provider for county agencies, supporting a portfolio that spans the airport (FLL), Port Everglades, transit, public safety, libraries, and human services. Under CIO Domenic DiLullo (in the role since March 2024), the division's stated priorities are cybersecurity, modernization, and organizational culture — with active work streams in cloud migration strategy, ERP and AI governance, and AI-driven process automation. For a Dell SLG seller, Broward is a large, infrastructure-rich county account where the buying motion runs through the county Purchasing Division and cooperative contracts rather than the state's central vehicle.

Budget & Fiscal

Broward adopted an $8.8B total budget for FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025–Sep 30, 2026). Most of that is enterprise funds (airport, seaport, water/wastewater), grants, and pass-throughs; the discretionary property-tax-supported portion is about $1.7B. ETS's own operating IT budget has been reported in the ~$28M range — the figure to anchor IT-specific spend against.
