Overview

Miami-Dade County is the largest county in Florida and one of the largest local governments in the United States, serving roughly 2.7 million residents across Miami and 30+ municipalities. It operates under a strong-mayor / county-commission structure: Mayor Daniella Levine Cava is the elected chief executive who proposes the budget and runs day-to-day operations, with a 13-member Board of County Commissioners as the legislative body. Central technology is delivered by the Information Technology Department (ITD), which supports all 26 county departments with a staff of around 900 covering enterprise applications, network and radio systems, the data center, and cybersecurity. The county has positioned itself as a public-sector AI leader — its AI Roadmap and policy framework have drawn national recognition — and runs sizable enterprise programs (ERP / finance and supply chain, criminal-justice systems) that touch infrastructure, cloud, and security spend. For a Dell SLG seller, Miami-Dade is a marquee county account: a large, modernization-minded buyer where the budget is approved annually by the Commission and most large IT purchases route through the county's central procurement organization.

Budget & Fiscal

The Board of County Commissioners adopted a $12.9B FY2025-26 budget in the early hours of Sept. 19, 2025, after closing a roughly $402M shortfall while holding the millage rate flat (total adopted operating and debt millage of 9.5778, no property-tax-rate increase). IT is funded within departmental and capital lines rather than a single published figure; ITD's scale is best read through its ~900-person workforce and the enterprise systems it operates.

Initiatives & Priorities

County AI Roadmap & governance

Miami-Dade has published an AI Roadmap and AI policy (approved-tools requirement, mandatory human review) and has been recognized as a leading U.S. county for structured AI adoption. Priority use cases include an AI assistant for public-benefits applications and an HR/benefits chatbot — pull-through for AI infrastructure, data platforms, and security.

AI Data
Enterprise systems modernization (ERP)

ITD operates an enterprise resource planning system for finance and supply-chain operations plus large criminal-justice systems spanning many agencies. These platforms anchor ongoing compute, storage, and data-center refresh needs.

Modernization Data Center
Cybersecurity program

The county runs a CITD-led (formerly ITD), CISO-owned cybersecurity program protecting county systems and resident data across all departments. A perennial buying center for endpoint, data protection, resilience, and recovery — and a competitive cybersecurity-services solicitation is in the procurement pipeline as of 2026.

Cybersecurity
Cloud, data-center & legacy modernization

CITD manages the county data center, network, and radio systems and is steadily modernizing a ~30-year legacy estate toward hybrid cloud and shared services. Leadership has publicly acknowledged legacy lock-in that can't be 'lift-and-shifted' — a strong fit for infrastructure, migration, as-a-service, and managed-services offerings.

Procurement

Large Miami-Dade IT buys route through the county's central procurement organization (historically the Internal Services Department; now branded Strategic Procurement), which awards and manages county contracts and cooperative agreements. Solicitations are posted on the county site and via BidSync, and the county also offers cooperative contracts other Florida municipalities and agencies can ride. Confirm Dell's standing on the relevant county contracts and the state/co-op vehicles before forecasting.
