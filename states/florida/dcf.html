Overview

The Florida Department of Children and Families (DCF) is the state's primary human-services agency, responsible for child welfare (abuse and neglect intake, investigations, and case management), mental health and substance-abuse services, and public-benefits eligibility through the ACCESS Florida program (SNAP, Temporary Cash Assistance, and Medicaid eligibility determination). It is one of Florida's most data-intensive agencies: it operates statewide eligibility and case-management systems, the MyACCESS self-service portal, and the Florida Safe Families Network (FSFN) child-welfare case system. DCF processes very high transaction volumes — by its own public figures it handles, on average, hundreds of thousands of Medicaid applications, redeterminations, and assistance requests each month. The agency is in the middle of a multi-year modernization to a federally compliant CCWIS (Comprehensive Child Welfare Information System), making it an active modernization account where data integration, cloud, and security work all surface. For a Dell SLG seller, DCF is a large, infrastructure- and modernization-driven buyer reached primarily through Florida's central procurement vehicles rather than agency-only RFPs.

Budget & Fiscal

DCF is a large direct spender funded through Florida's annual General Appropriations Act, with a substantial federal match flowing through SNAP, Medicaid eligibility, and Title IV-E child-welfare funding. Governor DeSantis' FY2026–27 'Floridians First' budget proposed more than $4.8B for the Department — the proposed figure, not a final enacted appropriation, so confirm the signed General Appropriations Act before forecasting.

Initiatives & Priorities

CCWIS child-welfare modernization

DCF is replacing/modernizing its child-welfare technology toward a federally compliant CCWIS, in a modular, phased approach starting with intake, investigations, and case management, and building new enterprise-architecture foundations. Independent IV&V oversight is in place. A multi-year program — strong surface for data, cloud, and integration infrastructure.

ACCESS Florida / MyACCESS eligibility

The ACCESS Florida eligibility engine and the MyACCESS self-service portal determine SNAP, cash-assistance, and Medicaid eligibility at very high volume. Reliability, capacity, and customer-facing digital experience are recurring priorities — an infrastructure and benefits-modernization play.

Data integration & cybersecurity

DCF holds highly sensitive child-welfare, behavioral-health, and benefits data and shares it across agencies (e.g., Medicaid, juvenile justice). Data integration, identity, and security controls are persistent requirements alongside the CCWIS rebuild.

Behavioral-health capacity

Procurement

DCF buys through Florida's centralized procurement framework, administered by the Department of Management Services (DMS). Confirm the right vehicle before pursuing a deal: vendors must register in the Vendor Information Portal, and most commodity IT flows through State Term Contracts and MyFloridaMarketplace; larger or specialized programs (like CCWIS) run as dedicated solicitations.
