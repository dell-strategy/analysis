Overview

The Florida Department of Transportation (FDOT) is the state's lead transportation agency, headquartered in Tallahassee and operating through seven geographic districts plus Florida's Turnpike Enterprise (FTE). It plans, designs, builds, maintains, and operates the State Highway System and oversees aviation, rail, transit, seaports, and the statewide intelligent-transportation network. For a technology seller, FDOT is a heavily instrumented, data- and edge-rich account rather than a back-office IT shop: it runs the SunGuide advanced traffic-management software that serves as the hub of Florida's ITS network across regional Transportation Management Centers (TMCs), deploys connected-vehicle (CV) corridors, and operates one of the nation's largest all-electronic tolling systems through FTE — a 515-mile turnpike serving roughly 3M daily customers via SunPass and interoperable transponders. Technology is led by the Office of Information Technology (OIT) under the agency CIO, while statewide IT standards and most enterprise procurement sit above FDOT at the Department of Management Services (DMS) and the state's central IT body, which is itself being restructured in 2026.

Budget & Fiscal

FDOT is one of Florida's largest capital agencies — its spending power shows up in the multi-year Work Program rather than a back-office IT line. The adopted Five-Year Work Program for FY2026-2030 totals roughly $32.8B, and the FY2025-26 state budget signed by Governor DeSantis directed about $15.1B to transportation, including ~$13.7B for the State Transportation Work Program. Most technology rides on these capital and operations programs (ITS, tolling, TMC operations) plus DMS enterprise vehicles, not a standalone agency IT appropriation.

Initiatives & Priorities

SunGuide ITS & TMC modernization

SunGuide is FDOT's enterprise advanced traffic-management software and the hub of the statewide ITS network, integrating field devices, software, and network applications across regional Transportation Management Centers and enabling TMC-to-TMC interoperability and data archiving. Ongoing 24/7/365 TMC operations and the suite of interconnected TSM&O software keep sustained demand for resilient compute, storage, networking, and managed services at the core and the edge.

ITS Traffic Management Edge
Connected & automated vehicles (CAV)

FDOT deploys connected-vehicle strategies on select corridors — e.g., District Six's US-1 corridor in the Keys — to let infrastructure and vehicles exchange real-time travel data, with 2026 lessons-learned/best-practices guidance covering integration with central systems (SunGuide, SCMS, DEPOT). CAV expands the field/edge device footprint and the need for low-latency data ingestion, security, and roadside-to-cloud connectivity.

Connected Vehicle Edge Data
All-electronic tolling & SunPass (FTE)

Florida's Turnpike Enterprise runs a 515-mile, fully all-electronic tolling system (since Nov 2021) serving ~3M daily customers via SunPass and interoperable transponders, collecting on the order of $1.35B annually. High-volume transaction processing, image/plate recognition, and customer-account systems point to sustained data-platform, analytics, and resilient-infrastructure demand in a self-funding business unit.

Tolling Data Platform Analytics
AI in operations & engineering

FDOT has begun exploring AI in its core mission — including a solicitation to apply AI to FDOT Standard Specifications for road and bridge construction — alongside data-heavy ITS, asset-management, and safety analytics. Early-stage AI/ML demand spans document and specification automation, traffic analytics, and predictive maintenance; any AI/cloud offering touching state data must clear Florida's statewide security and procurement gates.

Procurement

Selling into FDOT means navigating both the agency's own competitive procurements and Florida's centralized purchasing machinery. FDOT advertises commodity and contractual-services solicitations through the State of Florida Vendor Bid System and requires vendors to register in MyFloridaMarketplace (MFMP); districts and FTE also run their own RFP/ITN procurements for ITS, TMC operations, and tolling systems. Most catalog-style hardware, software, and IT services flow through DMS state term contracts. Confirm Dell's coverage on the relevant DMS vehicles and FDOT's Approved Products List context before forecasting.
