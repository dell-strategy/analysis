Overview

Hillsborough County is Florida's fourth-most-populous county, anchored by Tampa, with roughly 1.6 million residents and ongoing rapid growth. It is governed by a Board of County Commissioners with a professional County Administrator as chief executive, overseeing more than 5,200 employees and a wide service portfolio (public safety support, utilities/water, transportation, libraries, parks, health and human services). Technology is centralized: the County's Information & Innovation Office / IT Services function is led by a Chief Information & Innovation Officer who sits on the executive team, and it supports enterprise infrastructure, applications, data, and cybersecurity across departments. For a Dell SLG seller, Hillsborough is a high-value standalone account — large enough to run its own competitive procurements and cooperative buys, while also fitting inside the broader Florida public-sector and Tampa Bay regional growth story.

Budget & Fiscal

Hillsborough County's all-funds budget is large — reported at roughly $9 billion when Florida's state-level efficiency task force reviewed it in 2025 — spanning operating, capital, and enterprise (utility) funds. IT and innovation spending is a department-level slice of that total; exact figures live in the County's adopted budget documents and should be confirmed against the current adopted book before forecasting.

Procurement

Hillsborough County runs its own Procurement Services department and competitive solicitations, and it also buys through cooperative and piggyback contracts. The County's Chapter 10 procurement policy expressly allows cooperative procurement and use of other governments' contracts, with State of Florida and GSA awards exempt from piggyback limits — confirm Dell's coverage on each vehicle before pursuing a deal.
