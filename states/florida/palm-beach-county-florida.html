Overview

Palm Beach County is the third-most-populous county in Florida — roughly 1.5–1.6 million residents across West Palm Beach and 39 municipalities — and one of the largest counties by land area east of the Mississippi. County government operates under a County Administrator (CEO model) reporting to an elected seven-member Board of County Commissioners, with a workforce of about 7,100. Central IT is delivered by Information Systems Services (ISS), now publicly branded the Office of Technology and Innovation (OTI), which serves most departments under the Board plus some constitutional officers. The shop runs the largest county-owned fiber-optic network in Florida, a unified-communications platform for roughly 10,000 users, an inventory of 350+ in-house applications, Oracle databases, enterprise GIS, data-center colocation and hosting, and a 24x7 help desk. Its stated highest priorities are cybersecurity, broadband/connectivity, and centralized IT-security oversight. For a Dell SLG seller, Palm Beach is a large, infrastructure-rich county account in transition — a new County Administrator (Joseph Abruzzo, mid-2025) and a CIO succession (longtime CIO Archie Satchell retired January 2026; deputy now acting) — where the buying motion runs through the county Purchasing Department and cooperative contracts rather than the state's central vehicle.

Budget & Fiscal

Palm Beach County adopted a $9.6B total budget for FY2026 (Oct 1, 2025–Sep 30, 2026). The General Fund operating portion is about $2.5B, balanced at a countywide rate of 4.5000 mills — held flat from FY2025 — generating roughly $1.5B in ad valorem taxes (about $112M more than the prior year). IT capital costs are borne directly by the Board of County Commissioners rather than allocated through the ISS cost plan, so technology spend is split between ISS's recovered operating costs and Board-funded capital.

Initiatives & Priorities

Cybersecurity & centralized security oversight

Cybersecurity is a stated top priority. The Enterprise IT Security division operates on the NIST Cybersecurity Framework (Identify/Protect/Detect/Respond/Recover) and is working to improve centralized oversight of IT security across departments and constitutional officers — an opening for security hardening, monitoring, identity, and resilience.

Fiber network & connectivity

ISS/OTI builds and maintains the largest county-owned fiber-optic network in Florida and treats broadband/connectivity as a top priority — a driver for network infrastructure, edge, and the underlying compute/storage that rides on it.

Data center, hosting & colocation

The county is a growing data-center and hosting provider not just internally but to other government, education, health-care, and nonprofit agencies (colocation, hosting, network/internet service). That as-a-service posture is a natural fit for ISG infrastructure refresh and hybrid-cloud conversations.
