Overview

The Florida Department of Corrections (FDC) is the largest state agency by personnel and operates the third-largest state prison system in the United States, behind only Texas and the federal Bureau of Prisons. The department incarcerates roughly 80,000–90,000+ inmates across 140+ facilities — major institutions, annexes, work camps, work-release centers, and forestry/road camps — and supervises well over 100,000 offenders in the community through probation and parole. FDC employs about 24,000 people, the majority of them certified correctional and probation officers. For a technology seller, FDC reads as a high-volume infrastructure and security-modernization account rather than a procurement conduit: it runs its own data systems, facility-security technology, networking, and offender-management platforms, and is in the middle of a multi-year modernization of legacy systems. Leadership has just turned over: long-serving Secretary Ricky Dixon retired effective June 30, 2026, and Governor DeSantis elevated Deputy Secretary Richard Comerford — a 40-year FDC veteran — to Secretary, so the relationship map at the top of the agency is being rebuilt heading into FY2026-27.

Budget & Fiscal

FDC is a large direct spender. Its FY2026-27 request carried significant technology and facility-security line items on top of a major correctional-officer pay package, and the Governor's recommendations earmarked tens of millions specifically for IT modernization. Governor DeSantis signed the ~$117.6B FY2026-27 state budget on June 29, 2026 (~$810M in line-item vetoes). Notably he vetoed HB 5403E — a bipartisan prison-upgrade plan ($50M/yr for 40 years, a 600-bed prison hospital, and bond authority for new construction) — and, tied to it, the $2/hr correctional-officer pay raise, calling the linkage a way to 'hold hostage' pay to generate debt. Initiatives & Priorities

OBIS modernization & CIMS rollout

FDC is modernizing its legacy Offender-Based Information System (OBIS), including the launch of the Corrections Inmate Management System (CIMS) and foundational data infrastructure and governance for statewide analytics. A multi-year platform shift that pulls through compute, storage, data-platform, and integration needs.

Modernization Data
Facility security technology

Expanded body-worn camera deployment, inmate body scanners, biometric scanning (in partnership with FDLE), and contraband interdiction — including public support for FCC cellphone-jamming authority. Edge, networking, and storage at 140+ sites underpin these programs.

Security Edge
Records & infrastructure modernization

Enterprise records modernization (OnBase) is digitizing inmate and personnel records for security, accessibility, and compliance — part of a broader move off legacy systems toward modern, mobile-enabled platforms. Networking and IT-modernization scope across a large, distributed facility footprint.

Infrastructure Modernization
Intelligence & contraband detection

FY2026-27 request to expand intelligence capabilities using modern technologies to identify and remove contraband, plus radio/tower refresh. Data, analytics, and ruggedized edge/comms infrastructure across institutions.

Analytics Networking
CLEO generative-AI platform

FDC's custom generative-AI assistant (Corrections Large Language Environment Online), launched ~April 2025 on Microsoft Azure Government inside the agency's secure network. Modules span chat, a RAG-based policy library, legacy-mainframe data cleanup, and analytics/reporting off the data warehouse; AI also screens ~450M annual inmate-call minutes, with facial-recognition and video/voice analysis planned. Signals an active AI/data-platform agenda pulling through compute, storage, GPU, and data-management needs.

Procurement

FDC buys through Florida's centralized purchasing framework plus its own agency solicitations. The Department of Management Services (DMS) is the only entity authorized to establish State Term Contracts, and all vendors register and transact through MyFloridaMarketPlace (MFMP).
