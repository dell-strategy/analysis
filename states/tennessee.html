Statewide AI Action Plan
Four strategic pillars

Tennessee enters FY2026 with a $59.5B all-funds budget and an unusually strong AI-infrastructure story for an SLG seller. First, the state is now a national AI-compute epicenter: xAI's 'Colossus' in Memphis is one of the largest AI supercomputers on earth (scaling toward 2GW and ~555,000 GPUs as of January 2026), and Oak Ridge National Laboratory is standing up the 'Lux' AI cluster (early 2026) ahead of its next-gen 'Discovery' exascale system. Second, state government itself is moving fast on AI: a State AI Advisory Council released Tennessee's first statewide AI Action Plan in November 2025, and Strategic Technology Solutions (STS) has already deployed ChatGPT Enterprise to thousands of employees. Third, IT is centralized and in 'execution' mode under new State CIO Kristin Darby, including an ERP modernization that explicitly demands 'native AI architecture.' For Dell, the buying motion runs through the Central Procurement Office's statewide contracts, and the data-and-compute-readiness pillar of the AI plan is a direct opening for governed, secure infrastructure.

Where Dell can play
  • AI-infrastructure / 'AI factory' demand across the Memphis (xAI Colossus), Nashville, and Oak Ridge corridors (PowerEdge, networking, services).
  • State AI Action Plan's 'Data & Compute Readiness' pillar — secure statewide infrastructure for safe AI experimentation, a fit for governed on-prem / private-cloud AI.
  • ERP modernization requiring 'native AI architecture,' plus broad enterprise modernization and automation under STS.
  • Endpoint, client, and lifecycle refresh across a ~1,200-person central IT org and the agencies it serves.

Budget & Fiscal

Tennessee budgets annually on a July 1–June 30 fiscal year. Governor Lee's FY2026 recommended budget totals roughly $59.5B from all revenue sources (about $29.5B in state dollars). There is no single consolidated 'state IT budget' line — technology spend is distributed across agencies and the centralized Strategic Technology Solutions (STS) organization within Finance & Administration, supplemented by large federal flows such as BEAD broadband funding.

Tennessee Statewide AI Action Plan3

Adopted Nov 2025

The State AI Advisory Council — co-chaired by CIO Kristin Darby and F&A Commissioner Jim Bryson — unanimously adopted Tennessee's first statewide AI Action Plan in November 2025. Its four pillars are Modernization & Pilots, Data & Compute Readiness, Workforce & Change Enablement, and Safety/Security/Accountability. The 'Data & Compute Readiness' pillar (secure statewide infrastructure for safe AI experimentation) is a direct opening for governed, auditable on-prem and private-cloud AI.

Procurement & Competition

Tennessee centralizes most non-IT and technology purchasing through the Central Procurement Office (CPO), a division of the Department of General Services created under TCA § 4-56-104. The CPO issues statewide contracts that state agencies — and, for many categories, local governments and nonprofits — can buy from without running their own solicitation. Suppliers must be registered in the state's Edison system to do business. Strategic Technology Solutions (STS) sets statewide IT direction, standards, and security policy within Finance & Administration.

Competitive landscape

Tennessee is a centralized, contract-driven market where incumbents hold statewide and cooperative agreements across compute, client, and services. Differentiation comes less from price and more from AI-infrastructure capability, governed/auditable AI offerings aligned to the state's AI Action Plan, and services that map to STS modernization and the Memphis/Oak Ridge compute ecosystem.
