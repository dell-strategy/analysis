⚖️ Operating Model: Accelerating to Consolidated

Historically federated, a 2025 near-miss cyber crisis (95-hour response) forced a violent acceleration toward centralization. Act 2025-369 formally stripped agencies of autonomy, granting the Office of Information Technology (OIT) absolute statutory authority over architectures, cybersecurity, and AI via the TQAB.

OIT enforces compliance via IT Procurement Policy 230, requiring approval for all hardware, software, and services to suffocate "shadow IT" deployments.

⚠️ Friction Points & Chargeback

OIT operates via an Internal Service Fund (ISF). They charge agencies back for premium enterprise services.

The Vise (Sales Opportunity):

The legislature caps agency operational budgets (OpEx) at ~1% growth, but OIT mandates expensive, secure infrastructure. Agencies lack the internal budgets to pay OIT chargebacks (facing sanctions if unpaid for 60 days). Dell APEX custom solutions can shift infrastructure from capital outlay to predictable OpEx, solving this structural friction point.

Alabama's fiscal year runs Oct 1 to Sept 30. Budgets are constitutionally bifurcated into the Education Trust Fund (ETF) and the State General Fund (SGF). No deficits are allowed.

💳 Understanding IT Spend Objects

  • Object 1200 (Capital Outlay): Net-new project investments, servers, and hardware >$5,000. Funds do not carry over, causing a massive Q3/Q4 procurement surge (April-Sept).
  • Object 0800 / 0900 (O&M): Recurring operational spend, software licenses, and OIT chargebacks. Highly restricted and capped by the legislature.

The top 3 most heavily funded, multi-year technological transformation initiatives currently active in the state.

1. Alabama Medicaid Enterprise Systems Mod (AMMP)

▼

Executive Mandate: Deprecate the aging monolithic AMMIS system due to federal CMS mandates, utilizing 90/10 federal match.

Owning Agency: Alabama Medicaid Agency (CIO Mason Tanaka).

IT Requirement: Modular implementation of Enterprise Data Services (EDS) data warehouse. Requires infinite scalability, zero single points of failure, HIPAA compliance, and strict CONUS data residency. Target for high-availability enterprise servers and storage arrays.

2. Message Switch System (MSS) Modernization

▼

Executive Mandate: Replace legacy CPI-based systems to ensure mission-critical communication across federal/state/local law enforcement. RFP closed March 31, 2026.

Owning Agency: Alabama Law Enforcement Agency (ALEA).

IT Requirement: COTS fully managed MSS. Absolute requirement for active-active DR/COOP across geographically separated US sites. 24/7/365 uptime and stringent FBI CJIS security policy compliance.

3. Statewide AI Governance & Cloud Readiness

▼

Executive Mandate: Execute EO 738 and TQAB (Feb 2026) directives to deploy GenAI safely to mitigate workforce shortages.

Owning Agency: OIT & Dept of Finance.

IT Requirement: Establish highly secure, isolated "sandbox" cloud infrastructures for agency pilots. Requires NIST AI-RMF compliance, unstructured data sprawl management, and IAM modernization in MS GCC.
