Section 1

Executive Summary & Macro Landscape

🔗 View Full PDF

This section provides the high-level political and administrative context shaping IT procurement in Michigan. Understanding the Governor's priorities dictates where funding flows.

Political & Economic Climate

Michigan currently operates under a Democratic trifecta (Governor Gretchen Whitmer, House, and Senate), leading to streamlined passage of executive priorities. Economically, the state is heavily focused on transitioning its legacy automotive dominance into an EV and advanced manufacturing hub, while grappling with demographic shifts and the need to modernize aging infrastructure. State revenues remain stable, bolstered by lingering federal funds, but there is a distinct shift toward ensuring long-term operational sustainability (O&M) for newly built digital assets.

Core Administrative Goals (Current FY)

  • ✔
    Infrastructure & Advanced Manufacturing: Heavy investments in EV infrastructure, battery plants, and the digital backbone required to support smart state logistics.
  • ✔
    Digital Equity & Broadband Expansion: Utilizing BEAD funding to connect rural and underserved urban areas, requiring robust backend core routing and data processing at the edge.
  • ✔
    Whole-of-State Cybersecurity: Hardening not just state executive branches, but extending cyber resilience services to local municipalities (K-12, county governments) via DTMB.

Section 3

IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

How Michigan buys and manages technology. Understanding this dictates whether we sell to a central authority or individual agencies.

Operating Model

Consolidated

Michigan operates under a highly Consolidated IT model via the Department of Technology, Management & Budget (DTMB). DTMB acts as a centralized IT service provider to executive branch agencies. Hardware, data center operations, network, and enterprise software contracts are heavily centralized. Selling infrastructure usually means selling to DTMB Enterprise Architecture and Operations teams, not individual agencies directly.

Friction Points

⚠
  • Chargeback Rates: Agencies often complain about the opaque and high chargeback costs from DTMB for standard compute/storage, driving a desire for "Shadow IT" SaaS solutions.
  • Agility vs. Standardization: Large agencies (like MDHHS) require rapid deployment for new programs, but DTMB's rigorous security and standardization reviews can cause bottlenecks.
  • Cloud Repatriation: Unpredictable public cloud egress fees have caused budgetary friction, creating a desire for predictable, cloud-like on-prem consumption.

Section 5

Strategic Initiatives & Projects (Top 3)

The funded modernization efforts. This interactive section details where the state is actively spending money right now.

Whole-of-State Cybersecurity & Resilience

Mandate Executive / SLCGP
Owner DTMB (CISO)
Tech Need Immutability, Air-Gaps

Driven by the rise in ransomware targeting local municipalities, Michigan is utilizing federal grants to offer centralized security services to smaller government entities. The core requirement for the State data centers is ensuring absolute recoverability in the event of a breach. They are moving past perimeter defense and looking for immutable storage and isolated recovery environments for mission-critical databases.

Citizen Experience & MiLogin Modernization

Mandate Citizen Exp.
Owner Multi-Agency (HHS, SOS)
Tech Need Cloud-Native, APEX

The Governor's mandate is to make interacting with the state as easy as banking online. This requires unifying backend databases and modernizing monolithic legacy applications (especially in Health & Human Services and Secretary of State). They need the agility of the public cloud but are restricted by data sovereignty and cost, driving a need for modern, container-ready infrastructure on-premises.

Enterprise Data & AI Readiness

Mandate Operational Eff.
Owner DTMB Enterprise Arch.
Tech Need Unstructured Data, GPU

Before Michigan can deploy advanced AI (GenAI chatbots for citizens, predictive maintenance for infrastructure), they must consolidate massive silos of unstructured data. Departments like Transportation (video feeds, IoT) and State Police (bodycams, evidence) are drowning in data. The current initiative focuses on building scalable data lakes and preparing the compute tier to handle future AI workloads locally to ensure data privacy.

Section 6

Procurement, Vendor Landscape & Compliance

Understanding how to transact and the competitive landmines to avoid.

📋

Procurement Vehicles

  • MiDEAL: State's extended purchasing program (allows locals to buy off state contracts).
  • NASPO ValuePoint: Primary cooperative vehicle utilized by DTMB for hardware/software.
  • Direct DTMB Master Contracts: Essential to be listed here for large enterprise purchases.
⚔️

The Battlefield

  • Incumbents: Heavy historical presence of HPE in legacy compute. NetApp in certain agency file storage silos.
  • Vulnerabilities: Aging 3-tier architectures requiring heavy management. Agencies experiencing sticker shock from unmanaged AWS/Azure sprawl.
👮‍♂️

Compliance Drivers

  • StateRAMP: Michigan was an early adopter. Any cloud or SaaS-like offering (including APEX) must align with StateRAMP security controls.
  • CJIS / HIPAA: Strict on-prem requirements for sensitive law enforcement and health data.
