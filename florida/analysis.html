IT Governance & Service Delivery

Understanding how Florida buys IT is critical. This section outlines the structural friction between central IT and independent agencies.

⚖️

Operating Model: Federated

Florida operates on a strongly Federated model. While FLDS sets security baselines and data rules, individual agencies (Transportation, Law Enforcement) control their own massive budgets and make autonomous execution decisions.

Key Friction Points

  • Chargeback Complexity:

  • Mandate vs. Budget:

    FLDS mandates strict security tools, but agencies must carve out their own operational budget to procure them, causing friction.

Strategic Initiatives & Projects

Explore the top 3 funded modernization efforts. Click the tabs below to reveal the executive mandate, owning agency, and underlying IT requirements.

🔒 Enterprise Cybersecurity Resilience

Executive Mandate:

Protect state critical infrastructure from ransomware and ensure rapid, guaranteed recovery of citizen services.

Owning Agency:

FLDS + All Exec Agencies

Underlying IT Requirement:

Immutable backup architectures, air-gapped data vaults, Zero Trust network access. Agencies must rapidly move away from legacy tape and single-site replication.

☁️ Cloud-First Citizen Portal Modernization

Executive Mandate:

"Florida First" initiative to make government services (licenses, health benefits) accessible via responsive mobile interfaces.

Owning Agency:

DHSMV & DCF

Underlying IT Requirement:

Mainframe refactoring, containerized applications, and notably, Hybrid-Cloud Repatriation as public cloud egress costs have spiraled out of control for large datasets.

🚓 Statewide Law Enforcement Analytics

Executive Mandate:

Enhance public safety by allowing real-time video/data sharing and predictive analytics across county lines.

Owning Agency:

FL Dept. of Law Enforcement (FDLE)

Underlying IT Requirement:

Massive unstructured data storage (bodycams), strict CJIS-compliant environments (requires on-premise control), and AI-ready high-performance compute clusters at the edge.
