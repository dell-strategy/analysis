📊 Executive Summary & Macro Landscape

This section provides the foundational context for the State of Tennessee account. Political & Economic Climate

Led by Governor Bill Lee (R), Tennessee maintains a conservative fiscal approach characterized by balanced budgets, significant rainy-day fund reserves, and an aversion to long-term debt for operational expenses. The economic climate is robust, driven by corporate relocations and automotive manufacturing growth. Technology investments are heavily scrutinized for ROI, favoring solutions that consolidate sprawl and improve citizen-facing digital experiences.

Core Administrative Goals (FY24/25)

  • ▶

  • ▶
    3. Public Safety & Cyber Resilience Strengthening the security posture of state agencies and local municipalities against ransomware, leveraging SLCGP funds.

IT Governance & Service Delivery

Operating Model: Hybrid / Centralizing

Tennessee operates under a hybrid model led by Strategic Technology Solutions (STS) under the Dept. of Finance & Administration. While STS mandates infrastructure, network, and security standards centrally, individual large agencies (like TDOT, TennCare) retain significant control over their application layers and specific hardware needs aligned to federal programs.

⚠ Friction Points & Chargebacks

STS utilizes a chargeback model where agencies pay for central IT services. Friction often occurs when agencies feel STS-provided infrastructure (like state private cloud) is more expensive or less agile than hyperscaler options. Opportunity: APEX can provide cloud-like agility on-prem with predictable billing to ease chargeback tension.

💵 Grants & Alternative Funding

  • SLCGP: Utilizing federal cyber grants to push endpoint security and backup/recovery mandates down to local counties.
  • BEAD / Infrastructure: Massive influx of federal dollars flowing through the Dept. of Economic & Community Development for broadband mapping and edge expansion.

Funded Alignment: Enterprise Data Center & Edge

Map to the "Rural Broadband" and "Legacy ERP" initiatives. Position PowerEdge and PowerStore as the foundational infrastructure for the state's hybrid cloud strategy. As they rethink expensive public cloud deployments for consistent ERP workloads, introduce Dell APEX to provide Opex flexibility without giving up data sovereignty.

Whitespace & Advisory: AI Readiness & Unstructured Data Sprawl

Agencies like TennCare and Transportation are exploring AI but lack a governed data foundation. Dell must lead with advisory services. Introduce PowerScale as the solution to their unstructured data sprawl, framing it not just as storage, but as the mandatory first step for secure, compliant, on-prem AI model training against state data.

Competitive Displacement: Cyber Recovery Vaulting

Capitalize on the "Public Safety & Cyber" mandate. Existing backup solutions are vulnerable. Execute a displacement motion against legacy software vendors by proposing an isolated, air-gapped PowerProtect Cyber Recovery vault. Leverage the current CISO's focus on Zero Trust and the availability of SLCGP grant funding to finance the project.
