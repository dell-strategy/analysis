1. Executive Summary & Macro Landscape

This section provides a high-level strategic overview of the State of Oklahoma's current political and economic operating environment. It is designed to give Account Executives immediate context regarding the administration's core priorities, enabling alignment of Dell messaging with state-level mandates such as economic development and modernization.

Political Climate
Conservative Supermajority

Governor Kevin Stitt (R) in second term. High focus on fiscal conservatism, agency efficiency, and commercial business attraction ("Top 10 State").

Economic Status
Stable, Revenue Surplus

Historically reliant on energy, currently diversifying. State holds significant budget reserves, allowing for selective capital investments in infrastructure and IT modernization.

Core IT Mandate
Citizen Experience & Cyber

Drive to digitize citizen services (Oklahoma.gov portal) and harden state defenses against ransomware targeting SLG entities.

Strategic Outlook for Dell

Oklahoma operates a highly consolidated IT environment under the Office of Management and Enterprise Services (OMES). While agencies hold budgets, OMES dictates architecture. The state has aggressively pushed a "Cloud First" strategy over the last five years, which is now creating localized friction points regarding unpredictable OpEx cloud costs and data gravity issues.

The Play: Dell is positioned to intercept this cloud fatigue by leading with APEX for cloud-like economics on-prem, and robust unstructured data solutions (PowerScale) for agencies generating massive edge data (DPS, ODOT) that is too expensive to move to public cloud.

2. Organization of the Power Structure & Key Stakeholders

This section maps the key decision-makers within the state government. It intentionally separates political/financial authority from technical authority. Understanding this dynamic is crucial for complex sales cycles; AEs must sell the business value to the agency (the check writer) while proving technical superiority to OMES (the gatekeeper).

The Check Writers (Financial/Business)

Governor's Office
Sets macro priorities. If an IT project doesn't align with the "Top 10 State" initiative, it struggles for funding.
Legislative Appropriations Chairs
Hold the ultimate purse strings. They look for ROI and operational savings. Major capital IT projects require their blessing.
Agency Directors (e.g., DHS, ODOT, DPS)
Own the programmatic budgets. They buy solutions that solve citizen problems or fulfill federal mandates. They rely on OMES to execute.

The Gatekeepers (Technical)

OMES - State CIO
Leads the Office of Management and Enterprise Services. Mandates statewide IT architecture, cloud strategy, and major enterprise agreements.
OMES - State CISO
Controls the statewide cybersecurity posture. Veto power over any architecture that doesn't meet stringent state or federal (CJIS, HIPAA) compliance.
Embedded Agency IT Directors
Though under the OMES umbrella, these directors understand the specific line-of-business needs of their assigned agency. Key champions for tailored solutions.
Analysis Focus: Do not bypass OMES to sell directly to an agency, and do not sell purely speeds-and-feeds to OMES without agency business backing. A successful Dell campaign requires a dual-track strategy engaging both columns simultaneously.

This section explains how IT is consumed and paid for within the state. Understanding the friction within the service delivery model highlights pain points Dell can solve (e.g., if central IT is perceived as slow, Dell APEX can be positioned to accelerate infrastructure delivery while maintaining central control).

Operating Model: Highly Consolidated

The State of Oklahoma operates under a legislatively mandated consolidated IT model. The Office of Management and Enterprise Services (OMES) acts as the central IT provider for over 100 state agencies, boards, and commissions.

Friction Points & Chargeback Reality

Chargeback Complexity: OMES operates on a cost-recovery model. Agencies are billed for compute, storage, and network usage. Complex billing often leads to disputes between agencies and OMES over IT costs.
Perception of Agility: Agencies often feel central procurement and provisioning is too slow for modern demands, driving a desire for "shadow IT" or direct public cloud instances to bypass OMES queues.
Dell Angle - The Broker Model: OMES is trying to shift from "builder" to "broker" of services. Dell APEX aligns perfectly here. It allows OMES to offer cloud-like agility and billing to the agencies, while keeping the infrastructure secure, on-premise, and out of the volatile public cloud pricing models.

5. Strategic Initiatives & Projects

This section lists the actual projects currently funded or mandated. AEs should map Dell infrastructure directly to these initiatives during client conversations, shifting the narrative from selling hardware to enabling government outcomes.

+

+

Mandate: Department of Public Safety (Highway Patrol) faces exponential data growth from high-definition body-worn cameras and dash-cams. Legislative mandates require long-term retention.

Dell Alignment: Massive whitespace for PowerScale (Isilon) and ECS object storage. Pitch total cost of ownership (TCO) against AWS/Azure for cold video storage.

IT-Level: Cloud Repatriation / "Cloud Smart"

+

Mandate: OMES pushed hard to public cloud 3-5 years ago. They are now experiencing "sticker shock" regarding operational costs and data retrieval fees. Strategy shifting from "Cloud First" to "Cloud Smart" (hybrid).

Technical Requirement: On-premise infrastructure that operates with the financial flexibility and operational simplicity of the public cloud.

Dell Alignment: The primary target for Dell APEX. Offer OMES a way to bring workloads back on-prem for security and cost control, while maintaining an OpEx consumption model.

6. Procurement, Vendor Landscape, & Compliance

This section details the operational reality of selling into Oklahoma. Even with budget and a perfect technical fit, deals die here without the correct contract vehicle and compliance certifications.

📋

Procurement Vehicles

  • NASPO ValuePoint: Primary cooperative purchasing arm. Dell holds master agreements here; ensure Oklahoma has signed the Participating Addendum (PA).
  • Statewide Contracts (SWC): OMES negotiates SWCs for standard IT commodities (PCs, basic servers). Required first-stop for agency buyers.
  • OMNIA Partners / Sourcewell: Frequently used cooperative alternatives for broader technology solutions if NASPO doesn't fit the bill.
⚖

Compliance Drivers

  • StateRAMP: Oklahoma is actively pushing towards StateRAMP requirements for cloud and SaaS vendors. Dell's alignment with FedRAMP/StateRAMP is a major competitive advantage.
  • CJIS (Criminal Justice Info Services): Mandatory for DPS/Law enforcement data. Storage solutions must support specific encryption and access controls.
  • AI Governance: Nascent, but OMES is evaluating policies on data privacy before allowing generative AI models access to state data sets.

Vendor Landscape & Incumbents

Compute / Virtualization Heavy VMware footprint. Significant market disruption opportunity for Dell to offer alternative stacks or HCI consolidation in wake of Broadcom acquisition.
Public Cloud Microsoft Azure and AWS hold major enterprise agreements. Goal is not to replace, but to position Dell storage adjacent to public cloud for data sovereignty.
Endpoints Highly competitive. Dell Client Solutions Group (CSG) must compete on lifecycle management services and security integrations, not just unit price.

7. Actionable Dell Opportunities (The "So What")

Based on the analysis of Oklahoma's budget, governance friction, and strategic mandates, the following are the highest-probability, highest-impact plays for the Dell Account Team.

Play 1: The Cloud Repatriation Intervention

Primary Target: OMES

The Pain: Agencies are draining operational budgets paying public cloud egress fees and experiencing performance latency for heavy on-prem applications pushed to the cloud prematurely.

The Solution: Pitch Dell APEX Private Cloud. It gives OMES the financial model they want (OpEx, pay-per-use) and the control they need, while rescuing agencies from unpredictable public cloud bills.

Action Item: Request a meeting with OMES Cloud Architecture team. Bring a TCO (Total Cost of Ownership) analysis comparing AWS storage vs. APEX on-prem over 3 years.

Play 2: Unstructured Data & The Edge

Primary Target: Dept of Public Safety & Dept of Transportation

The Pain: Explosive growth in edge data. DPS has HD body-cam video requiring long-term, CJIS-compliant retention. ODOT is deploying IoT sensors on infrastructure.

The Solution: Lead with PowerScale (Isilon). Demonstrate its ability to handle massive unstructured data lakes natively, providing easier searchability and lower cost-per-terabyte than public cloud block storage.

Action Item: Bypass central IT initially; map directly to the Agency IT Director at DPS. Ask how they are currently forecasting budget for the next 5 years of body-cam data retention.

Play 3: Federal Cyber Grant Intercept

Primary Target: State CISO

The Pain: The state is receiving SLCGP federal funds that must be utilized for cybersecurity resilience, but they lack the manpower to build complex custom solutions.

The Solution: Position Dell PowerProtect Cyber Recovery as an air-gapped, turn-key vault to protect critical state data from ransomware. It is a highly definable capital purchase that fits grant criteria perfectly.

Action Item: Align with Dell Security specialists. Present the CISO with a blueprint of how Cyber Recovery creates a "vault" for their most critical citizen data (tax, health records).
