3. IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

Understanding Atlanta's specific blend of centralized and decentralized technology procurement.

Operating Model: Hybrid-Federated

The City operates a Hybrid-Federated model. AIM consolidates core IT for "General Fund" agencies (Police, Fire, Courts). However, "Enterprise Funds" (Dept of Aviation, Dept of Watershed Management) operate with massive, federated autonomy, maintaining separate IT budgets for complex IoT, SCADA, and 5G DAS networks.

Key Friction Points for Dell to Solve:

  • Chargeback Avoidance (Internal Service Fund): Departments are billed for consuming central AIM services, creating friction.
  • Shadow IT Outbreaks: A recent City Auditor report found the Dept. of Parks & Recreation bypassing enterprise Oracle systems to manually track $750M "Moving Atlanta Forward" capital projects in Excel to avoid AIM soft costs.
  • Data Dashboard Inaccuracies: Decentralized tracking causes central data visualization failures.

Procurement Vehicles

  • Cooperatives Preferred: Highly risk-averse, the city relies heavily on NASPO ValuePoint, OMNIA Partners, Sourcewell, and Texas DIR to avoid RFP cycles.

The Battlefield

  • CDW-G Monopoly: Deeply entrenched for 14 years across four contract cycles, providing both hardware and professional services.

Compliance Drivers

  • NIST Framework: A Dec 2025 City Auditor report explicitly evaluated AIM against NIST standards, making it the baseline for future buys.

  • StateRAMP: Required for cloud/hybrid workloads to ensure data sovereignty.
