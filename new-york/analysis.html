IT Governance & Vendor Landscape

Understanding how NYS buys and who we are competing against.

⚖ Operating Model: Hybrid/Federated

NYS Office of Information Technology Services (ITS) acts as a centralized provider for infrastructure, networking, and security. However, large agencies (DOH, DMV, Labor) retain significant autonomy over application layers and specific hardware needs.

Operational Friction Points

  • ⚠ Chargeback Model: Agencies often complain about opaque chargebacks from ITS, driving "shadow IT" purchases where agencies try to procure solutions via alternative budgets.
  • ⚠ Procurement Speed: Centralized ITS reviews delay agency modernization timelines, creating a desire for pre-vetted, "as-a-service" solutions that bypass CapEx delays.

🛡 Compliance & Vehicles

OGS Centralized Contracts NASPO ValuePoint MWBE Mandates (30%)

Key Regulatory Drivers

  • CJIS & HIPAA: Strict data sovereignty requirements favor on-prem or sovereign cloud.
  • Executive Order 18: Mandates strict cyber hygiene, pushing endpoint security.

The Battlefield (Incumbents)

Massive legacy footprints from IBM (Mainframes/Services) and Cisco (Networking). AWS & Azure hold dominant public cloud contracts, but repatriation murmurs exist due to cost overruns at ITS.

⏱ Tech Refresh Targets

Typical modernization triggers within NYS agencies:

  • Storage Arrays (SAN/NAS) 5-7 Years (Targeting legacy EMC & NetApp footprints)
  • Compute / HCI 3-5 Years (Targeting Cisco UCS & aging VMware clusters)
  • Client / Endpoints 3-4 Years (Heavy focus on mobile workforce & secure edge)
