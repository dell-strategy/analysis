Macro Landscape & Briefing

This section provides a high-level overview of Philadelphia's current political and economic climate under Mayor Cherelle Parker's administration. It highlights the core administrative goals driving current fiscal year spending, offering Account Executives critical context for aligning Dell's messaging with the Mayor's top public mandates.

🚨

Public Safety & Tech

Massive push for visible crime reduction. Heavy investment in edge surveillance, mobile command units, and modernized CAD systems requiring robust data infrastructure.

🌿

Clean & Green City

Focus on sanitation, illegal dumping enforcement, and smart waste management. Relies heavily on IoT sensors and field mobility solutions.

💻

Digital Equity & Access

Closing the digital divide via broad broadband expansion and end-user device procurement for underserved communities and civic centers.

Macro Climate Note

Philadelphia is operating with cautious optimism. Following federal stimulus (ARPA) drawdowns, the city is shifting toward self-sustaining operational models. The Parker administration heavily prioritizes "tangible results" over abstract modernization, meaning IT pitches must explicitly connect to frontline citizen services.

IT Governance, Financials & Procurement

This section breaks down the City's operational model, budgeting lifecycle, and purchasing vehicles. The visualizations highlight the scale of the overall budget versus the dedicated IT spend, assisting in sizing enterprise agreements and identifying capital vs. operational expenditure boundaries.

⚙ Operating Model: Hybrid / Federated

Philadelphia operates on a primarily federated model with the central Office of Innovation and Technology (OIT) attempting to centralize core infrastructure.

Friction Points:

Agencies often resist OIT chargebacks, leading to shadow IT. Public Safety (Police/Fire) maintains heavy autonomy over their specific tech stacks, creating siloed data environments.

📜 Procurement Vehicles

  • Primary: COSTARS (PA State Contract) - Preferred for rapid hardware/software.
  • Secondary: NASPO ValuePoint & OMNIA Partners.
  • Compliance: Strict CJIS compliance required for any Public Safety data. Emerging focus on AI governance board approvals.
