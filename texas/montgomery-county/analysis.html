Executive Summary & Macro Landscape

High-level briefing on the current state of Montgomery County. Rapid demographic growth from the Houston metro area is straining existing county infrastructure, driving a strong need for scalable IT solutions under a fiscally conservative administration.

Hyper-Growth Entity

Population approaching 700,000. Suburban expansion is maximizing current data center capabilities and forcing a modernization of citizen service portals.

Conservative Governance

Fiscally conservative Commissioners Court. High emphasis on multi-year ROI, CAPEX predictability, and keeping property taxes low.

Agency Autonomy

Agencies (especially Law Enforcement) operate with high autonomy, often controlling their own substantial IT budgets alongside Central IT.

Top 3 Administrative Goals (Current FY)

01.

Public Safety Scaling

Expanding Sheriff's Office capabilities, including pervasive body-worn cameras and real-time crime center build-outs.

02.

Digital Citizen Experience

Overhauling legacy portals for taxes and permits to reduce in-person foot traffic.

03.

Cyber Resilience

Hardening critical infrastructure against ransomware threats, driven by recent regional attacks.

Financial/Influencer

CC

Commissioners Court

Precincts 1-4

Financial Purchaser
Tech Stance: Require majority vote for all IT contracts over $50k. Heavily scrutinize sole-source justifications and strongly favor leveraging State cooperative contracts (DIR) to ensure competitive pricing.

IT Governance Model

HYBRID

Central IT provides core networking, email, and ERP. Large agencies manage specific mission-critical apps locally.

Operational Friction Points

Chargeback Disputes

Agencies resist paying Central IT for increased storage limits, leading to departmental data silos and unmanaged NAS arrays.

Security Posture

Central IT mandates Zero Trust, but legacy agency applications cannot support modern authentication.

Procurement Speed

Central IT requires a 60-day architectural review for departmental purchases, causing agencies to attempt bypasses.
