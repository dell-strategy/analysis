Macro Landscape

Executive Summary & Account Posture

This section provides a high-level overview of Harris County's current political and economic climate. It is designed to give Account Executives immediate context regarding the county's overarching administrative goals, demographic pressures, and primary strategic drivers for the current fiscal year. Understanding these macro elements is crucial for positioning Dell's solutions as strategic enablers rather than simple IT commodities.

🏛

The Entity

3rd most populous county in the US (4.7M+ residents). Encompasses Houston. Massive geographical footprint requiring highly resilient and distributed infrastructure.

💵

Economic Climate

Strong tax base, but facing pressure from inflation and the winding down of ARPA funds. Shifting focus from emergency COVID spending to long-term operational efficiency.

🛡

Political Climate

Led by a progressive Commissioners Court focusing heavily on digital equity, criminal justice reform, and severe weather (flood) resilience and continuity of operations.

Core Administrative Goals (FY Current)

➤

Justice Administration Modernization

Massive backlog in the court system and high data generation from law enforcement (body cams). Requires massive, secure, and compliant unstructured data storage (Dell PowerScale opportunity).

➤

Disaster Resilience & Continuity

Following recent hurricanes and winter storms, there is a strict mandate for highly available data centers and robust disaster recovery/cyber recovery vaults (Dell PowerProtect/Cyber Recovery Vault).

➤

Digital Equity & Broadband

Expanding connectivity to underserved precincts. Drives need for edge computing and modern, manageable endpoints for field workers (Dell Client/Edge solutions).

IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

HYBRID MODEL

Transitioning from Federated to Consolidated

Harris County operates in a Hybrid/Federated IT model. Universal Services (US) acts as the central IT provider managing core network, enterprise data centers, ERP, and baseline cybersecurity. However, large elected officials (Sheriff, Clerk, DA) operate independently.

Friction Point: Chargebacks

Universal Services charges agencies back for compute/storage. This often drives agencies to procure their own "shadow IT" or rogue cloud instances to avoid centralized fees.

Dell Advantage

Position Dell APEX to Universal Services as a way to offer cloud-like, consumption-based internal pricing to agencies, keeping data on-premise but solving the chargeback friction.
