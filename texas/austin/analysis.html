3. IT Governance & Service Delivery

Understanding how the city buys and deploys technology is crucial. This section maps the internal bureaucracy and friction points between central IT and independent agencies.

Operating Model: Hybrid / Federated

The City of Austin utilizes a hybrid model. The central Communications and Technology Management (CTM) department provides core enterprise services (network, primary data center, email, enterprise security). However, massive enterprise agencies operate heavily federated IT divisions with significant independent budgets.

Centralized (CTM) Core Network, Enterprise Cyber, ERP
Federated (Independent) Austin Energy, Austin Water, Airport (ABIA)

Operational Friction Points

  • ✕
    Shadow IT in Agencies: Departments with specific OT (Operational Technology) needs often bypass CTM for edge infrastructure, creating security blind spots.
  • ✕
    Chargeback Model Disputes: CTM utilizes an internal service fund chargeback model. Departments frequently complain about the transparency of cloud/storage billing, driving a desire to repatriate or independently procure storage.
  • ✕
    Procurement Bottlenecks: Centralized security reviews (CISO office) cause 6-8 month delays for agency-specific software and hardware deployments.

Mandate: Vision Zero objective. Implement intelligent traffic systems, connected vehicle corridors, and real-time pedestrian analytics.

6. Procurement, Vendor Landscape & Compliance

Understanding the procurement pathways and the entrenched competition to navigate the buying process efficiently.

Contract Vehicles
  • Texas DIR: The absolute primary vehicle. If it's not on DIR, it's an uphill battle.
  • BuyBoard: Widely used for commodity hardware.
  • OMNIA Partners: Used occasionally for complex enterprise solutions bypassing state lists.
The Battlefield
  • Compute: Heavy Cisco UCS footprint aging out. HP competitive in edge.
  • Storage: NetApp entrenched in Austin Water. Pure Storage expanding in CTM.
  • Client: Dell has a strong incumbent position, but faces constant Lenovo price pressure on basic endpoint RFPs.
Compliance Drivers
  • TX-RAMP: State-level FedRAMP equivalent. Non-negotiable for cloud services.
  • CJIS: Critical for all APD data storage/compute. Drives on-prem preference.
  • SB 475: State data management/privacy mandates affecting records retention.
