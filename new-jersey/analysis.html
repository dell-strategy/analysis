Power Structure & IT Governance

Identify key decision-makers and understand the organizational friction points. State IT is a complex web of centralized mandates vs. decentralized agency budgets.

IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

Operating Model

Federated moving to Hybrid

NJOIT (Central IT) provides shared networks, data centers, and email. However, massive agencies (Human Services, Transportation) retain immense autonomous IT budgets and shadow IT silos.

Primary Friction Point: Chargebacks

The Public Cloud Escape Hatch

Agencies complain that NJOIT data center hosting and compute chargeback rates are too high. To avoid these internal costs, agencies frequently attempt to spin up workloads directly in AWS/Azure using departmental credit cards or decentralized purchasing, leading to unstructured data sprawl and security blind spots.

Strategic Initiatives & Vendor Landscape

Align Dell solutions to funded mandates. Understand the procurement vehicles required to transact and the entrenched competitors to displace.

Top 3 Funded Modernization Efforts

Executive Mandate: Citizen Experience & Digital Government.

Owning Agency: NJOIT working across all citizen-facing agencies (MVC, Labor, Human Services).

IT Requirement: Scalable, highly available identity management, data integration platforms, and secure multi-cloud access.

Executive Mandate: Cost Reduction & Modernization.

Owning Agency: NJOIT.

IT Requirement: Retiring aging 3-tier architecture. High requirement for Hyperconverged Infrastructure (HCI) and cloud-like consumption models (Storage-as-a-Service) to make NJOIT chargebacks competitive with AWS.

Executive Mandate: Cyber Resilience & Homeland Security.

Owning Agency: NJ Office of Homeland Security and Preparedness (NJCCIC).

IT Requirement: AI-driven threat hunting capabilities, massive immutable data vaulting for ransomware recovery, and edge security for connected municipal endpoints.

Procurement & Compliance

  • Vehicles: NASPO ValuePoint (primary for hardware), NJSTART (eProcurement), OMNIA Partners, NJSBA (for K-12/local tie-ins).
  • Purchasing: Highly favors cooperative purchasing to bypass lengthy state RFP processes.

Regulatory Drivers:

StateRAMP CJIS HIPAA NJ AI Task Force Mandates

The Battlefield (Incumbents)

  • ▪ Networking: Heavy Cisco entrenchment statewide.
  • ▪ Compute/Storage: Mixed environment (Dell, HPE). Aging SANs present in tier-2 agencies.
  • ▪ Cloud: Significant AWS and Azure footprint due to agency "shadow IT" bypass of central services. Ripe for repatriation analysis.

Whitespace & Advisory

Identifying Blind Spots

  • AI-Ready Unstructured Data: Agencies (like Human Services/Transportation) are hoarding massive amounts of unstructured data with no AI governance strategy. Lead with Dell Advisory Services to assess unstructured data sprawl (PowerScale positioning) before they attempt costly AI pilot programs.

Competitive Displacement

Exploiting Vulnerabilities

  • Cloud Repatriation (AWS/Azure): Target tier-2 agency CIOs who bypassed NJOIT for public cloud and are now facing massive egress fees and budget overruns. Execute a TCO analysis to rip-and-replace public cloud workloads with on-prem VxRail or PowerStore.
