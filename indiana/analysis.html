Analyst Insight: The "Consolidation" Factor

Indiana is a Highly Consolidated IT environment. The Indiana Office of Technology (IOT) controls the infrastructure (servers, network, storage, end-user compute).

The Trap: Pitching infrastructure solely to Agency Directors (e.g., Dept of Health) is often a dead end. They own the *business problem* (funding), but IOT owns the *architectural standard*.

The Win: Sell the outcome to the Agency to secure funding, then work with IOT to validate the architecture.

Follow the Money: Budget & Procurement

Understanding Indiana's "Chargeback Model" is critical. IOT operates largely as a business, charging agencies for seats, storage, and compute. Agencies request funding from the State Budget Agency, pay IOT, and IOT procures from vendors.

The Operational Reality

Indiana operates a centralized "Hybrid Cloud" model. They have historically repatriated specific workloads from public cloud due to cost unpredictability, creating an optimal environment for flexible on-prem consumption models.

Strategic Displacement Matrix

X-Axis = Entrenchment. Y-Axis = Vuln. to Displacement. Bubble Size = Hardware Footprint.

Compute & Storage

Historically diverse (HPE/Cisco presence), but shifting towards standardization.
Gap: Aging SAN infrastructure in secondary agencies (DNR, BMV) is reaching EOL.

End User Compute (Client)

Strong standardization on Windows 11. 4-year refresh cycles are strict.
Gap: Remote work created a sprawl of unmanaged endpoints. Need for unified endpoint management solutions.

Cybersecurity

Mandate for StateRAMP compliance for all vendors. Focus on Ransomware resilience.
Gap: Offline backups (Air-gapped) are a stated priority but implementation is patchy across smaller agencies.
