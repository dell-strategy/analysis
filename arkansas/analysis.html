IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

Operating Model: Aggressive Consolidation

Following the McKinsey diagnostic, Executive Orders 25-10 and 25-15 mandated the end of siloed IT. The central IT authority, formally DIS, was elevated and renamed the Department of Shared Administrative Services (DSAS), Office of State Technology (OST).

OST is now legally mandated to enforce centralized procurement, eliminate duplicative applications, and manage statewide technology projects. An enterprise IT Governance Team ensures no project advances without aligning with strict state architectural standards.

⚠️

Primary Friction Points

  • ➤ DIAMONDS Chargebacks: OST acts as a cost-recovery agency, billing agencies via the DIAMONDS system. Fluctuating mandated chargeback rates create severe budget friction for agency CIOs seeking predictable costs.
  • ➤ Procurement Bottlenecks: Any IT procurement over $100,000 must endure a mandatory 10-day "State Technology Cost Analysis" by DFA to ensure it doesn't deviate from OST's Shared Technical Architecture.
