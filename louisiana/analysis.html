Political & Economic Climate

Operating under Governor Jeff Landry (R), the administration has signaled a strong pivot toward operational efficiency, strict budgetary oversight, and modernizing legacy government services. The state is leveraging massive federal infrastructure influxes while balancing historical challenges in state tax revenues. There is a high appetite for technological consolidation that proves immediate ROI and bolsters state security posture.

Top 3 Administrative IT Goals:

  1. Cybersecurity Resilience: Hardening state/local endpoints against ransomware (driven by SLCGP).
  2. Digital Equity & Broadband: Deploying $1B+ in BEAD funding, demanding edge compute.
  3. Legacy Modernization: Retiring mainframes in HHS (Health) and DMV for nimble platforms.

IT Operating Model

Consolidated via Office of Technology Services (OTS)

OTS acts as the centralized MSP. However, noting recent executive action, agencies like LED (Sec. Bourgeois) have secured exemptions to bypass OTS bottlenecks. This creates a hybrid sales landscape.

Known Friction Points

Chargeback Complexity: Agencies complain about opaque pricing. CIO Broussard was explicitly hired to fix this deep-seated friction.

Procurement Delays: Governor Landry signed Act 590 to bypass these delays, signaling urgency for modern, pre-approved purchasing.
