Executive Summary

Navigating severe fiscal constraints, political shifts, and a survival-driven pivot toward AI and automation.

Macro Landscape

The City of Baton Rouge and the Parish of East Baton Rouge is navigating one of its most volatile periods. Entering FY 2026, the local government is defined by a seismic shift in leadership (Mayor-President Sid Edwards), unprecedented macroeconomic constraints driven by the municipal secession of St. George (costing ~$50M in revenue), and an aggressive pivot toward AI/automation to maintain baseline operations.

Fiscal Triage Required

To balance the $1.1B budget without risking the AA2 bond rating, the administration executed a $15M cut to the General Fund, resulting in a staggering 33% reduction in the civilian workforce (~420 positions eliminated).

Three Strategic Pillars

Public Safety Focus

Restoring law & order. Hired 100 new officers; instituted historic $7.5M pay raise. Heavy reliance on Real Time Crime Center (RTCC) while cutting expensive OpEx like ShotSpotter.

AI & Automation

Mitigating the 33% staff deficit by actively pursuing AI automation for 311 citizen requests, code enforcement, and intelligent data routing.

Digital Transparency

Maintaining "Top 10 Digital City" status. Launched IT Capital Projects Dashboard to guarantee taxpayer transparency regarding infrastructure spend.

IT Governance & Service Delivery Model

A Centralized model heavily strained by municipal secession.

Centralized, Consolidated, & Expanding

The Information Services (IS) department serves as the sole authority for enterprise architecture, managing network support, application dev, cybersecurity, and GIS for 4,000+ employees.

The St. George Complexity: Due to a recent Intergovernmental Agreement, Baton Rouge's central IT must now act as a Managed Service Provider (MSP) for the newly independent City of St. George. They run St. George's 311 systems, floodplain data, and tax routing. This mandates extreme multi-tenant data segmentation.

1 Architecture, 2 Cities

Requires rigorous cross-jurisdictional network routing and access control.

Operational Friction Points

1. The Automation Deficit

IS must support two cities with 33% fewer technicians. Massive bottleneck in Tier 1/2 support. Desperate need for predictive hardware analytics and self-healing protocols.

2. Cross-Jurisdictional Chargebacks

Baton Rouge must recover shared infrastructure costs from St. George via Indirect Cost Allocation Plans (ICAP). Hardware must support granular telemetry and usage-based metering.

3. The CapEx vs. OpEx Paradox

Finance strictly forbids using reserve funds for recurring O&M. Public cloud SaaS/IaaS faces extreme scrutiny. High preference for capitalized, bonded on-prem infrastructure.
